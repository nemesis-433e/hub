【なにやら】

1. なにやら

（1）なにやら変へんな臭においがする。／Something smells strange.
（2）みんなで集あつまってなにやら相談そう だんをしているらしい。／It seems that they’re all getting together and discussing something.
（3）なにやら雨あめが降ふりそうな天気てん きですね。／It looks kind of like it’s going to rain, doesn’t it.
（4）この曲きょくを聞きいていたらなにやら悲かなしい気分き ぶんになってしまった。／When I listened to this music, I somehow felt sad.
（5）あの一家いっ かはなにやら伊豆い ずの方ほうへ引越ひっ こしをするそうです。／I heard that, for some reason, that family is moving to the Izu area.
Indicates that something cannot be clearly defined. Means “I don’t know what it is, but...,” “I don’t know for sure, but...,” or “I don’t know the exact reason, but....”

2. ...やらなにやら

［Nやらなにやら］

［A-い／V-る　やらなにやら］

（1）お菓子か しやらなにやらを持もち寄よってパーティーを開ひらいた。／We all brought cookies and things and had a party.
（2）子供こ どもの病気びょう きやらなにやらで、落おち着ついて考かんがえる暇ひまもなかった。／What with my child’s illness and everything, I didn’t even have time to sit down and think.
（3）酔よっぱらって、泣なき叫さけぶやら何なにやらの大騒おお さわぎを演えんじたあげく、大おおいびきをかいて寝込ね こんでしまった。／After getting drunk, screaming and whatnot, and overall making a big fuss, he fell fast asleep, snoring loudly.
Indicates that there are many other things similar to the thing that is specifically mentioned. Often implies that many different things or events are jumbled together in a disorderly fashion.
