【うる】

［R-うる］

（1）彼かれが失敗しっ ぱいするなんてありえない。／It’s impossible for him to fail.
（2）それは彼女かの じょになしえた最大限さい だい げんの努力ど りょくだったに違ちがいない。／It must been the best effort that she could put forth.
（3）その絵えのすばらしさは、とても言葉こと ばで表あらわしうるものではない。／It can’t be described in words how wonderful that picture is.
（4）確たしかに外国人がい こく じん労働者ろうどうしゃが増ふえればそういう問題もん だいも起おこり得えるだろう。／If the number of foreign workers increases, those kinds of problems might possibly occur, too.
（5）彼かれの自殺じ さつは誰だれもが予期よ きし得えなかったことだけに、そのショックは大おおきかった。／No one could have expected his suicide, so it was a great shock to us.
（6）彼かれの仕事し ごとぶりには失望しつ ぼうの念ねんを禁きんじ得えない。／I cannot help being disappointed with his way of working.
うる joins to a verb stem. Both うる and える can be used in the dictionary form, but in the masu-form, negative form, and ta-form, only えます, えない, and えたare used. These expressions mean “one can do a certain action” or “it is possible that a certain situation will be realized.” In the negative, it means “one cannot do an action” or “it is impossible for a situation to be realized.” V-れる indicates potential, as in 書ける and 読める, and is only used with volitional verbs. うる, on the other hand, can also be used with non-volitional verbs, as in (1) and (4). However, unlike V-れる, it cannot be used to express an ability.
（×）彼はフランス語が話しうる。／He is possible to speak French.
（○）彼はフランス語が話せる。／He can speak French.
Generally considered to be written language, but ありえない can also be used in daily conversation.
