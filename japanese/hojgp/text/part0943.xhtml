【まだ】

1. まだ...ない

（1）A:昼ひるご飯はんは、もう食たべましたか。／Have you already eaten lunch?
B:いいえ、まだ食たべていません。／No, I haven’t eaten yet.
（2）A:この本ほんは、もう読よみましたか。／Have you read this book already?
B:いいえ、まだです。／No, not yet.
（3）事故じ この原因げん いんは、まだわかっていない。／The cause of the accident is still unknown.
（4）子こ:お母かあさん、ご飯はんまだ？／Child:Mom, is dinner ready yet?
母はは:もうちょっと待まってね。／Mother: Wait a little while longer.
（5）風邪か ぜはまだよくならない。／My cold is still not better.
（6）その時ときはまだ何なにが起おこったのかわからなかった。／At that time I still did not know what had happened.
（7）外国がい こくには、まだ一度いち ども行いったことがない。／I have not yet been abroad even once.
Shows that something that had been planned or was expected to happen has not happened as yet, or is not completed.
The phrase いいえ、まだ...ていません (no, not yet done) is often used as the negative response to もう...ましたか (done yet?) questions. いいえ、まだです (not yet done) can also be used. If いいえ、まだ...ません (no, not yet V) is used, it may be interpreted that the speaker has no intention of doing so and therefore there are situations in which this is inappropriate.
（×）A:昼ごはんはもう食べましたか。／Have you had your lunch yet?
B:いいえ、まだ食べません。／No, I’m not going to yet.

2. まだ〈continuation from the past〉

（1）A:敏子とし こは何なにをしているの？／What is Toshiko doing?
B:おねえちゃんは、まだ電話でん わをしているよ。／My sister’s still on the telephone.
（2）もう一週間いっ しゅう かんになるのに、父ちちと母はははまだけんかをしている。／Even though it’s been a week, Dad and Mom are still fighting.
（3）子こどもの時ときに大おおきな地震じ しんがあった。あの時ときのことは、今いまでもまだはっきりと覚おぼえている。／When I was a child there was a big earthquake. Even now, I remember that day clearly.
（4）今年ことしになっても、日本に ほんの経済けい ざいはまだ低迷てい めいを続つづけている。／Even this year, the Japanese economy is still sluggish.
（5）A:昔むかし、みんなで温泉おん せんに行いったことがあったね。／A long time ago we all went to a hot spring together, didn’t we?
B:ああ、まだおじいさんが生いきていたころだね。／Ah, that was when Grandfather was still alive.
（6）昔むかしと違ちがって、 60 代だいといってもまだ若わかい。／Unlike in the past, people in their sixties are still young.
（7）9月がつなのにまだ暑あつい。／Even though it’s September, it’s still hot.
（8）さなえちゃんは偉えらそうなことを言いってても、まだ子供こ どもだねえ。／Sanae-chan may talk big, but she is still a child.
（9）まだ未成年み せい ねんなのに酒さけを飲のんではいけない。／As you are still a minor, you must not drink alcohol.
Often used in the form まだV-ている, demonstrates a continuous condition or situation. When used in the form まだV-ていた, as in example (5), demonstrates a point in the past at which a certain condition or situation continued and which differs from the present. Therefore, (5) means “He is not alive now, but he was still living then.” In examples (7) - (9) the same condition as in the past continues in the present and a lack of progression to the expected natural next stage or condition is emphasized. In (7), although summer has passed and it should be a pleasantly cooler time, it is not. Example (8) means that she has not become an adult. Example (9) means that you have not yet reached the age at which it is permissible to drink alcohol.

3. まだ〈continuation into the future〉

（1）これから、まだもっと寒さむくなる。／From now on it will become even colder.
（2）雨あめは、まだ二に、三日さん にち続つづくだろう。／This rain will probably continue for another two or three days yet.
（3）景気けい きはまだ当分とう ぶんよくならないと思おもわれる。／It is thought that business is unlikely to improve for the time being.
（4）まだこの株かぶは値上ね あがりする。／This stock will continue to increase in value.
（5）まだまだこれからが大変たい へんですよ。／From now on it will become even more serious.
Demonstrates that the present condition or situation will continue into the future also. When まだまだ is used it means “even more” or “yet more” and shows that a condition or situation continues to a higher level or for a longer period of time. まだまだ cannot be used when, as in example (2), a specified time period such as “two or three days” or “three days” is given.

4. まだ...ある

（1）開演かい えんまでには、まだ時間じ かんがある。／There is still some time until the start (of a play or concert).
（2）目的地もく てき ちまで、まだ 20 キロはある。／There are still twenty kilometers to our destination.
（3）食糧しょく りょうはまだ三日分みっ か ぶんほど残のこっている。／We still have about three days’ worth of food left.
（4）まだ他ほかにも話はなしたいことがある。／There are still other things I would like to discuss.
Expresses that a thing or time is left or remaining.

5. まだ〈time lapse〉

（1）まだ一時間いち じ かんしかたっていない。／Still only one hour has passed.
（2）日本に ほんにきて、まだ半年はん としだ。／It has only been half a year since I came to Japan.
（3）まだ 10 分ぶんほどしか勉強べん きょうしていないのに、もう眠ねむくなってきた。／Even though I have been studying for only about ten minutes, I have already become sleepy.
（4）震災しん さいからまだ一年いち ねんにしかならないのに、街まちの復興ふっ こうはめざましい。／Even though it is only one year since the earthquake, the town’s reconstruction is remarkable.
（5）もう夕方ゆう がたかとおもったが、まだ3時じだ。／I thought it was already evening but it is still only three o’clock.
In conjunction with time expressions, used to emphasize that only a little time has passed since a particular occurrence or event.

6. まだ〈comparison〉

（1）何日なん にちもかかって、長ながいレポートを書かかされるよりは、一日いち にちですむ試験し けんの方ほうがまだいい。／An exam that can be finished in one day is still better than being made to write a long report that takes days.
（2）家事か じはみんな嫌きらいだが、掃除そう じよりも洗濯せん たくの方ほうが、まだましだ。／Everyone hates housework, but doing the laundry is at least not as bad as cleaning.
（3）A:ああ、いやだ。試験し けんが5つもある。／Oh, it’s terrible. I have five exams!
B:君きみなんか、まだましな方ほうだよ。僕ぼくなんか、11もあるよ。／You are luckier than some. I have eleven!
（4）今度こん どの地震じ しんで家いえも財産ざい さんもなくしたが、命いのちがあっただけ、まだ救すくわれる。／I lost both my home and my fortune in this earthquake, but I’m just glad to be alive.
When used in forms such as まだいい and まだましだ this expresses that neither of two things is good but that one is a little preferable to the other.
