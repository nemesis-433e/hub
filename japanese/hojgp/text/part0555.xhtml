【どうり】

1. ...どうりがない

（1）こんなに難むずかしい本ほんが子供こ どもに読よめる道理どう りがない。／It’s absurd to think that a child should be able to read such a difficult book.
（2）上司じょう しなら部下ぶ かにどんな命令めい れいをしてもよいなどという道理どう りはない。／It’s not acceptable to think that if you’re the boss you can order your subordinates around any way you like.
（3）そんな道理どう りはない。／That makes no sense.
Means that no matter how one thinks about something, there is no reason or basis to accept it as correct. The particle が sometimes changes to は, as in (2) and (3).

2. どうりで

（1）A:彼女かの じょ 13 歳さいまでアメリカで育そだったんだって。／I heard that she grew up in the U.S. until she was thirteen.
B:へえ。どうりで英語えい ごの発音はつ おんがいいわけだね。／Really? It’s no wonder her English pronunciation is so good.
（2）A:彼女かの じょの両親りょう しんは学者がく しゃだよ。／Both of her parents are scholars.
B:道理どう りで彼女かの じょも頭あたまがいいはずだ。／So that explains why she's so smart too!
Used when someone learns the most reasonable explanation for a state of affairs and accepts it, with the sense of “of course, that makes sense/that must be why.”
