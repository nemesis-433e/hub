【おいそれと（は）...ない】

［おいそれと（は）V-れない］

（1）子供こ どもを産うんだばかりの母ははネコにはおいそれとは近ちかづけない。／We can’t easily get close to a mother cat that has just had kittens.
（2）君きみならできるとおだてられても、あんな大役たい やくは責任せき にんも重おもいし、おいそれとは引ひき受うけられない。／Even though you flatter me that I can do it, I can’t easily undertake such an important task with so much responsibility.
（3）当時とう じは大変たい へんな不景気ふ けい きで、大学だい がくを出でたからといっておいそれと就職しゅう しょくできるような時代じ だいではなかった。／Those were really hard times, so even if someone had graduated from a university, it was not easy to get a job.
（4）お礼れいにと言いってお金かねを差さし出だされたが、何なにか下心した ごころがありそうなので、おいそれと受うけ取とるわけにはいかなかった。／They offered me money to show their gratitude, but I couldn’t easily accept it, because they seemed to have some kind of ulterior motive.
Means that, for some reason, an action can’t be easily done. Used with sentence-final expressions such as the negative form of the potential V-れる and indicates that the action expressed by a verb is impossible. Sometimes おいそれと...する modifies nouns, such as 時代 in (3). In that case, too, the noun is followed by a negative expression. V-るわけにはいかない, as in (4), expresses impossibility.
