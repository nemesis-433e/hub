【かえって】

（1）親切しん せつで言いったつもりなのだが、かえって怒おこらせてしまったようだ。／I meant to say it out of kindness, but it made her angry instead.
（2）間まに合あうようにと思おもってタクシーに乗のったのに、渋滞じゅう たいのせいでかえって遅おそくなってしまった。／Even though I took a cab so I would arrive in time, I ended up being late because of a traffic jam.
（3）昨日きのう買かったカーテンは少すこし派手は ですぎたかなと思おもっていたが、かえって部屋へ やが明あかるくなってよかった。／I was afraid that the curtains I bought yesterday were too loud, but I’m happy to say that they’ve made the room brighter.
（4）A:お見舞み まいに来きてくれたお礼れいに、川井かわ いさんにはお菓子か しでも持もって行いこうか。／Shall I bring a box of cookies or something to thank Kawai-san for visiting me when I was ill?
B:いや、そんなことをしたら、かえって向むこうが気きを遣つかうよ。／No, if you did that, it would just make her feel beholden.
（5）A:この間あいだはひどいことを言いってしまって、悪わるかった。／I’m sorry I said such an awful thing the other day.
B:いや、かえって良よかったよ。あれから君きみの言葉こと ばを思おもい出だしてぼくもいろいろ反省はん せいしたんだ。／No, it was actually good. After that, I thought over many things that you had said.
Used when a result is the opposite of one’s intention or expectation. Example (1) means “contrary to expectation, something said out of kindness to another person made him angry,” and (4) means “one would expect that bringing a box of cookies would make someone happy, but instead it would make her feel beholden.” Example (5) means “I thought I hurt the other person because I said such an awful thing, but it was actually a good chance for him to reflect on his conduct.” Indicates an outcome that is contrary to what one might expect in normal circumstances. It is therefore not likely to be used for unpredictable events.
（×）今日は雨が降ると思っていたが、かえっていい天気になった。／I thought it would rain today, but instead it was a beautiful day.
（○）今日は雨が降ると思っていたが、いい天気になった。／I thought it would rain today, but it turned out to be a beautiful day.
