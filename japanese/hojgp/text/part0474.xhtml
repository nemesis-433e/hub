【ては】

［N／Na　では］

［A-くては］

［V-ては］

A combination of the te-form of a predicate plus は. When used with nouns or na-adjectives, it takes the te-form of だ and becomes では, but in written language であっては can also be used. In spoken language, this form is often reduced to ちゃ or じゃ.

1. ...ては〈condition〉

Accompanied at the end of the sentence by an expression conveying some kind of negative evaluation. Means that the condition indicated by ...ては will result in an undesirable outcome. Often used when recommending that such an outcome be avoided.

a. ...ては

（1）この仕事し ごとに時給じ きゅう 500 円えんでは人ひとがみつかりません。／You won’t find anyone to do this job for 500 yen an hour.
（2）先方せん ぽうの態度たい どがそんなにあやふやでは将来しょう らいが心配しん ぱいだ。／That kind of shilly-shallying attitude on their part really makes me worried about the future.
（3）コーチがそんなにきびしくては、だれもついてきませんよ。／If the coach is that strict, no one will go along with him.
（4）そんなに大おおきな声こえを出だしては魚さかなが逃にげてしまう。／That loud a voice will scare all the fish away.
（5）そのことを彼女かの じょに言いってはかわいそうだ。／Telling her that would be really mean.
Generally followed by a negative comment. Indicates that a certain condition is problematic or should be avoided.

b. V-ていては

（1）そんなにテレビばかり見みていては目めが悪わるくなってしまうよ。／Watching that much TV will hurt your eyesight.
（2）そんなにたばこばかり吸すっていては、体からだに障さわりますよ。／That kind of constant smoking will ruin your health.
（3）そんなに先生せん せいに頼たよっていては、進歩しん ぽしませんよ。／You’ll never make any progress if you just depend on your teacher all the time.
Expresses a warning or piece of advice. Used to point out a problem someone has and to encourage a different course of action.

c. Vのでは

（1）そんなに遠とおくから通勤つう きんしていらっしゃるのでは大変たい へんですね。／It must be hard for you to commute from so far away.
（2）年間ねん かん 200 万まん円えんもかかるのではとてもその大学だい がくには行いけない。／If it costs two million yen a year, you certainly can’t go to that university.
（3）A:山下やま したさん子供こ どもが5人にんいるんですって。／I heard Yamashita-san has five children.
B:5人にんもいるのでは、毎日まい にち大騒おお さわぎだろうな。／With five children, it’s probably bedlam every day!
（4）そんなふうに頭あたまごなしに否定ひ ていされたのでは議論ぎ ろんにならないじゃないですか。／With that kind of flat-out rejection, the discussion doesn’t go anywhere, does it.
When のでは follows a verb, the meaning is “with that sort of situation.” The last part of the sentence then states the outcome of such a situation, or the speaker’s reaction or opinion. In spoken language, Vんでは and Vんじゃ are often used.

d. V-る／V-ない　ようでは

（1）最初さい しょの日ひから、仕事し ごとに遅刻ち こくするようでは困こまる。／His coming to work late from the very first day is a real problem for us.
（2）この程度てい どの練習れん しゅうに参まいるようでは、もうやめたほうがいい。／If just this much practice is too much for you, you’d better quit.
（3）そんなささいなことで傷きずついて泣なくようではこれから先さきが思おもいやられる。／If such a little thing hurt her and made her cry, what’s going to happen in the future?
（4）こんな簡単かん たんな文書ぶん しょも書かけないようでは、店長てん ちょうの仕事し ごとはつとまらない。／He’s not fit to be shop manager if he can’t write something this easy.
（5）こんな短みじかい距離きょ りも歩あるけないようでは夏なつの登山と ざんはとても無理む りだ。／If he can’t walk that short a distance, then the summer mountain-climbing trip is completely out of the question.
Used with expressions that convey a negative meaning, such as 困る (troublesome), いけない (wrong), and 無理だ (impossible). Means “this sort of situation is problematic.” Often used to disparage or criticize another person. In some contexts, may be used to scold or reprove someone directly.

2. V-ては〈repetition〉

Attaches to a verb and expresses repetition of an action or phenomenon.

a. V-てはV

（1）家計か けいが苦くるしいので、母はははお金かねの計算けい さんをしてはため息いきをついている。／Funds were severely limited, and Mother let out sigh after sigh as she worked on the household budget.
（2）子供こ どもは二に、三歩さん ぽ歩あるいては立たち止どまって、母親はは おやの来くるのを待まっている。／The little child stopped every two or three steps and waited for her mother.
（3）その女性じょ せいは誰だれかを待まっているらしく、1ページ読よんでは顔かおをあげて窓まどの外そとを見みている。／The woman reading the book appears to be waiting for someone, and she lifts her head every page or so to look out the window.
（4）一行いち ぎょう書かいては考かんがえ込こむので、執筆しっ ぴつはなかなかはかどらない。／He pauses to think after every line, so his writing is making little progress.
（5）学生がく せいの頃ころは、小説しょう せつを読よんでは仲間なか まと議論ぎ ろんしたものだ。／When I was a student, I used to read novels and then discuss them with my friends.
Expresses an action repeated after a certain time. In (5), it is used together with たものだ and expresses the recollection of a formerly repeated action.

b. V-てはV、V-てはV

（1）書かいては消けし、書かいては消けし、やっと手紙て がみを書かき上あげた。／After countless writings and erasures, he finally finished the letter.
（2）作つくってはこわし、作つくってはこわし、を何度なん どもやりなおして、ようやく満足まん ぞくできるつぼができあがった。／Again and again he made it, only to break it and start over, and at last he completed the pot to his satisfaction.
（3）降ふってはやみ、降ふってはやみの天気てん きが続つづいている。／The weather continues with rain off and on.
（4）食たべては寝ね、寝ねては食たべるという生活せい かつをしている。／His life is nothing but eating and sleeping, sleeping and eating.
Two verbs are used twice in the same order to indicate that an action or phenomenon is repeated. As in (4), the order may be switched to create the form V1-てはV2、V2-てはV1.
