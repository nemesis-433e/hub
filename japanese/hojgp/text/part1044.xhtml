【らしい】

1. Nらしい

a. NらしいN

（1）最近さい きんは子供こ どもらしい子供こ どもが少すくなくなった。／Recently there seem to be fewer truly childlike children.
（2）男おとこらしい男おとこってどんな人ひとのことですか。／What kind of person is a manly man?
（3）あの人ひとは本当ほん とうに先生せん せいらしい先生せん せいですね。／That person is very much a teacher’s teacher.
（4）このところ雨あめらしい雨あめも降ふっていない。／There really hasn’t been any measurable rain recently.
Repeating the same noun, conveys the model of the thing denoted by it.

b. Nらしい

（1）今日きょうは春はるらしい天気てん きだ。／Today the weather is like spring.
（2）弱音よわ ねを吐はくなんて君きみらしくないね。／It’s not like you to be so negative.
（3）彼かれはいかにも芸術家げい じゅつ からしく奇抜き ばつなかっこうで現あらわれた。／He turned up in a striking outfit you would expect an artist to wear.
（4）彼女かの じょが選えらんだ花束はな たばはいかにも彼女かの じょらしいやさしい色合いろ あいだった。／The bouquet (of flowers) she chose had gentle hues, just like her.
Attaches to a noun, and expresses that the typical properties of that thing are very apparent.

2. ...らしい

［N／Na／A／V　らしい］

（1）天気てん き予報よ ほうによると明日あ すは雨あめらしい。／According to the weather forecast, it looks like rain tomorrow.
（2）新あたらしく出でたビデオカメラはとても便利べん りらしい。／The video camera that has just been launched is said to be very handy.
（3）みんなの噂うわさでは、あの人ひとは国くにでは翻訳家ほん やく かとしてかなり有名ゆう めいらしい。／According to what everyone’s saying, that woman is quite famous as a translator in her country.
（4）彼かれはどうやら今いまの会社かい しゃを辞やめて、自分じ ぶんで会社かい しゃを作つくるらしい。／It seems that he’s going to quit the company he’s at now and start his own.
（5）兄あにはどうも試験し けんがうまくいかなかったらしく、帰かえってくるなり部屋へ やに閉とじ込こもってしまった。／It seems that my older brother’s exam didn’t go well. As soon as he came home he shut himself in his room.
（6）その映画えい がは予想よ そう以上い じょうにおもしろかったらしく、彼かれは何度なん どもパンフレットを読よみ返かえしていた。／That movie must have been more interesting than expected; he reread the flier many times.
（7）料理りょう りはいかにも即席そく せきで用意よう いしたらしく、インスタントのものがそのまま並ならんでいた。／It looked as though the dishes were prepared very quickly; many instant food items were lined up as they were.
Attached to the end of a sentence, expresses the speaker’s thinking that the content of the sentence is quite certain to be so. The basis of this judgement stems from external information or observable objective things and not simply the speaker’s impression. For example, the judgement 雨らしい in example (1) is made according to the weather forecast. The judgement made in (5), 試験がうまくいかなかったらしい (that the exam did not go well), is based on the fact that the brother shut himself in his room immediately after returning home.
For the differences between this and みたいだ and そうだ, please refer to Usage 2 in みたいだ.
