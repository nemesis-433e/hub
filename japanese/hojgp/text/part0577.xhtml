【ところ2】

1. V-たところ〈resultative〉

（1）先生せん せいにお願ねがいしたところ、早速さっそく承諾しょう だくのお返事へん じをいただいた。／On making a request of my teacher, I immediately received a reply of consent.
（2）駅えきの遺失物い しつ ぶつ係がかりに問とい合あわせたところ、届とどいているとのことだ。／Upon contacting the lost and found office at the station, I was told that my article had been turned in.
（3）ホテルに電話でん わしたところ、そのような名な前まえの人ひとは泊とまっていないそうだ。／When I called the hotel, I was told that there was no one by that name staying there.
（4）教室きょう しつに行いってみたところが、学生がく せいは一人ひとりも来きていなかった。／When I went and took a look at the classroom, there wasn't a single student there.
ところ is attached to the ta-form of an action verb to express the occurrence of a situation and the triggering of a discovery. The situations that appear before and after this expression are not in a direct cause-effect relationship; rather, their relationship is something like “when I did..., by chance/it so happened that such-and-such was the case.” The situation in the second clause is a discovery on the part of the speaker that was triggered by the action in the first clause, and this discovery is stated using language that denotes a previously-realized fact. Sometimes ところ is followed by the particle が, as in (4), creating the form V-たところが. In this case, the second clause usually expresses a situation contrary to expectation.

2. V-たところが〈adversative connective〉

（1）親切しん せつのつもりで言いったところがかえって恨うらまれてしまった。／I spoke with the intention of being kind, and instead I find myself being resented.
（2）高たかいお金かねを出だして買かったところが、すぐ壊こわれてしまった。／I spent a lot of money to buy it, and it broke right away.
（3）仕事し ごとが終おわって急いそいで駆かけつけてみたところが、講演こう えんはもうほとんど終おわってしまっていた。／After finishing work I rushed to get there as soon as I could, only to find that the performance was already almost over.
This adversative usage can be replaced by のに (even though) and expresses that an outcome is contradictory to anticipation or expectation. In contrast to the resultative usage shown above, が is usually not omitted.
