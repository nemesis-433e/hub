【めく】

［Nめく］

（1）少すこしずつ春はるめいてきた。／Little by little it became spring-like.
（2）どことなく謎なぞめいた女性じょ せいがホールの入いり口ぐちに立たっていた。／A woman with something of a mysterious air was standing in the entrance of the hall.
（3）彼かれは、皮肉ひ にくめいた言いい方かたをした。／He spoke cynically.
（4）彼かれの作つくり物ものめいた笑わらいが、気きになった。／I was worried by his forced smile.
Attached to a noun, this expresses that “(something) has that element to (it).” For example, in (1) it is used around the end of winter to express that the weather is gradually becoming more like spring. The nouns which can be used with this are restricted. When it is used to modify a noun it is used in the form NめいたN, as in examples (3) and (4).
