【はんたいに】

1. はんたいに

（1）あの子こは、靴くつを反対はん たいにはいている。／That child is wearing his shoes on the wrong feet.
（2）父ちちは酒さけが一滴いっ てきも飲のめない。反対はん たいに母はははとても酒さけに強つよい。／My father can’t drink at all. In contrast, my mother holds her liquor very well.
（3）彼かれはどろぼうに飛とびかかったが、反対はん たいにやられてしまった。／He lunged at the robber but got pinned down instead.
（4）今学期こん がっ きは、いっしょうけんめい勉強べん きょうしたが、成績せい せきは反対はん たいにさがってしまった。／I studied really hard this term, but my grades went down instead of up.
Means “conversely.” As shown in (1), it is sometimes used with respect to two elements that have been reversed, such as “left and right” or “up and down.” It can also be used to express contrasting situations, as in (2), or a result that is the opposite of what is normally expected, as in (3) and (4).

2. ...と（は）はんたいに

（1）姉あねは友ともだちと騒さわぐのが好すきだが、私わたしは姉あねと反対はん たいに静しずかに音楽おん がくでも聞きいている方ほうが好すきだ。／My older sister likes to party with friends, but I’m the opposite and like to do things like listening quietly to music.
（2）私わたしの部屋へ やは南みなみむきで陽ひあたりがいいが、うるさい。それとは反対はん たいに妹いもうとの部屋へ やは、陽ひあたりは悪わるいが静しずかだ。／My room faces south and gets a lot of sunshine, but it’s noisy. In contrast, my little sister’s room doesn’t get a lot of sunshine, but it’s quiet.
（3）山田やま ださんが晩年ばん ねんいい作品さく ひんを残のこしたのと反対はん たいに、若わかくして賞しょうをとった石田いし ださんはその後ごぱっとしなかった。／Yamada-san left behind some fine works in his later years, but Ishida-san, who had received an award at a young age, was not very impressive after that.
（4）弟おとうとが有名ゆう めいになっていくのとは反対はん たいに、兄あにの人気にん きは衰おとろえてきた。／The younger brother is becoming famous and, conversely, the older brother is losing popularity.
Means “on the contrary.” Can be used to compare two contrasting things, as shown in (1), (2), and (3), or to describe two situations that are changing in inverse proportion to each other, as shown in (4).
