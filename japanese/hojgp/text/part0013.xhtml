【あげく】

1. ...あげく

［Nのあげく］

［V-たあげく］

（1）さんざん悩なやんだあげく、彼かれには手紙て がみで謝あやまることにした。／After worrying and worrying, I decided to apologize to him in a letter.
（2）考かんがえに考かんがえたあげく、この家いえを売うることに決きめた。／After thorough consideration, I have decided to sell this house.
（3）弟おとうとは6年ねんも大学だい がくに行いって遊あそびほうけたあげくに、就職しゅう しょくしたくないと言いい出だした。／After fooling around for six years at university, then my younger brother said that he didn’t want to get a job.
（4）それは、好すきでもない上司じょう しの御機嫌ご き げんを取とったり、家族か ぞくに当あたり散ちらしたりの大騒おお さわぎをしたあげくの昇進しょう しんであった。／It was a promotion that finally came about after the great fuss of currying favor with the boss he doesn’t like and taking out his anger on his family.
（5）姉あねは籍せきを入いれないだの一緒いっ しょに住すまないだのと言いって親おやと対立たい りつし、すったもんだのあげくにようやく結婚けっ こんした。／My sister finally married, after much quarreling with our parents and indecision about things like registering her marriage and whether or not to live together.
Whenあげくis followed by an expression of some situation, it means that such a conclusion/solution/progress was reached after the previous state had continued long enough. In many cases, that continuous state is a mental burden or nuisance for the person. あげくに is also used, as in (5), and あげくのN is used before a noun, as in (4).

2. あげくのはてに（は）

（1）部長ぶ ちょうはますます機嫌き げんが悪わるくなり、あげくの果はてには関係かん けいない社員しゃ いんにまでどなり散ちらすようになった。／The manager’s mood went from bad to worse, and to top it off, he even yelled at employees who had nothing to do with it.
（2）彼女かの じょは我慢が まんに我慢が まんを重かさねたあげくの果はてに、私わたしのところに相談そう だんに来きた。／After persevering and persevering, she finally came to me for advice.
This is used to describe a result that arises after a certain situation has continued for a long time and reached some sort of limit. Generally that result is not good.
