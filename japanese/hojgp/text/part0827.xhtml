【にむかって】

1. Nにむかって〈direction〉

（1）この飛行機ひ こう きは現在げん ざいボストンに向むかっています。／This plane is now heading to Boston.
（2）病人びょう にんはだんだん快方かい ほうに向むかっています。／The patient is gradually recovering.
（3）両国りょう こくの関係かん けいは最悪さい あくの事態じ たいに向むかって一気いっ きに進すすんでいった。／The relationship between the two countries has escalated to the worst possible scenario.
（4）春はるに向むかってだんだん暖あたたかくなってきた。／It has gotten warmer on its way to spring.
（5）このトンネルは出口で ぐちに向むかって下くだり坂ざかになっている。／This tunnel slopes down toward the exit.
Expresses a direction of something moved, or a resultant state after some time or some situation has changed. にむかって in (1)-(3) indicates a destination of “this plane,” “the patient,” or “the relationship between the two countries,” respectively, and can be used as a sentential predicate, as shown in (1) and (2). にむかって in (4) and (5) is followed by a clause expressing some change and expresses the meaning of “some change of state occurs as it is approaching somewhere.” For instance, にむかって in (4) expresses that as spring is coming, some change of state, namely, a rise in temperature, is caused.

2. Nにむかって〈face something〉

（1）机つくえに向むかって本ほんを読よむ。／She sits at the desk to read a book.
（2）黒板こく ばんに向むかって座すわる。／He sits in front of the blackboard.
（3）マリア像ぞうに向むかって祈いのりを捧ささげる。／She prays to the statue of the Virgin Mary.
（4）私わたしの部屋へ やは正面しょう めんに向むかって左側ひだり がわにあります。／My room is on the left side of the house as you face it.
With a noun expressing a person or a thing, means to be in a position directly facing some person or thing.

3. Nにむかって〈other person〉

（1）親おやに向むかって乱暴らん ぼうな口くちをきくな。／Don’t speak so roughly to your parents!
（2）敵てきに向むかって発砲はっ ぽうする。／He fires at the enemy.
（3）上司じょう しに向むかって反抗的はん こう てきな態度たい どを示しめす。／She shows a defiant attitude toward her boss.
With a noun expressing a person, indicates the person towards whom someone takes some attitude or action. Can be rephrased as ...にたいして.
