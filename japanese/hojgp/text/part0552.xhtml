【どうも】

1. どうも〈uncertain〉

（1）母ははのことがどうも気きになってならない。／Somehow I can’t help thinking about my mother.
（2）最近さい きん、彼かれはどうも様子よう すがおかしい。／There’s been something strange about him recently.
（3）あの人ひとの考かんがえていることはどうもよく分わからない。／I just don’t understand what makes that person tick.
（4）努力ど りょくはしているのだが、どうもうまくいかない。／We’re doing our best, but it’s just not going well.
（5）今日きょうは朝あさからどうも気分き ぶんがふさぐ。／For some reason I’ve been feeling down since this morning.
（6）A:奥おくさんの具合ぐ あいはいかがですか。／How is your wife doing?
B:それがどうもね...。／Well, you know, it’s hard to say....
Expresses the speaker’s uncertainty or doubt regarding a current situation or his/her own senses or emotions. Means something like, “I don’t really know why that is so/why I feel this way.” The predicate is the negative form of a verb/adjective or an expression that conveys a negative evaluation, such as 変だ (it’s strange), おかしい (odd), or 気分がふさぐ (feel down). (6) is a circumlocution that does not verbalize the intended あまりよくない (not very good). どうも can often be substituted with なんだか or 何となく.

2. どうも　...そうだ／...ようだ／...らしい

（1）この空模様そら も ようではどうも雨あめになりそうだ。／This sky looks like we’re going to get some rain.
（2）彼かれの言いったことはどうも全部ぜん ぶうそのようだ。／It seems as if everything he said was a lie.
（3）おじの病気びょう きはどうもガンらしい。／Our uncle's illness looks like it might be cancer.
When accompanied by expressions such as そうだ (look/seem), ようだ (looks like), or らしい (appear/seem), どうも expresses a conjecture on the part of the speaker based on some piece of evidence.

3. どうも〈dismay〉

（1）ちっとも勉強べん きょうしないで遊あそんでばかりで、どうも困こまった息子むすこです。／My son gives me no end of headaches, since he never studies and only wants to have a good time.
（2）A:先輩せん ぱい、一曲いっ きょく歌うたってくださいよ。／(to a senior student/colleague) Senpai, sing a song for us, please.
B:これは、どうもまいったな。／Oh boy, now what do I do?
Used along with expressions like 困った (troublesome/distressing) and まいった (Oh, no!), this emphasizes a feeling of dismay or slight surprise.

4. どうも〈salutation〉

（1）お手紙て がみどうもありがとうございます。／Thank you very much for your letter.
（2）お待またせしてどうもすいません。／I am so sorry to have kept you waiting.
（3）先日せん じつはどうも。／Thank you for the other day.
Used in greetings and salutations to emphasize a feeling of appreciation or apology. As in (3), the latter part of the sentence is sometimes omitted. Actually often used in expressions like どうも、どうも, which is merely a conventionalized greeting that conveys no particular feeling.
