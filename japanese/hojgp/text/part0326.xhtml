【そうだ2】

［Na そうだ］

［A-そうだ］

［R-そうだ］

Has the same conjugation as na-adjectives and so changes to そうにV and そうなN. When it is used in the negative form, it is possible to say Na そうではない and A-そうではない, but R-そうではない is hardly ever used. R-そうもない／そうにない／そうにもない are used instead.

1. ...そうだ〈appearance, manner〉

a. ...そうだ

［Na そうだ］

［A-そうだ］

（1）その映画えい がはおもしろそうだ。／That movie looks interesting.
（2）彼女かの じょはいつもさびしそうだ。／She always seems lonely.
（3）おいしそうなケーキが並ならんでいる。／Delicious-looking cakes are lined up all in a row.
（4）今日きょうは傘かさを持もって行いった方ほうがよさそうだ。／It would probably be better to take along an umbrella today.
（5）あの人ひとはお金かねがなさそうだ。／That person seems to have no money.
（6）久ひさしぶりに彼かれに会あったが、あまり元気げん きそうではなかった。／I saw him for the first time in a long while, and he didn’t look very well.
（7）子供こ どもは人形にん ぎょうをさも大事だい じそうに箱はこの中なかにしまった。／The child tucked the doll into the box as if it were very precious.
（8）いかにも重おもそうな荷物に もつを持もっている。／He’s carrying a very heavy-looking piece of luggage.
（9）彼かれは一見いっ けんまじめそうだが実じつは相当そう とうな遊あそび人にんだ。／On the surface he looks like a serious person, but he’s really quite a playboy.
（10）このおもちゃはちょっと見みたところ丈夫じょう ぶそうだが、使つかうとすぐに壊こわれてしまう。／At first glance this toy looks sturdy, but it breaks right away when you use it.
Expresses the speaker’s judgment regarding a state of affairs, based on something s/he has seen, heard, or done. いい becomes よさそう, as in (4), and ない becomes なさそう, as in (5). ...そうだ is sometimes accompanied by adverbs such as さも or いかにも for emphasis, as in (7) and (8). It is usually not used to refer to attributes that can easily be identified by sight alone, such as きれいだ or 赤い.
（×）彼女はきれいそうだ。／She seems pretty.
（○）彼女はきれいに見える。／She looks pretty.
Also, when used with 一見 or ちょっと見たところ, as in (9) and (10), it is often followed by a clause that indicates “but this is not actually the case.”
Seeみたいだ (Usage 2) for the difference between そうだ and みたいだ.

b. ...そうにみえる

［Na そうにみえる］

［A-そうにみえる］

（1）誕生たん じょうパーティーで彼女かの じょはいかにもしあわせそうに見みえた。／She looked very happy at the birthday party.
（2）彼かれは若わかそうに見みえるが来年らい ねんは60才さいになる。／He looks young, but next year he’ll be sixty.
（3）なんだか気き分ぶんが悪わるそうに見みえますが大丈夫だい じょう ぶですか。／You don’t look well. Are you all right?
（4）その問題もん だいはむずかしそうに見みえたがやってみるとそうでもなかった。／The question looked hard, but after I tried answering it, it wasn’t so bad.
Means that something looks a certain way, based on outside appearance.

c. ...そうにしている

［Na そうにしている］

［A-そうにしている］

（1）彼女かの じょはいつもはずかしそうにしている。／She always seems shy.
（2）先生せん せいはお元気げん きそうにしておられたので、安心あん しんしました。／Our teacher was looking well, so I was relieved.
（3）その人ひとはコートも着きずに寒さむそうにしていた。／That person, not even wearing a coat, looked cold.
（4）その子こはいやそうにして遊あそび場ばからひとり離はなれて座すわっていた。／The child was sitting alone away from the playground, looking annoyed.
Attaches to adjectives that express a feeling or sensation and means that an action is being performed with a particular demeanor or manner. Can be replaced by そうだ, but then the meaning that some kind of action is being performed is lost.

2. R-そうだ〈possibility of occurrence 1〉

a. R-そうだ

（1）星ほしが出でているから明日あ すは天気てん きになりそうだ。／The stars are out, so tomorrow will probably be sunny.
（2）今年ことしは雨あめが多おおいから、桜さくらはすぐに散ちってしまいそうだ。／It’s been raining a lot this year, so the cherry blossoms will likely fall quickly.
（3）服ふくのボタンがとれそうだ。／A button on my (suit) is about to come off.
（4）反対はん たい運動うん どうは全国ぜん こくに広ひろがりそうな気配け はいだ。／It looks like the opposition movement will probably spread all over the country.
（5）今日きょう中じゅうに原稿げん こうが書かけそうだ。／I’ll probably be able to finish the manuscript sometime today.
（6）今夜こん やは涼すずしいからぐっすり眠ねむれそうだ。／It’s cooler tonight so I think I’ll be able to sleep really well.
（7）暑あつくて死しにそうだ。／This heat is killing me.
（8）ジェット機きの音おとがうるさくて、気きが変へんになりそうだ。／The noise from the jet planes is about to drive me crazy.
Used to express the judgment that there is a strong possibility an event could occur. Attaches to verbs that do not express intention, such as なる and 落ちる, as well as to V-れる verbs that express potential, such as 書ける and 眠れる. When accompanied by expressions such as もうちょっとで or 今にも, as below, it expresses the feeling that a situation is imminent or urgent.
（例）あの古い家はもうちょっとで倒れそうだ。／That old house looks like it’s about to fall down.
（例）その子は今にも泣き出しそうな顔をしていた。／The child looked like he was about to burst into tears.
Examples (7) and (8) are idiomatic expressions that express the degree of severity of the situation in a figurative way.

b. R-そうになる

（1）道みちが凍こおっていて、何度なん どもころびそうになった。／The road was icy and I nearly fell down several times.
（2）車くるまにぶつかりそうになって、あわてて道みちの端はしにとびのいた。／I was about to be hit by a car, so I frantically jumped over to the side of the road.
（3）びっくりして持もっていたグラスを落おとしそうになった。／I was surprised and nearly dropped the glass I was holding.
（4）私わたしには子供こ どものころ犬いぬにかまれそうになった記憶き おくがある。／I remember nearly being bitten by a dog when I was a child.
（5）私わたしには、くじけそうになるといつもはげましてくれる友ともがいる。／I have friends who always encourage me when I am about to lose heart.
Means that a situation beyond the speaker’s control comes very close to occurring. Often used to describe past events, as in (1)-(4) above. Sometimes used with adverbs such as あやうく (barely, narrowly) and あわや (nearly, on the verge of), in which case it conveys the sense that a situation is pressing or urgent.
（例）山で遭難して、あやうく命を失いそうになった。／I had a mishap in the mountains and nearly lost my life.
In (1) and (3), R-そうになる can be replaced by V-るところだ.

c. R-そうもない

R-そうにない

（1）この本ほんは売うれそうもない。／This book probably won’t sell.
（2）仕事し ごとは明日あ すまでには終おわりそうもない。／It doesn’t look like I’ll be finished this work before tomorrow.
（3）雨あめは夜よるに入はいっても止やみそうになかった。／Even after nightfall, the rain showed no sign of letting up.
（4）一人ひとりの力ちからではとうてい出来で きそうにもない。／This doesn’t at all seem like something one person can do alone.
（5）民家みん かはちょっとやそっとでは壊こわれそうもないほど頑丈がん じょうな造つくりだった。／Traditional Japanese-style houses were so well-built that it would take a lot to make them collapse.
（6）社長しゃ ちょうは歳としをとってはいるが、元気げん きだからなかなか辞やめそうにもない。／The company president is getting older, but since he’s in good health, it doesn’t look like he’ll be quitting any time soon.
Means that a certain event is not likely to happen. Used in the forms R-そうもない, R-そうにない, and R-そうにもない.

3. R-そうだ〈possibility of occurrence 2〉

（1）あの様子よう すでは二人ふたりはもうじき結婚けっ こんしそうだ。／Judging from the way things are going, those two will probably get married very soon.
（2）彼かれはもう10日かも無断む だんで休やすんでいる。どうも会社かい しゃを辞やめそうだ。／He’s been off work without notice for ten days already. It looks as if he’s going to quit the company.
（3）彼女かの じょは熱心ねっ しんにパンフレットを見みていたから、誘さそったら会員かい いんになりそうだ。／Since she was looking at the pamphlet so intently, she’d probably become a member if someone asked her to join.
（4）あんなに叱しかったら、あの子こは家出いえ でしそうな気きがします。／If you scold him so much, I have a feeling he’s likely to run away from home.
Attaches to verbs that express the intentional act of a third person, and expresses the judgment that there is a strong possibility such an action or event will occur. Unlike そうだ (Usage 2), this そうだ is not usually used to refer to the speaker.
（×）私は会社をやめそうだ。／It looks as if I’m going to quit the company.

4. R-てしまいそうだ

（1）おいしいから全部ぜん ぶ食たべてしまいそうだ。／It’s delicious, so I just might finish it all.
（2）1度どやめていたタバコをまた吸すってしまいそうだ。／Even though I quit smoking, I feel like I might be about to start again.
（3）警察けい さつのきびしい尋問じん もんを受うけたら、組織そ しきの秘密ひ みつをしゃべってしまいそうな気きがする。／If I get grilled by the police, something tells me I’ll end up giving away all the organization’s secrets.
Used with verbs that express an intentional act and expresses a feeling of fear or concern that something may happen against one’s will. Often used to describe one’s own acts/actions.
