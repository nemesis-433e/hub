【ところが】

1. ところが〈contrary to prediction〉

（1）天気てん き予報よ ほうでは今日きょうは雨あめになると言いっていた。ところが、少すこし曇くもっただけで、結局けっ きょくは降ふらなかった。／The weather forecast was for rain today. However, it was only a little cloudy, and we never got any rain after all.
（2）ダイエットを始はじめて3週間しゅう かんになる。ところが、減へった体重たい じゅうは、わずか1キロだけだ。／It’s been three weeks since I started dieting. But I’ve only lost one measly kilogram.
（3）いつもは8時半じ はんごろ会社かい しゃに着つく。ところが、今日きょうは交通こう つう事故じ こに巻まき込こまれ、1時間遅じ かん おくれで到着とう ちゃくした。／She always gets to work around 8:30 a.m. But today she was involved in a traffic accident and arrived an hour late.
（4）兄あには大変たい へんな秀才しゅう さいである。ところが弟おとうとは大だいの勉強べん きょう嫌ぎらいで、高校こう こうを無事ぶ じに卒業そつ ぎょうできるかどうかも危あやぶまれている。／The older brother is really brilliant. On the other hand, the younger brother absolutely hates to study, and there's concern about whether or not he'll even be able to finish high school.
（5）A:春休はる やすみはゆっくりされているんでしょうね。／I guess you’re taking it easy this spring vacation, right?
B:ところが、締しめ切きり原稿げん こうがあってそうもしていられないんです。／As a matter of fact, I have an article deadline, so I really can't take it easy at all.
（6）A:来週らい しゅうのパーティには是非ぜ ひいらしてくださいね。／Please tell me you’ll come to the party next week!
B:ところが、その日ひ急きゅうに予定よ ていが入はいってしまったんです。／Actually, something unexpected has come up that I have to do that day.
Used to connect two sentences when the content of the second sentence contradicts or clashes with what is naturally anticipated or expected from the first sentence. Also used when there is a relationship of contrast between two situations, as in (3) and (4). In conversations like (5) and (6), it is used to tell the addressee something like, “That’s what you may think, but the reality is different.” In other words, this usage signals a discrepancy between the other person’s expectations and reality.
In all cases, the sentence after ところが contains an expression that indicates an established fact. Non-factual expressions, including expressions of intention, desire, command, or conjecture, cannot be used. Note the examples of incorrect usage given below.
（×）合格はかなり難しそうだ。ところが、受験してみるつもりだ（意志）／挑戦してみたい（希望）／頑張れ（命令）／ひょっとしたら受かるかもしれない（推量）。／It looks like it will be difficult to pass. However, I intend to try to take the test. (intention)/...I want to take up the challenge. (desire)/...do your best! (command)/...I might just pass after all. (conjecture)

2. ところが〈discovery〉

（1）急いそいで家いえを出でた。ところが、途中と ちゅうで財布さい ふを忘わすれていることに気きがつき、あわてて引ひき返かえした。／I left home in a hurry. Then, on the way, I realized I had forgotten my wallet and hurried back.
（2）友人ゆう じんの家いえに電話でん わした。ところが、1週間前しゅう かん まえから海外旅行かい がい りょ こうに行いって留守る すだという。／I called my friend’s house. But I was told that he’s not home because he’s been out of the country on a trip since a week ago.
Used when the speaker is surprised by a new turn of events, or an event that was hard to anticipate from the situation or course of events described in the previous sentence. The sentence after ところが is information newly-discovered by the speaker that contradicts what would naturally be anticipated from the situation described in the previous sentence.
This usage of ところが does not usually allow for substitution with expressions like しかし, けれども, or だが, and when substitution is possible, the meaning of the sentence changes.
