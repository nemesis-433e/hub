【たらどうか】

［V-たらどうか］

（1）別べつの方法ほう ほうで実験じっ けんしてみたらどうでしょうか。／How about experimenting with a different method?
（2）少すこしお酒さけでも飲のんでみたらいかがですか。気分き ぶんがよくなりますよ。／Why don’t you take a little something—even alcohol? You’ll feel better.
（3）遊あそんでばかりいないで、たまには勉強べん きょうしたらどう？／Instead of playing all the time, how about studying occasionally?
（4）さっさと白状はく じょうしたらどうなんだ。／Now, come on—let's fess up!
（5）アメリカに留学りゅう がくしてみたらどうかと先生せん せいに勧すすめられた。／I was encouraged by my teacher to consider study abroad in the U.S.
（6）A:吉田よし だ君くん、パーティーには出席しゅっ せきしないって。／Yoshida-kun said that he won’t join the party.
B:もう一度いち ど誘さそってみたら？／Why don’t you invite him again?
Idiomatic expression used in a proposal or a recommendation. Oten used in the form V-てみたらどうか. Has almost the same meaning as てはどうか, but たらどうか is more colloquial. In colloquial language, たらどうなの／どうかしら is used by women, たらどうなんだ is used by men, and たらどう is used by both men and women. However, in formal situations, たらいかがですか／いかがでしょうか is used.
(3) and (4) are examples of when the listener simply won’t follow the speaker’s warning or recommendation; they imply the speaker’s irritation. The latter half of (6) is abbreviated and pronounced with a rising tone.
