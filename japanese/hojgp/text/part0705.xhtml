【なまじ】

（1）なまじ急いそいでタクシーに乗のったために、渋滞じゅう たいに巻まき込こまれてかえって遅刻ち こくしてしまった。／Because I was in a rush and jumped into a taxi, I ended up getting caught in a traffic jam and arriving late.
（2）今いまの段階だん かいでなまじ私わたしが発言はつ げんすれば、かえって事態じ たいを混乱こん らんさせることになりかねない。／If I were to make an incomplete comment at this stage, it would only confuse the situation.
（3）なまじ自信じ しんがあったのがわざわいして、重大じゅう だいなミスを犯おかしてしまった。／Overconfidence in my own ability led me to make to a serious mistake.
（4）なまじ彼女かの じょの状況じょう きょうが理解り かいできるだけに、こんな仕事し ごとはとても頼たのみづらい。／It’s because I understand her current situation that it’s hard to ask her to take this job on.
（5）なまじの知識ち しきは役やくに立たたないどころか邪魔じゃ まになることもある。／A little knowledge is not just useless; it can even be a hindrance.
（6）彼女かの じょの前まえではなまじなことは言いわない方ほうがいい。彼女かの じょはこの問題もん だいを徹底的てっ てい てきに調しらべているらしいから、我々われ われもそのつもりで準備じゅん びしなければ負まけてしまう。／We shouldn’t say anything half-baked in front of her. It sounds like she’s been investigating this problem thoroughly, so if we don’t prepare with that in mind, we’ll be outplayed.
Expresses a situation in which something positive ends inconclusively or only halfway, without displaying its full value. Used to say that something with an intrinsically positive value actually results in a negative outcome.
When なまじ modifies a noun, it is used in the patterns なまじのN and なまじなN, as in (5) and (6). In these cases, なまじ can be replaced by 中途半端な (halfway, unfinished). For instance, (5) means that knowledge, which is usually a good thing to have, instead becomes a hindrance because it is superficial.
