【にも】

1. Nにも

In this pattern, the particle も is added to the combination of a noun with the particle に attached, in order to emphasize the noun.

a. Nにも〈time, place, direction, other person, etc.〉

（1）あそこにも人ひとがいます。／There are people over there too.
（2）田中た なかさんにも教おしえてあげよう。／I’ll tell Tanaka-san, too.
（3）箱根はこ ねにも日光にっ こうにも行いきました。／I went not only to Hakone but also to Nikko.
も is used to emphasize phrases with the particle に that express various meanings, adding the meaning that “the same can be said not only about that, but also about something else, too.” When it is not necessary to add the meaning of “also, too,” Nに is used by itself.

b. Nにも〈target of respect〉

（1）ご家族か ぞくのみなさまがたにもおすこやかにお過すごしのことと拝察はいさつ申もうし上あげます。／I trust that your family is also doing well. (formal conventional greeting)
（2）皆々様みな みな さまにもご健勝けん しょうにお過すごしの由よし、お喜よろこび申もうし上あげます。／I am pleased to know that you are all fine. (formal conventional greeting)
にも is placed after a noun that indicates someone of higher status, and it is used to express the speaker’s respect for that person. Used only in idiomatic expressions, such as greetings in a very formal letter. ...におかれましては is an even more formal expression that may be used instead.

2. V-ようにも

a. V-ようにも...ない

（1）助たすけを呼よぼうにも声こえが出でない。／Even though I tried to cry out for help, I had no voice.
（2）機械き かいを止とめようにも、方法ほう ほうが分わからなかったのです。／Even though I wanted to stop the machine, I didn’t know how.
（3）先さきに進すすもうにも足あしが疲つかれて一歩いっ ぽも踏ふみ出だせなかった。／Although I tried to move forward, my legs were too tired to take a step.
（4）手術しゅ じゅつをしたときはすでに手遅て おくれで、助たすけようにも助たすけようがなかったのです。／When she had the operation, it was already too late, so there was no way to help her.
Placed after the intentional form of a verb, such as 呼ぼう (will call out) or 止めよう (will stop), and followed by a negative expression. Means “even though I want to do something, I cannot do it.”

b. V-ようにもV-れない

（1）少すこし休やすみたいけれど、忙いそがしくて休やすもうにも休やすめない。／I’d like to rest a little, but I’m too busy to take time off.
（2）こんなに遠とおくまで来きてしまっては、帰かえろうにも帰かえれない。／Having come such a long way, I couldn’t go home even if I tried.
（3）こんな恐おそろしい事件じ けんは、忘わすれようにも忘わすれられない。／No matter how hard I try, I can’t forget a horrible incident like this.
（4）土砂崩ど しゃ くずれで道みちがふさがれており、それ以上い じょう進すすもうにも進すすめない状態じょう たいだった。／The road was blocked by a landslide, so the situation was that no one could proceed beyond that point, try as they may.
にも is placed after the intentional form of a verb, such as 帰ろう (will go home) or 忘れよう (try to forget), and followed by the negative potential form of the same verb. Means “even though I want to do something, I cannot do it” or “I cannot do...no matter how hard I try.”
