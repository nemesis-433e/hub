【まえ】

1. Nのまえに

（1）駅えきの前まえに大おおきなマンションが建たった。／A big apartment building has been built in front of the station.
（2）僕ぼくの前まえに田中た なかが座すわっていた。／Tanaka was sitting in front of me.
（3）食事しょく じの前まえに手てを洗あらいましょう。／Let’s wash our hands before our meal.
（4）授業じゅ ぎょうの前まえに先生せん せいのところへ行いくように言いわれた。／I was told to go and see the teacher before the class.
Represents a spatial or a chronological relationship. Examples (1) and (2) show the spatial relationship where N is positioned right in front or ahead of something/someone. In the cases of (3) and (4), it indicates that something takes place before the event N.

2. V-るまえに

（1）食事しょく じをする前まえに手てを洗あらいましょう。／Let’s wash our hands before we eat.
（2）私わたしは、夜よる寝ねる前まえに軽かるく一杯いっ ぱい酒さけを飲のむことにしている。／I always have a little nightcap before I go to bed.
（3）大学だい がくを卒業そつ ぎょうする前まえに、一度いち どゆっくり仲間なか まと旅行りょ こうでもしてみたい。／I would love to take a leisurely trip with my friends before I graduate from college.
（4）結婚けっ こんする前まえには、大阪おお さかの会社かい しゃに勤つとめていました。／Before I got married, I was working for a company in Osaka.
Taking the form of XまえにY, it indicates that the event Y takes place before the event X. Regardless of the tense of the sentence, the verb immediately preceding ...まえに always takes the dictionary form.
（○）食事をする前に手を洗った。／Before eating, we washed our hands.
（×）食事をした前に手を洗った。／Before we ate, we washed our hands.

3. Nをまえに（して）

（1）国会議員こっ かい ぎ いんのA氏しは記者団き しゃ だんを前まえに終始しゅう し上機嫌じょう き げんだった。／Congressman A stayed in an upbeat mood in front of the pool of reporters.
（2）テーブルの上うえの書類しょ るいの山やまを前まえに、どうしたらいいのか、途方と ほうにくれてしまった。／Facing the mountain of paperwork on the table, I had absolutely no idea what I should do.
（3）試験し けんを前まえにして、学生がく せいたちは緊張きん ちょうしていた。／As the examination was approaching, students were getting nervous.
（4）首相しゅ しょうは出発しゅっ ぱつを前まえに、記者会見き しゃ かい けんを行おこなう予定よ てい。／Before his departure, the Prime Minister plans to hold a press conference.
Represents a spatial or chronological relationship. Examples (1) and (2) indicate that something/someone is facing something/someone. In the cases of (3) and (4), an event is just about to take place. For the chronological relationship, it is possible to replace it with ...をひかえて.
