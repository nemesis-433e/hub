【わ...わ】

1. ...わ...わ（で）

（1）昨日きのうは山登やま のぼりに行いったが、雨あめに降ふられるわ道みちに迷まようわで、散々さん ざんだった。／Yesterday I went mountain-climbing but it rained and I got lost; it was terrible.
（2）今週こん しゅうは試験し けんはあるわレポートの締切しめ きりは近ちかいわで、寝ねる間まもない。／This week I have an exam and the deadline of my report is close; there’s barely time to sleep.
（3）このごろ忙いそがしくて、もう家事か じはたまるわ、まともな食事しょく じはしないわ...。／I’m so busy these days; the housework is piling up, I’m not eating properly....
（4）あいつは高校生こう こう せいのくせにタバコは吸すうわお酒さけは飲のむわ無断む だん外泊がい はくはするわ、悪わるいことばかりしていて親おやを泣なかせている。／That guy’s a senior high school student but he smokes, drinks alcohol and stays out all night without permission; he reduces his parents to tears just doing bad things all the time.
When several bad things occur all at once, this is used to list them as exemplificatory and to emphasize a feeling of annoyance. What follows are details of how (it) was terrible because of those things or that the person was annoyed because of those things.

2. V-るわV-るわ

（1）新あたらしくできた水族館すい ぞく かんに行いったら、人ひとがいるわいるわ、魚さかななんか全然ぜん ぜん見みえないぐらいの人出ひと でだった。／When I went to the new aquarium there were people on top of people! It was such a large turnout that I couldn’t see the fish at all.
（2）忙いそがしくて新聞しん ぶんがたまるわたまるわ、もう2週間分しゅう かん ぶんも読よんでいない。／I’m really busy and the newspapers keep piling up; I haven’t read them for two weeks already.
（3）部屋へ やを久ひさしぶりに掃除そう じしたら、ごみが出でるわ出でるわ、段だんボール箱ばこにいっぱいになった。／When I cleaned my room for the first time in a long while, there was so much rubbish that it filled a cardboard box.
Repeating the same verb, expresses surprise that the quantity of a thing or the frequency of an occurrence is higher than expected. Often, details of the resulting situation follow.
