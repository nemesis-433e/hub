【ぬ】

An auxiliary verb that expresses negation in classical Japanese. In modern Japanese, traces of ぬ remain in the ん found in expressions like ...ません (do not...), 知らん (don’t know), and 好かん (don’t like). Also used in a number of idiomatic fixed expressions.

1. V-ぬ

（1）知しらぬ存ぞんぜぬで（＝知しらないと主張しゅ ちょうし続つづけて）押おし通とおす。／She persists in denying that she knows anything at all.
（2）知しらぬが仏ほとけ（＝相手あい てが知しらないうちに何なにか相手あい てにとって都合つ ごうの悪わるいことをする場合ば あいに用もちいる）。／Ignorance is bliss. (= an expression used when someone does something disadvantageous to the other person without that person noticing)
（3）予期よ きせぬ（＝予期よ きしない）事件じ けんが起おこった。／An unexpected incident has occurred.
（4）急いそいで対策たい さくを考かんがえなければならぬ。／We must quickly consider countermeasures.
Used in fixed expressions that are idioms. Means ...ない (not...). (4) is the literary form of the expression ...なければならない.

2. V-ぬうちに

（1）誰だれにも気付き づかれぬうちにここを抜ぬけ出だそう。／Let’s get out of here before anyone finds us.
（2）暗くらくならぬうちに家いえにたどり着つけるといいのだが。／I hope I can get home before it gets dark....
Literary form of the expression ...ないうちに.

→【うち】2c

3. V-ぬばかり

（1）おまえは馬鹿ば かだと言いわぬばかりの顔かおをした。／The look on his face was almost as if he were saying, “You’re stupid.”
（2）泣なかぬばかりに懇願こん がんした。／On the verge of tears, she implored me.
Used in fixed, idiom-like expressions and means “it looks as if someone is about to do something.” Literary form of the expression V-んばかり.

→【ばかり】6

4. V-ぬまでも

（1）この崖がけから落おちたら、死しに至いたらぬまでも重傷じゅう しょうはまぬがれないだろう。／If you fall off this cliff, you probably can’t escape serious injury, even if you don’t die.
（2）実刑じっ けいは受うけぬまでも罰金ばっ きんは払はらわせられるだろう。／Even if he’s not sentenced to imprisonment, he’ll probably be made to pay a fine.
Literary form of the expression ...ないまでも.

→【ないまでも】

5. V-ぬまに

（1）鬼おにのいぬ間まに洗濯せん たく（＝邪魔じゃ まになる人ひとがいない間あいだにしたいことをする）。／When the cat’s away, the mice will play. (= do whatever you want while a person who will stop you is away)
（2）知しらぬ間まにこんなに遠とおくまで来きてしまった。／I have come this far without realizing it.
Used in idiomatic fixed expressions. Means “while not doing....”
