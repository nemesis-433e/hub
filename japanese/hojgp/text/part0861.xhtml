【のだろう】

［N／Na　なのだろう］

［A／V　のだろう］

This expression is a combination of のだ and だろう. In spoken Japanese, the form んだろう is often used.

1. ...のだろう〈supposition, conjecture〉

...んだろう

（1）大川おお かわさんはうれしそうだ。何なにかいいことがあったのだろう。／Okawa-san looks happy. I guess something good must have happened to him.
（2）子供こ どもはよく眠ねむっている。今日きょう一日いち にちよく遊あそんだのだろう。／The child is sleeping soundly. I guess she played hard all day today.
（3）大おおきなスーパーマーケットができて一年いち ねんもしないうちに、前まえの八百屋や お やは営業えい ぎょうをやめてしまった。きっと、お客きゃくをみんなとられたのだろう。／In less than a year after the big supermarket was built, the fruit and vegetable store out front closed down. I’m sure all their customers were taken away by the supermarket.
（4）実験じっ けんに失敗しっ ぱいしたのにこのような興味深きょう み ぶかい結果けっ かが得えられたのには、何なにか別べつの要因よう いんがあるのだろう。／There must be some other factor that explains why we were able to obtain interesting results like this, even though the experiment failed.
（5）この製品せい ひんは特別とく べつに売うれ行ゆきがいい。きっと宣伝せん でんが上手じょう ずなんだろう。／This product sells particularly well. The ad campaign must be pretty good.
Used with falling intonation to express a conjecture. When の is inserted right before だろう, it implies a judgment on the part of the speaker with respect to a certain event, such as a supposition about the reason or cause for it.

2. ...のだろう〈confirmation〉

...んだろう

（1）A:10 年ねんぶりの同窓会どう そう かいだね。君きみも行いくんだろう？／It’s the first class reunion in ten years. You’re going too, right?
B:うん、行いくつもりだ。／Yes, I’m planning to.
（2）A:来月らい げつディズニーランドに行いくの。／I’m going to Disneyland next month.
B:え、また？　もう何回なん かいも行いったんだろう？／What, again? Haven’t you already been there lots of times?
A:うん。でもおもしろいんだもん。／Yeah, but it’s fun!
（3）A:来週らい しゅうは試験し けんだから、週末しゅう まつは忙いそがしいんだろう？／You have exams next week, so you’re probably busy over the weekend, aren’t you?
B:うん。まあね。／Yeah. Sort of.
（4）A:新あたらしいコンピュータ買かったんだって？　新型しん がたは便利べん りなんだろう？／I heard you bought a new computer. The new models are really handy, aren’t they?
B:ええ。本当ほん とうに便利べん りですよ。／Yes, it’s really handy.
Used with rising intonation to seek confirmation. When の／ん is placed right before だろう, it implies that the speaker is trying to confirm something on the basis of information or a supposition derived from the preceding context or situation. Usually used in spoken Japanese by males. Often used in the form んだろう.

3. ...のだろうか

...んだろうか

（1）子こどもたちが公園こう えんにたくさんいる。今日きょうは学校がっ こうが休やすみなのだろうか。／There are lots of children in the park. I wonder if today is a school holiday.
（2）A:山口やま ぐちさんこの頃ごろ元気げん きがないね。／Yamaguchi-san doesn’t look good these days.
B:うん。顔色かお いろも悪わるいし、体からだの具合ぐ あいでも悪わるいのだろうか。／Yeah. She looks pale, so I wonder if maybe she’s sick or something.
（3）A:来年らい ねんは入試にゅう しだというのに、太郎た ろうったら、全然ぜん ぜん勉強べん きょうしようとしないんですよ。／That Taro—even though he has entrance exams next year, he doesn’t want to study at all.
B:うん。あれでどこかの高校こう こうに入はいれるんだろうか。心配しん ぱいだなあ。／I know. If he keeps this up, I wonder whether he’ll be able to get into a high school. I’m worried about him.
（4）A:山下やま したさん、うれしそうね。／Yamashita-san looks happy, doesn’t she?
B:ほんとだね。何なにかいいことでもあったんだろうか。／She really does. I wonder if something good happened to her.
Expresses a feeling of suspicion or worry on the part of the speaker. Used when the speaker is making a conjecture about something, based on contextual information or the situation at hand.
