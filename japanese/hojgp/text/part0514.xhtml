【と2】

1. 数すう量りょう詞し+と〈repetition〉

（1）人々ひと びとは一人ひとり、また一人ひとりとやってきた。／One person came, and then another and another.
（2）星ほしが、一ひとつ、また一ひとつと消きえていく。／One by one the stars disappear.
（3）白鳥はく ちょうが一羽いち わ、また一羽いち わと湖みずうみに降おり立たった。／The swans alighted on the lake one by one.
As in 一人、また一人 (one person, then another) or 一つ、また一つ (one by one), the quantifier “one” is repeated to indicate the sporadic repetition of an event. Used in written language.

2. 数すう量りょう詞し+と〈cumulative increase〉

（1）人々ひと びとは一人ひとり、二人ふたりと集あつまってきた。／The people assembled in ones and twos.
（2）このコンクールも二回に かい、三回さん かいと回かいを重かさねるうちに、だんだんよくなってきた。／This contest has gradually improved, after being held on two or three occasions.
（3）二度に ど三度さん どと失敗しっ ぱいを繰くり返かえして、ようやく成功せい こうにこぎつけた。／After two or three repeated failures, I finally managed to achieve success.
A situation of gradually increasing amount or frequency is expressed by listing a small number with the next largest one.

3. 数すう量りょう詞し+とV-ない

（1）禁煙きん えんしようという彼かれの決心けっ しんは三日みっかと続つづかなかった。／His decision to quit smoking didn’t even last for three days.
（2）あの人ひとは気きが短みじかいから、5分ふんと待まっていられない。／That person has little patience, so she can’t wait even five minutes.
（3）A:これだけビールを買かっておけばだいじょうぶでしょう。／If we buy this much beer, it should be enough, right?
B:いや、客きゃくが多おおいから1時間じ かんともちませんよ。／No, there are a lot of guests, so it won’t even last an hour.
（4）あんなに宣伝せん でんしたのに、参加者さん か しゃは二に十人じゅう にんと集あつまらなかった。／After all that publicity, we didn't even get twenty participants.
This pattern uses a quantifier indicating a short time or a small amount, followed by the negative form of a verb, to express the notion that this limited number was not nearly sufficient.

5. 擬ぎ態たい語ご+と

（1）彼かれはゆっくりと立たち上あがった。／He stood up slowly.
（2）雨あめがザーッと降ふってきた。／The rain started to fall in buckets.
（3）雨あめがぽつり、ぽつりと降ふり始はじめた。／It began to rain, a drop here, a drop there.
（4）列車れっ しゃはガタンガタンとがたんと動うごき始はじめた。／The train began to move with a bump and a jerk.
（5）傷口きず ぐちがずきんずきんと痛いたむ。／The wound throbbed with pain.
と is attached to mimetic or onomatopoeic words to express the manner of an action or effect. とmay be omitted in some cases. Also, repetition of the mimetic or onomatopoeic word, as in (3)-(5), implies that the action/effect is repeated or that the onset of the action/effect is slow and gradual.
