【なにか】

1. なにか〈things〉

（1）冷蔵庫れい ぞう こに何なにか入はいっているから、お腹なかがすいたら食たべなさい。／There’s something in the fridge, so when you get hungry, eat.
（2）この穴あなは何なにかでふさいでおいたほうがいいでしょう。／We should probably cover this hole with something.
（3）何なにか質問しつ もんはありませんか。／Do you have any questions?
（4）壁かべに何なにか堅かたいものがぶつかったようなあとがある。／There’s a mark on the wall where it looks like something hard hit it.
（5）私わたしに何なにかお手伝て つだいできることはありませんか。／Is there anything I can do to help?
Indicates a thing or event that cannot be referred to in definite terms. Often used adverbially, but can also be used with particles, as in なにかで (see (2) above), なにかが, and なにかを. In colloquial Japanese, なんか is used instead.

2. なにか〈appearance〉

（1）彼かれの態度たい どは何なにか不自然ふ し ぜんだ。／There’s something about his behavior that’s a little odd.
（2）彼女かの じょのことが何なにか気きになってしかたがない。／I just can’t get her out of my mind.
（3）この景色けしきを見みているといつも何なにか寂さびしい気持き もちになってくる。／This scenery always makes me feel a little melancholy.
Implies “I don’t know exactly why I feel this way, but for some reason I do.” In colloquial Japanese, なにか becomes なんか.

3. ...かなにか

［N／V　かなにか］

（1）コーヒーか何なにか飲のみませんか。／Would you like some coffee or something?
（2）はさみか何なにかありませんか。／Do you have scissors or something?
（3）石いしか何なにかの堅かたいもので殴なぐられた。／I got hit with something hard, like a stone or something.
（4）吉田よし ださんは、風邪か ぜをひいたか何なにかで会社かい しゃを休やすんでいます。／Yoshida-san is off work because he has the flu or something.
...かなにか follows a noun or verb and is used to indicate a thing or event that cannot be referred to in definite terms, but is similar to the one indicated by the preceding noun or verb. Particles that follow Nかなにか, such as が and を, are often omitted. In colloquial Japanese, かなにか is replaced by かなんか.

4. Nやなにか

（1）休やすみの日ひは雑誌ざっ しや何なにかを読よんでのんびり過すごします。／I take it easy on my days off, reading magazines and other stuff.
（2）かばんの中なかには洗面用せんめんよう具ぐや何なにかの身みの回まわり品ひんが入はいっていた。／Inside the suitcase were toiletries and other personal effects.
（3）A:何なにを盗ぬすまれたんですか。／What was stolen?
B:金庫きん こは荒あらされていなかったんですが、たんすの中なかの宝石ほう せきや何なにかがなくなっています。／The safe wasn’t ransacked, but some jewelry and other things that were in my drawers are missing.
...やなにか attaches to a noun and is used to indicate a certain item and other things similar to that item. In contrast to Nかなにか, which means “a thing that is similar to N,” Nやなにか means that there is N and also other things similar to N. Nやなにか is usually followed by a particle. In colloquial Japanese, Nやなんか is used instead.

5. なにか〈cross-examination〉

（1）それならなにか。この会社かい しゃを辞やめてもいいんだな。／And just what does that mean? You’re looking to quit this company, aren’t you?
（2）君きみはなにか、僕ぼくに責任せき にんがあると言いいたいのか。／So do you mean to say that I’m the one to blame for this?
なにか is uttered with a rising tone and used to press the addressee strongly for an explanation or answer. It often conveys a feeling of criticism or reproach on the part of the speaker. Male speakers use this expression in spoken Japanese to address others of equal or lower status.
