【なにかしら】

（1）なにかしらアルバイトをしているので、生活せい かつには困こまりません。／Because I do part-time work of one kind or another, I can make ends meet.
（2）いつもなにかしらお噂うわさを聞きいております。／I have been hearing a lot about you.
（3）家いえのことがなにかしら気きにかかったので、急いそいで帰かえってきた。／Something worried me about my house, so I hurried back home.
（4）息子むすこは最近さい きんなにかしら反抗的はん こう てきな態度たい どを取とる。／Recently my son has been rebelling against anything and everything.
Indicates a thing or event that cannot be described in definite terms. Also implies that there is not just a single thing or event, but many others as well. This expression originally comes from なにか知らぬ or なにか知らん (I don’t know what...).
