【とすると】

すると is attached to the particle と and refers to the preceding clause. Means “if we assume that to be a fact,” or “in the event that...occurs,” or “based on the present situation/facts.” Followed by the expression of a judgment that would hold in the event that the preceding assumption is made. This pattern is used to establish a set of hypothetical conditions and conveys the meaning “in the event that a certain assumption is made.” と by itself does not have this meaning, so とすると can hardly ever be replaced by と.

1. ...とすると〈not-yet-realized condition〉

［N／Na　だとすると］

［A／V　とすると］

（1）医学部い がく ぶに入はいるとすると、一体いっ たいどのくらいお金かねが必要ひつ ようなのだろうか。／If I were to enter the school of medicine, I wonder just how much money I would need.
（2）もし、今後こん ごも雨あめが降ふらないとすると、水不足みず ぶ そくになるのは避さけられないだろう。／If the lack of rainfall continues, it probably won't be possible to avoid a water shortage.
（3）仮かりに被告ひ こくが言いっていることが事実じ じつだとすると、彼女かの じょは嘘うその証言しょう げんをしていることになる。／If we assume that the defendant is telling the truth, that means her testimony is a lie.
Means “if we assume that such-and-such is a fact/will occur,” even though the speaker doesn’t actually know whether or not the thing in question is a fact/will occur. Often accompanied by 仮に (supposing that) or もし (if).

2. ...とすると〈already-realized condition〉

［N／Na　だとすると］

［A／V　とすると］

（1）1時間じ かん待まってまだ何なんの連絡れん らくもないとすると、途中と ちゅうで事故じ こにでもあったのかもしれない。／If there hasn’t been any word from him/her whatsoever after waiting a full hour, it could mean that there was an accident or something along the way.
（2）A:図書館と しょ かんは明日あしたから2週間しゅう かん休館きゅう かんになります。／The library is going to close tomorrow for two weeks.
B:2週間しゅう かん休館きゅう かんだとすると今日きょうのうちに必要ひつ ような本ほんを借かりておかなければならないな。／ If it's going to be closed for two weeks, then I guess I’ll have to borrow the books I need today.
Means “taking into account the current situation/facts,” based on the actual situation or information received from another party. かりに (supposing that) and もし (if) do not combine with this usage.

3. （だ）とすると

（1）A:今年ことしの2月がつの平均へい きん気温き おんは平年へい ねんより数度すう ども高たかいそうですよ。／They say that this year the average temperatures in February have been several degrees above normal.
B:とすると、桜さくらの開花かい かも早はやくなるでしょうね。／If that’s so, then the cherry trees will probably bloom early, won’t they?
（2）脱線だっせん事故じ こで今日きょう一日いち にち電車でん しゃは不通ふ つうの見込み こみだという。だとすると、道路どう ろは相当そう とう混雑こん ざつするだろう。／I heard that, because of a derailment, the trains are expected to stop running all day today. If that’s the case, the roads will probably be pretty congested.
Refers to the previous sentence or a statement made by another person and means “taking into account the present situation/facts.”
