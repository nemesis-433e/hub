【つつ】

Used with a verb stem. Usually used in written language or formal conversation.

1. R-つつ〈simultaneity〉

（1）彼かれは、「春はるももう終おわりですね」と言いいつつ、庭にわへ目めをやった。／Saying “Spring is over, isn’t it,” he cast his eyes to the garden.
（2）静しずかな青あおい海うみを眺ながめつつ、良子よし こは物思もの おもいにふけっていた。／Yoshiko was in deep thought while looking at the quiet blue sea.
（3）この会議かい ぎでは、個々こ この問題点もん だい てんを検討けん とうしつつ、今後こん ごの発展はっ てんの方向ほう こうを探さぐっていきたいと思おもいます。／At this meeting, I hope that as we consider each problem point, we can explore ways to move forward for developing.
（4）その選手せん しゅはけがした足あしをかばいつつ、最後さい ごまで完走かん そうした。／Favoring his injured leg, the athlete ran the race to completion.
Expresses that the same subject does one action while also doing another. Has roughly the same meaning as ...ながら, but ...つつtends to be used in written language.

2. R-つつ〈adversative connective〉

a. R-つつ

（1）夏休なつ やすみの間あいだ、勉強べん きょうしなければいけないと思おもいつつ、毎日まい にち遊あそんで過すごしてしまった。／I knew I had to study during the summer vacation, but I spent it having fun every day.
（2）早はやくたばこをやめなければいけないと思おもいつつ、いまだに禁煙きん えんに成功せい こうしていない。／Even while thinking that I really ought to quit smoking right way, I still haven’t succeeded in giving it up.
（3）その言いい訳わけはうそと知しりつつ、わたしは彼かれにお金かねを貸かした。／I lent him money, all the while knowing that the pretext was a lie.
（4）青木あお きさんは事業じ ぎょうのパートナーを嫌きらいつつ、常つねに協力きょう りょくを惜おしまなかった。／Aoki-san, even while disliking his project partner, never held back from cooperating with him.
Used to link two contrary matters. For example, (1) means “although I thought.” Close to the adversative use of のに or ながら. “All the while knowing it was a lie” in (3) is an often-used idiomatic expression.

b. R-つつも

（1）彼かれは、歯痛は いたに悩なやまされつつも、走はしり続つづけた。／He continued running even while being bothered with a toothache.
（2）「健康けん こうのために働はたらき過すぎはよくないのよ」と言いいつつも、彼女かの じょは決けっして休暇きゅう かをとらないのだ。／She keeps saying “Overwork is not good for your health,” yet never takes a vacation herself.
（3）医者い しゃに行いかなければと思おもいつつも、忙いそがしさに紛まぎれて忘わすれてしまった。／All the while knowing I had to see a doctor, I was distracted by being busy and forgot all about it.
（4）設備せつ びの再調査さい ちょう さが必要ひつ ようだと知しりつつも無視む ししたことが、今回こん かいの大事故だい じ こにつながったと思おもわれる。／It is believed that this calamity is linked to the fact that, although (the responsible parties) were well aware of the necessity for a follow-up evaluation of the facilities, they ignored it.
Same as【つつ】(Usage 2 a).

3. R-つつある

（1）地球ち きゅうは温暖化おん だん かしつつある。／The planet is on its way to becoming warmer.
（2）この会社かい しゃは現在げん ざい成長せい ちょうしつつある。／The company is currently growing.
（3）この海底かい ていでは長大ちょう だいなトンネルを掘ほりつつある。／A long tunnel is presently being dug below this sea bottom.
（4）手術しゅ じゅつ以来い らい、彼かれの体からだは順調じゅん ちょうに回復かい ふくしつつある。／His health has been improving as hoped since the operation.
（5）若わかい人ひとが都会と かいへ出でて行いくため、五ご百年ひゃく ねんの伝統でん とうのある祭まつりの火ひがいまや消きえつつある。／Because young people are leaving for the city, the 500-year-old tradition of having a fire at the festival is about to die out.
（6）彼かれは今いま自分じ ぶんが死しにつつあることを意識い しきしていた。／He was conscious of the fact that he was dying.
（7）その時代じ だいは静しずかに終おわりつつあった。／The era was quietly ending.
Used to express that an action or effect is moving in a certain direction. Often corresponds to ている, but there are also a few differences. つつある in (1)-(3) can be replaced by ている without changing the meaning very much, but when it is used with verbs which express instantaneous change, as in (4)-(7), replacing it will alter the meaning of the sentence. When used with verbs which express such change, using つつある gives the meaning that a change has taken place and is heading in the direction of completion.ている, on the other hand, expresses that the situation after the change has been completed. Therefore if 死につつある(dying) in (6) is replaced with 死んでいる(dead), the sentence would not make sense.
In addition, つつある cannot be used with verbs which have no meaning of completion, so 彼女は泣きつつある cannot be used.
