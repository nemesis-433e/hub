【といい】

［N／Na　だといい］

［A-いといい］

［V-るといい］

Formed by combining と1 (Usage 3) with いい. とよい is a slightly more formal form.

1. V-るといい〈suggestion, recommendation〉

（1）この株かぶは今いま買かうといいですよ。／It's good to buy this stock now.
（2）分わからないときは、この辞書じ しょを使つかうといい。／When you don’t understand, this dictionary is helpful.
（3）旅行りょ こうには、小ちいさいドライヤーを持もっていくといい。／Taking a small blow-dryer on trips is a good idea.
（4）疲つかれたようだね。仕事し ごとは急いそがなくてもいいから、ソファで少すこし寝ねるといい。／You seem tired. Since this work is not urgent, you should lie down on the sofa for a while.
（5）私わたしを疑うたがいたければ存分ぞん ぶんに疑うたがうといい。／If you want to doubt me, then go ahead and doubt!
Follows the dictionary form of a verb and expresses the notion of encouraging another person to take an action. Depending on the context, it can also be used to express a hands-off attitude, as in (5), which has the sense of “do as you like.” Cannot be used to dissuade someone from taking an action. In such cases, the form V-ないほうがいい is used.
（×）今買わないといい。／It’s good not to buy it now.
（○）今買わないほうがいい。／You shouldn't buy it now.
Similar expressions include たらいい and ばいい, but といい implies the encouragement that “generally speaking, doing such-and-such is appropriate.” The form といい cannot be used in interrogative expressions inquiring what one ought to do. In that case, たらいい／ばいい are used instead. When answering such questions, however, it is possible to use not only たらいい／ばいい but also といい. Then たらいい／ばいい convey the meaning that “such-and-such would be sufficient” to obtain a particular result. On the other hand, といい means that “such-and-such is generally an appropriate course of action.”
（×）うまくいかない時はどうするといいですか。／What’s good to do when things don't go well?
（◯）A:うまくいかない時はどう｛したら／すれば｝いいですか。／A：When things don't go well, what should I do?
B:山本さんに｛聞いたら／聞ければ／聞くと｝いいですよ。／It would be best to ask Yamamoto-san.

2. ...といい〈wish, desire〉

（1）生うまれてくる子供こ どもが、女おんなの子こだといいなあ。／It would be nice if the baby on the way is a little girl.
（2）学生がく せいがもっと積極的せっ きょく てきだといいのだが。／How I wish the students would take a little more initiative!
（3）勉強べん きょう部屋べ やがもっと広ひろいといいのになあ。／If only this study room were a little more spacious....
（4）旅行りょ こうの間あいだ、晴天せい てんが続つづくといい。／Let's hope that the fair weather holds during the trip.
（5）彼かれが時間じ かんに間まに合あうといいんだけど。／I hope that he makes it in time, but....
（6）みんながこのことを忘わすれていないといいが。／You would all do well never to forget this.
（7）学生がく せいの自発的じ はつ てきな活動かつ どうが今後こん ごも継続けい ぞくされるといい。／It would be nice to see these voluntary activities by the students continue.
Expresses a desire for something to happen. Often used with が, けど, のに, or （のに）なあ at the end of the sentence. When accompanied by が, けど, or のに, the implication is concern that “such-and-such may not be realized,” or that the present state of affairs does not reflect what is hoped for. Has essentially the same meaning as たらいい and ばいい, and can be substituted with these in almost all cases.

3. ...とよかった（のに）

（1）A:とても楽たのしい旅行りょ こうだったわよ。あなたも来くるとよかったのに。／It was a very enjoyable trip. It would have been nice if you had come along.
B:行いけるとよかったんだけど、急用きゅう ようができてしまってね。／It would have been great to be able to go, but something suddenly came up.
（2）本当ほん とうのことを言いってくれるとよかったのに。／If only you had told me the truth.
（3）この部屋へ や、もう少すこし日当ひ あたりがいいとよかったんだが。／I wish this room could have had a little more sunlight.
Expresses a feeling of regret or criticism of the listener, arising from the fact that something did not actually happen or that the present reality is contrary to expectations. This usage takes the forms ばよかった or たらよかった more often than とよかった. The sentence often concludes with expressions such as のに, のだが, or のだけれど. However, のに is not usually used regarding one's own actions.
（×）僕も行けるとよかったのに。／I should have been able to go, too....
（○）僕も行けるとよかったん｛だけど／だが｝。／If only I had been able to go, too....
