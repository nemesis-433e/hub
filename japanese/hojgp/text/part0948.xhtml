【まで】

1. NからNまで

（1）シンポジウムは1時じから3時じまで第だい3会場かい じょうで行おこないます。／The symposium will be held from one o’clock until three o’clock in venue number three.
（2）A:大阪おお さかから東京とう きょうまでどのぐらいかかりますか。／Approximately how long does it take from Osaka to Tokyo?
B:新幹線しん かん せんなら3時間じ かんぐらいでしょう。／If you take the shinkansen (bullet train) it will take about three hours.
（3）《ホテルで》／《at a hotel》
A:シングルでいくらですか。／How much is a single?
B:シングルのお部屋へ やは、7500 円えんから 12000 円えんまでとなっております。／A single room costs from 7,500 yen up to 12,000 yen.
（4）教科書きょう か しょの 25 頁ぺーじから 35 頁ぺーじまで読よんでおいてください。／Please read from page 25 to page 35 of the textbook in advance.
（5）この映画えい がは、子供こ どもからお年寄とし よりまでご家族か ぞくみんなで楽たのしんで頂いただけます。／This film can be enjoyed by all the family together, from children to the elderly.
（6）A:昼休ひる やすみは何時なん じまでですか。／Until what time is lunch break?
B:1時じまでです。／Until one o’clock.
Used with nouns of time, place, or quantity to indicate a range or span. When it is not necessary to state the starting point, Nまで can be used by itself.

2. Nまで〈destination〉

（1）バスに乗のらずに駅えきまで歩あるいて行いくことにした。／I decided to walk to the station without taking the bus.
（2）公園こう えんまで走はしりましょう。／Let’s run to the park.
（3）毎日まい にち学校がっ こうまで歩あるきます。／I walk to school every day.
（4）川幅かわ はばが広ひろくて向むこう岸ぎしまで泳およげそうもない。／The river is so wide it’s probably impossible to swim to the other side.
（5）先週せん しゅうの日曜日にち よう びは、散歩さん ぽがてら隣となりの町まちまで行いってみた。／Last Sunday I went out for a walk to the neighboring town.
（6）A:京都きょう とにはどうやって行いったらいいですか。／What would be a good way to get to Kyoto?
B:そうですねえ。山手やまのて線せんで東京駅とう きょう えきまで行いって、新幹線しん かん せんに乗のるのが一番いち ばん早はやいと思おもいますよ。／Hmm. I think the quickest way is to go to Tokyo Station on the Yamanote Line and then take the shinkansen (bullet train).
（7）わからないことがありましたら、係かかりまでおたずね下ください。／If there is something you don’t understand, please ask the person on duty.
Used with verbs such as 行く, 来る, 歩く, 走る, 泳ぐ and so on, expresses where the movement ends. Verbs such as 歩く, 走る, 泳ぐ and the like cannot be directly attached to に and へ, but they can be used together with まで.
（○）公園まで走りましょう。／Let’s run to the park.
（×）公園｛に／へ｝走りましょう。／Let’s run to the park.
（○）毎日学校まで歩きます。／I walk to school every day.
（×）毎日学校｛に／へ｝歩きます。／I walk to school every day.
（○）向こう岸まで泳いだ。／I swam to the other side.
（×）向こう岸｛に／へ｝泳いだ。／I swam to the other side.
Also, as can be understood from the next examples, as まで demonstrates the place at which continuing movement ends, it cannot be used when referring to two places at the same time.
（○）イタリアではローマとミラノ｛に／へ｝行った。／In Italy, I went to Rome and Milan.
（×）イタリアではローマとミラノまで行った。／In Italy, I went to Rome and Milan.
係まで in (7) has the same meaning as 係に, but its tone is more formal.

3. ...まで〈time〉

a. Nまで

（1）3時じまで勉強べん きょうします。／I will study until three o’clock.
（2）きのうは結局けっ きょく朝方あさがたまで飲のんでいた。／Yesterday I ended up drinking till the wee hours.
（3）私わたしはなまけもので、日曜日にち よう びはもちろん普通ふ つうの日ひでも、たいてい11 時じ頃ごろまで寝ねている。／I am a lazy person, so not only on Sundays but also on ordinary days I usually sleep until about eleven o’clock.
（4）ついこのあいだまでセーターを着きていたのに、この二三日に さん にち急きゅうに暖あたたかくなった。／Even though I was wearing a sweater until just the other day, these last two or three days it has suddenly become warm.
（5）祖父そ ふは死しぬ直前ちょく ぜんまで意識い しきがはっきりしていた。／My grandfather was conscious until just before he died.
Attached to a time noun, this indicates that an action or occurrence continued up until the point in time denoted by まで. After it, expressions which demonstrate the continuation of an action or situation are used. It cannot be used with expressions which demonstrate the occurrence of an event.
（×）5時まで到着します。／We will arrive by five o’clock.
（○）5時までに到着します。／We will arrive by five o’clock.
Please see the entry for までに regarding the difference between まで and までに.

b. V-るまで

（1）あなたが帰かえってくるまで、いつまでも待まっています。／I will wait forever until you return.
（2）私わたしがいいと言いうまで目めをつぶっていてください。／Please keep your eyes closed until I say it’s OK.
（3）辻つじさんは結婚けっ こんして退職たい しょくするまで、貿易ぼう えき会社がい しゃに勤つとめていたそうだ。／I heard that Tsuji-san had been working at a trading company until she got married and resigned.
（4）《医者い しゃが患者かん じゃに》もう少すこし暖あたたかくなるまで外出がい しゅつはしないほうがいいでしょう。／《a doctor to a patient》It is best that you don’t go out until it has gotten a little warmer.
（5）佐藤さ とうさんが会社かい しゃを辞やめるなんて、昨日きのう山田やま ださんに聞きくまで知しりませんでした。／I didn’t know that Sato-san would be leaving the company until I heard it from Yamada-san yesterday.
（6）肉にくがやわらかくなるまで、中火ちゅう びで煮にます。／Cook the meat over medium heat until it becomes tender.
Attached to a clause which includes an event, signifies the continuation of an action or situation until that event occurs. As in example (6), it is often used when explaining a procedure or process and here instructs one to stop boiling the meat at the stage where it has become tender after being cooked over medium heat.

4. ...まで〈degree〉

a. Nまで

（1）近頃ちか ごろは子供こ どもばかりか、いい年としをしたおとなまでマンガを読よんでいる。／These days, not only children but even fully grown-up adults read manga.
（2）きみまでそんなことを言いうのか。／Even you say such a thing?
（3）一番いち ばん信頼しん らいしていた部下ぶ かまでが、彼かれを裏切うら ぎった。／Even his most trusted subordinate betrayed him.
（4）子供こ どもにまでばかにされている。／Even children make fun of me.
（5）そんなつまらないものまで買かうんですか。／Are you going to buy even such a worthless thing?
（6）落おちぶれた身みには、風かぜまでが冷つめたい。／To a man who has fallen so low, even the wind seems cold and unfeeling.
（7）だんだん暗くらくなって来くるのにさがしている家いえは見みつからない。その上うえ、雨あめまで降ふってきた。／It is getting dark and we still can’t find the house we are looking for. On top of that, it has even started to rain.
（8）今年ことしはいいことばかりだ。新あたらしい家いえに引ひっ越こしたし、子供こ どもも生うまれた。その上うえ、宝たからくじまで当あたった。／This year has brought only good things. We moved to a new house and had a baby. On top of that, we even won the lottery.
（9）私わたしにも悪わるい点てんはあるが、そこまで言いわれたら、黙だまってはいられない。／I admit that I have my own bad points, but if you are going to say that much, I can’t keep quiet any more.
（10）生活せい かつに困こまって盗ぬすみまでするようになった。／I was so hard up that it led me to stealing.
Used to convey the speaker’s surprise when describing a situation in which something beyond the usual in addition to the usual things has happened. Example (1), for example, means “Usually manga are read by children. However, these days, not only children but also adults are reading them.” Example (7) means “It was bad enough that it was getting dark, but the fact that it started raining made the situation even worse.” Example (8) expresses, conversely, that on top of a good thing, an even better thing has happened. As in 子供にまで in example (4), this expression can take the pattern N + particle + まで.

b. V-るまでになる

（1）苦労く ろうの甲斐か いあって、やっと日本語に ほん ごで論文ろん ぶんが書かけるまでになった。／His efforts have paid off and he has progressed to where he can finally write an essay in Japanese.
（2）人工じん こう飼育し いくされていたひなは、ひとりで餌えさがとれるまでに成長せい ちょうした。／The hand-reared chick has grown enough to be able to get food by itself.
（3）リハビリの結果けっ か、ひじを曲まげられるまでになった。／As a result of rehabilitation, I have improved to where I can now bend my elbow.
In addition to なる, can be used together with verbs that show change such as 成長する, 育つ, 回復する, and 進歩する. Expresses how the current effect or situation has been reached after a long period of time or through effort. It is often used to express the outcome of striving to achieve something.

c. V-るまで（のこと）もない

（1）この程度てい どの風邪か ぜなら、医者い しゃに行いくまでのこともない。うまいものを食たべて、一日いち にちぐっすり眠ねむれば治なおる。／If your cold is no worse than this, there is no need to go to the doctor. You will recover if you eat tasty things and sleep soundly for one day.
（2）その程度てい どの用事よう じならわざわざ出向で むくまでもない。電話でん わでじゅうぶんだ。／You don’t need to make a point of going out to do that kind of errand. It’s enough to do it by telephone.
（3）皆みなさんよく御存知ご ぞん じのことですから、わざわざ説明せつ めいするまでもないでしょう。／It’s something you all know well, so I don’t think there’s a need for me to give any particular explanation.
（4）改あらためてご紹介しょう かいするまでもありませんが、山本やま もと先生せん せいは世界的せ かい てきに有名ゆう めいな建築家けん ちく かでいらっしゃいます。／There’s no need for me to introduce him again, but Yamamoto-sensei is an internationally famous architect.
（5）田中た なか先生せん せいは、御専門ご せん もんの物理学ぶつ り がくは言いうまでもなく、平和へい わ運動うん どうの推進者すい しん しゃとしてたいへん有名ゆう めいであります。／I don’t need to tell you that Tanaka-sensei’s specialty is physics, and he is very famous as a promoter of the peace movement.
（6）子供こ どもの頃ころ、兄あにが大事だい じにしていた万年筆まん ねん ひつを持もちだしてなくしてしまったことがある。後あとでひどく怒おこられたことは言いうまでもない。／When I was a child I took a fountain pen that was very important to my older brother and lost it. It goes without saying that he was very angry with me afterwards.
Means “it is not necessary to do....” Can be used in cases where, as in examples (1) and (2), “as the degree to which something is the case is so slight, it is unnecessary to do... / it is okay not to do....” Can also be used, as in examples (3) to (5), in cases where “as something is a matter of course, it is unnecessary to do....” In example (6), it means “of course, afterwards he was angry with me.”

d. ...までして

［Nまでして］

［V-てまで］

（1）色々いろ いろほしいものはあるが、借金しゃっ きんまでして買かいたいとは思おもわない。／There are many things I want but not to the extent that I would take out a loan to buy them.
（2）徹夜てつ やまでしてがんばったのに、テストでいい点てんが取とれなかった。／Even though I made the effort to study all night, I still didn’t get a good score on the test.
（3）彼かれが自殺じ さつまでして守まもりたかった秘密ひ みつというのは何なんだろう。／I wonder what secret he wanted to hide so much that he chose to commit suicide for it.
（4）彼かれは、友ともだちを騙だましてまで、出世しゅっ せしたいのだろうか。／I wonder if he wants to succeed even to the extent that he would cheat his friends.
（5）自然し ぜんを破壊は かいしてまで、山やまの中なかに新あたらしい道路どう ろをつくる必要ひつ ようはない。／It is not necessary to go so far as to destroy nature just to build new roads in the mountains.
Used with phrases that express extremities, this means “to do such a thing as....” As in examples (4) and (5), it is used to express criticism of methods of which the speaker does not approve used to achieve an aim. It is often used in such contexts as “It is not good to take such steps in order to...” or “(I) wouldn’t want to do such a thing in order to....” It can also be used, as in examples (2) and (3), to mean, “to have made greater efforts than usual” or “to have made tremendous sacrifices to reach a goal.”

5. ...までだ

a. V-るまで（のこと）だ

（1）父ちちがあくまで反対はん たいするなら、家いえを出でるまでのことだ。／If my father opposes it to the end, it is something I will leave home for.
（2）もし入学にゅう がく試験し けんに失敗しっ ぱいしても、私わたしはあきらめない。もう一年いち ねんがんばるまでのことだ。／Even if I fail the entrance exam, I won’t give up. It is something I’ll work hard for another year for.
Shows the speaker’s determination “not to be discouraged, even if the current method is no good, and to try another way.”

b. V-たまで（のこと）だ

（1）そんなに怒おこることはない。本当ほん とうのことを言いったまでだ。／You don’t have to be so angry. I only spoke the truth.
（2）妻つま:どうして子供こ どもたちに結婚けっ こんする前まえの話はなしなんかしたんですか。／Wife: Why did you talk about us before we were married in front of the children?
夫おっと:聞きかれたから答こたえたまでで、別べつに深ふかい意味い みはないよ。／Husband: I only told them because they asked. There wasn’t any particular reason.
Expresses that the speaker has carried out a certain action without an ulterior motive or agenda and simply for the reason given.

c. これ／それ　までだ

（1）いくらお金かねを貯ためても、死しんでしまえばそれまでだから、生いきているうちに楽たのしんだ方ほうがいい。／However much money you save, you can’t take it with you when you die, so it’s better to enjoy it while you’re alive.
（2）運うんがよかったと言いってしまえばそれまでだが、彼かれがあの若わかさで成功せい こうしたのにはそれなりの理由り ゆうがある。／You could just say he was lucky, but there must be a reason why he was successful at such a young age.
（3）もはや、これまでだ。／No more—I’ve had it.
Used in the form V-ば、それまでだ, means “that is the end.” Also, as in example (3), it is used idiomatically at times when the speaker has found him/herself in a desperate situation or facing a dilemma.

6. V-ないまでも

（1）喜よろこびはしないまでも、いやがりはしないだろう。／I wouldn’t go so far as to say she would delight in it, but she wouldn’t hate it, either.
（2）優勝ゆう しょうとは言いわないまでも、ベスト4ぐらいはねらいたい。／I wouldn’t go so far as to say I want to win, but I’m aiming at being somewhere around the top four.
