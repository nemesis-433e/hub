【が1】

1. Nが

（1）あの人ひとが山本やま もとさんです。／That person is Yamamoto-san.
（2）隣となりのうちには猫ねこが3匹びきいる。／There are three cats in the house next door.
（3）あ、財布さい ふが落おちている。／Look, there’s a wallet on the floor.
（4）この本ほんは表紙ひょう しがきれいですね。／This book has a beautiful cover, doesn’t it.
（5）私わたしはジャズが好すきです。／I like jazz.
（6）外交官がい こう かんになるには語学力ご がく りょくが必要ひつ ようだ。／You need to be good at languages to be a diplomat.
（7）彼かれは 10 ヶ国語こく ごができるらしい。／I heard that he can speak ten different languages.
Attaches to a noun and indicates that the noun is the subject of an action or state, as in (1), (2), (3), and (4), or that the noun is the object of a state, as in (5), (6), and (7). When used in proverbs or idioms, it sometimes attaches to words other than nouns, as in 負けるが勝ち (Sometimes you have to lose to win).

2. NがNだから

（1）親おやが親おやだから、子供こ どもがあんなふうに生意気なま い きになるのだ。／With such parents, it’s no wonder the child is so bratty.
（2）もう時間じ かんが時間じ かんだし、今いまから行いってもあのレストランは閉しまってるかもしれないよ。／Considering what time it is now, the restaurant might be closed by the time we get there.
（3）デパートをぶらぶら歩あるいていて、かわいいネックレスを見みつけた。とても気きに入いったのだが、なにしろ値段ね だんが値段ね だんだったので買かうのはあきらめた。／I was wandering around the department store and found a pretty necklace. I really liked it, but with the price being what it was, I decided not to buy it.
（4）A:再就職さい しゅう しょくしようと思おもったけど、なかなかむずかしいわ。／I thought I’d try to go back to work, but it’s really quite difficult to find a job.
B:そりゃ、年としが年としだもの。70すぎた人間にん げんなんか今いまどきどこも雇やとってくれないわよ。／Well, it’s your age. These days no one wants to hire someone in his seventies.
The same noun is repeated and then followed by a word expressing a reason, such as だから, ので, し, だもの, or もので. In many cases, this pattern expresses a negative evaluation of the noun and is used to describe an inevitable result that arises from it. For example, (1) means the parents are too indulgent of their children, (2) means it is too late to go to the restaurant, (3) means the price is far too expensive, and (4) means that, in B’s opinion, A is too old to get another job.

3. NがNだけに

（1）ここの料理りょう りは、素材そ ざいが素材そ ざいだけに味あじも格別かく べつだ。／The food here is exceptionally tasty because of the ingredients.
（2）この店みせは味あじは大たいしたことはないが、場所ば しょが場所ば しょだけにたいていいつも満員まん いんだ。／The food at this restaurant isn’t particularly good, but its location means it’s almost always full.
（3）この店みせはとても気きに入いっているのだが、場所ば しょが場所ば しょだけにそうしょっちゅうは来こられないのが残念ざん ねんだ。／I really like this shop, but because it’s located where it is, unfortunately I can’t come very often.
（4）その映画えい がは戦時中せん じ ちゅうの日本軍に ほん ぐんの侵略しん りゃくを扱あつかったもので、多おおくの評論家ひょう ろん かが絶賛ぜっ さんしている優すぐれた作品さく ひんだが、内容ない ようが内容ない ようだけに、一般的いっ ぱん てきな娯楽ご らく映画えい がと比くらべると興行こう ぎょう成績せい せきは格段かく だんに悪わるかった。／The movie deals with the aggression of the Japanese army during the war. It is an excellent work that many critics have praised highly, but because of the subject matter, box office returns were markedly worse than those of ordinary movies made for entertainment.
（5）学長がく ちょうが収賄しゅうわい容疑よう ぎで逮捕たい ほされた。今いままでも小ちいさな不祥事ふ しょう じはあったが、マスコミには騒さわがれないよう注意ちゅう いしてきた。しかし、今回こん かいはことがことだけに、マスコミの取材しゅ ざいからは逃のがれられないだろう。／The president of the university was arrested on suspicion of bribery. There have been minor scandals before, but care was always taken to keep the media from making anything of them. However, this time, with the situation being what it is, there will be no stopping the mass media from covering the story.
Repetition of the same noun indicates that a certain outcome is natural or inevitable, considering the characteristics of that noun. NがNだけに is followed by a description of the natural or inevitable outcome. The listener/reader does not know the exact nature of the noun’s characteristics until s/he reaches the description in the latter part of the sentence. For example, in (1), the description 味も格別だ (exceptionally tasty) tells us the ingredients are good. However, in the sentence 素材が素材だけに大した料理はできやしない (Because the ingredients are what they are, the food can’t be very good), the interpretation is that the ingredients are bad. (2) means a place that is conveniently located and easy to get to. On the other hand, (3) means a place that is inconveniently located and hard to get to. ことがことだけに in (5) is an idiomatic expression. In this example, it means “because this situation is serious.”

4. NがNなら...（が）

（1）時代じ だいが時代じ だいなら、この本ほんも売うれたかもしれないが、今いまの時代じ だいでは人間にん げんの生いき方かたを問とうような本ほんは若わかい人ひとには読よまれない。／If times were different, this book might have sold well, but in this day and age, young people don’t read books that raise questions about how to live.
（2）世よが世よならあいつも出世しゅっ せできただろうに。／In another time and place, she probably would have been successful.
（3）俺おれも大学だい がくが大学だい がくならもう少すこしましな仕事し ごとにもつけたのだろうが、この大学だい がくではせいぜいこの会社かい しゃぐらいがいいところだ。／I suppose if I had graduated from a better university, I could have found a better job, but this is the best company anyone from a university like mine could hope to work for.
Repetition of the same noun indicates that the speaker is making the supposition “if the conditions were suitable....” NがNなら is accompanied by expressions such asだろうが, だろうに or かもしれないが, and a statement or description of the actual state of affairs then follows. (1) means “if it were a time when many people thought seriously about how to live,” (2) means “if people in society evaluated him correctly,” and (3) means “if I had graduated from a better university.” This pattern is used to hypothesize a situation that is better than reality; it means “if the situation were X, a good result like Y would follow.” Because the situation hypothesized is one that is actually impossible, a sense of pity, regret, or resignation is implied.

5. NがNならNもNだ

（1）この生徒せい とはいつも教師きょう しに口答くち ごたえばかりして困こまる。親おやもすぐ学校がっ こうにどなりこんでくるし、まったく、親おやが親おやなら子こも子こだ。／This student always causes trouble to his teachers by answering back. His parents often come storming into the school with complaints, too. With such parents, it’s no wonder the children behave the way they do.
（2）まったく、おじさんがおじさんなら、おばさんもおばさんだよ。おじさんが頑固がん こなのはわかっているんだから、嘘うそでも「ごめんなさい」って言いえば喧嘩けん かなんかすぐにおさまるのに。／(to an aunt and uncle) Honestly, you two are both just as bad as each other. (to the aunt) You know he’s obstinate, so if you say “I’m sorry,” even if you don’t mean it, the quarrel will quickly be resolved.
The first noun (repeated in NがNなら) is different from the second noun (repeated in NもNだ), but the two nouns are related. The expression as a whole conveys a negative evaluation: both N’s are equally bad. (1) means that the parents and child are equally problematic, and (2) means that both the uncle and aunt are bad, because they are equally stubborn. Used to criticize both parties or elements.
