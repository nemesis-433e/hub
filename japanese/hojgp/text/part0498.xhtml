【でも2】

1. Nでも

（1）この機械き かいは操作そう さが簡単かん たんで、子供こ どもでも使つかえます。／This machine is easy to operate, so even a child can use it.
（2）この算数さん すうの問題もん だいは大人おとなでもむずかしい。／This arithmetic problem is difficult even for an adult.
（3）この森もりは、夏なつでも涼すずしい。／This forest is cool even in the summer.
The pattern XでもY gives X as an extreme example that would usually not be thought of as compatible with Y, in order to express the notion that because X is Y, other things are all the more Y.

2. N（+助じょ詞し）でも

（1）コーヒーでも飲のみませんか。／Would you like something to drink, like coffee, or...?
（2）待まっている間あいだ、この雑誌ざっ しでも見みていてください。／While you’re waiting, please feel free to take a look at this magazine.
（3）A:佐々木さ さ きさん、いませんね。／Sasaki-san isn’t here, is he?
B:ああ、昼食ちゅう しょくにでもでかけたんでしょう。／Ah, he’s probably out to lunch or something.
（4）山下やま したさんにでも聞きいてみたらどう。／Why not ask, say, Yamashita-san about it?
（5）A:先生せん せいのお宅たくへ行いくとき、何なにか持もって行いきましょうか。／Shall we take something to our professor’s house when we visit?
B:そうですね。ワインでも買かって行いきましょう。／Yes, let’s buy some wine or something to take along.
（6）この夏なつは、山やまにでも登のぼってみたい。／This summer I want to do something like mountain-climbing.
（7）病気びょう きにでもなったら困こまるから、日ひごろ運動うん どうするようにしている。／It would be a big problem if I got sick or something, so I make it a point to exercise daily.
（8）宿題しゅく だいのレポートは、図書館と しょ かんででも調しらべてみることにした。／For the report I got for homework, I decided to do some research in the library and wherever.
（9）避暑ひ しょにでも行いったら元気げん きになるかもしれない。／You might get well if you go to a summer resort or someplace to get out of the heat.
（10）こんな忙いそがしいときに客きゃくでも来きたら大たい変へんなことになる。／What with being this busy, if we get an unexpected guest or something it’ll be a real problem.
（11）寒さむいからなべものでもしたらどうでしょうか。／Since it’s so cold, how about having nabe (hot pot) or something?
Used to give an example, with the implication that there are also other choices. Depending on the context, it is often used to indicate a specific thing in an indirect manner.
For example, (1) suggests “coffee or some other kind of drink,” but in (2) what is actually being referred to is the concrete action of “looking at this magazine.” In (9)-(11), でも is used to give an example in a suppositional expression that means “for example, what if you (we) do such-and-such?” However, it should be noted that in usages like (9), what the speaker actually wants to say is something like “Why don’t you get out of the heat?” which is an expression that doesn’t include でも. In general, でも is often used in indirect statements.

3. R-でもしたら

（1）放ほうっておいて、病気びょう きが悪わるくなりでもしたら、どうするんですか。／If you let it go and your illness gets worse, then what will you do?
（2）そんな大金たい きん、落おとしでもしたら大変たい へんだから、銀行ぎん こうに入いれた方ほうがいいですよ。／With such a large sum of money, it would be terrible if you lost it or something, so you’d better put it in the bank.
（3）そんなにいうならこのカメラ、貸かしてあげるけど、気きをつけてよ、こわしでもしたら承知しょう ちしないから。／If you’re going to insist, I’ll lend you this camera, but be careful— I’m not going to let you get away with it if you break it or something.
（4）こどものころ、妹いもうとを泣なかしでもしたら、いつも一番上いち ばん うえの兄あにに怒おこられた。／When I was a child, if I ever did something like make my little sister cry, my oldest brother would always get mad at me.
When でもしたら is attached to the stem of a verb, it expresses the notion of a worst-case scenario. Often used to caution someone by raising the possibility of an accident, illness, or something else that would cause problems in the event of its occurrence.
