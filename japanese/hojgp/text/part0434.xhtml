【ってば】

（1）A:この字じ、間違ま ちがってるんじゃないか？／Isn’t this letter wrong?
B:あってるよ。／No, it’s right.
A:いや、絶対ぜっ たい、間違ま ちがってるってば。／Nope, I’m sure it’s wrong.
（2）A:宿題しゅく だいやったの？／Did you do your homework?
B:うん。／Yeah.
A:もう9時じよ。／It’s already nine o’clock.
B:やったってば。／I said I did it!
（3）A:お母かあさん／Mother?
B:....／....
A:お母かあさんってば。聞きいてるの？／Mother! Are you listening?
Used to emphasize the speaker’s point in informal conversation between people who are close to each other, particularly when the speaker is a little irritated because his/her point is not communicated. Also used to attract someone’s attention, as in example (3).
