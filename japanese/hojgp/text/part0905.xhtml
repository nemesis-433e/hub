【ひとつまちがえば】

（1）出産しゅっ さんというのは大変たい へんな仕事し ごとで、医学い がくの進すすんだ現在げん ざいでもひとつまちがえば命いのちにかかわる。／Giving birth is a huge undertaking, and even with the high standard of modern medicine, one slightest mistake can make a difference of life or death.
（2）政治家せい じ かの不用意ふ よう いな発言はつ げんが続つづいている。ひとつ間違ま ちがえば外交がいこう問題もん だいにも発展はっ てんしかねない。／Politicians have been making a series of verbal blunders. One careless utterance could escalate into a diplomatic incident.
（3）カーレースは、ひとつまちがえば、大事故だい じ こにつながることもある危険き けんな競技きょう ぎである。／Car racing is a dangerous pursuit in which a small error can lead to a major accident.
（4）ひとつ間違ま ちがえば大惨事だい さん じになるところだった。／At that point, one mistake could have turned it into a disaster.
（5）乗のる予定よ ていだった飛行機ひ こう きが墜落つい らくした。ひとつ間違ま ちがえば、私わたしもあの事故じ こで死しんでいたと思おもうとぞっとする。／The airplane I was supposed to catch crashed. It chills me to think that I came so close to dying in that accident.
Means “by a very small margin.”
Shows that there is a high probability of something disastrous happening, and whether this materializes or not is determined by something very small and marginal. Often used in the form of ひとつまちがえば...こともある／かねない (if it went the wrong way...it could result in ...).
In the cases of (4) and (5), disastrous situations have been narrowly avoided. In particular, ひとつ間違えば...ところだった indicates that “although it didn’t happen, it was very close.”
