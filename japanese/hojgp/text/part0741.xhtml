【に】

1. R-にV

（1）待まちに待まった帰国き こくの日ひがついにやってきた。／The long-awaited day to return to my country has finally come.
（2）電車でん しゃは遅おくれに遅おくれて、東京駅とう きょう えきに着ついたときは夜中よ なかを過すぎていた。／The train had a very long delay, so when we got to Tokyo Station, it was already past midnight.
（3）彼かれの死しを悼いたんで、人々ひと びとは泣なきに泣ないた。／People lamented his death, so they just cried and cried.
By repeating the same verb, emphasizes that a degree of an action or influence mentioned in the sentence is extreme. Often used in past contexts.

2. V-るにV-れない

（1）人手ひと でが足たりないのでやめるにやめられない。／Because they are short of hands, I can’t quit my job now.
（2）ものすごくおかしな話はなしだったけど、みんながまじめな顔かおをして聞きいているので、笑わらうに笑わらえなかった。／Although that was a very funny story, I couldn’t laugh because everyone was listening to it with such serious faces.
（3）戦時中せん じ ちゅうは言いうに言いえない苦労く ろうをしてきた。／I was subjected to unspeakable hardships during the war.
（4）事業じ ぎょうは失敗しっ ぱいするし、妻つまには逃にげられるし、全まったく泣なくに泣なけない気持き もちだ。／Since my business failed and my wife left me, I’m too sad even to cry.
（5）ここまで深入ふか いりしてしまっては、いまさら引ひくに引ひけない。／Because I am so involved in this, there’s no way I can pull back now.
By repeating the same verb, expresses the meaning “even though one wants to do it, it cannot be done” or “one cannot possibly do something.” V-るにV-れない in (3)-(5) are examples of idiomatic expressions, and those in (3) and (4) mean that the situation is too hard for a certain action to be taken, although the speaker wants to do it. V-るにV-れない in (5) expresses the situation where the speaker cannot stop doing it.
