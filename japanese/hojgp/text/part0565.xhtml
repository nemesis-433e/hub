【とき】

1. ...とき

［Nのとき］

［Na なとき］

［A-いとき］

［V-るとき］

（1）子供こ どもの時とき、田舎いなかの小ちいさな村むらに住すんでいた。／When I was a child, I lived in a small village in the countryside.
（2）暇ひまな時ときには、どんなことをして過すごしますか。／What kinds of things do you do in your free time?
（3）祖父そ ふは体からだの調子ちょう しがいい時ときは、外そとを散歩さん ぽする。／When my grandfather feels good, he goes out for a walk.
（4）ひまのある時ときにはたいていお金かねがない。／When you have time to spare, you usually don't have money to spend.
（5）寝ねている時ときに地震じ しんがありました。／When I was sleeping, an earthquake hit.
とき is placed after the dictionary form of a predicate that refers to a state and expresses that another state or event takes place concurrently.

2. ...たとき

［N／Na　だったとき］

［A-かったとき］

［V-たとき］

（1）先代せん だいが社長しゃ ちょうだった時ときは、この会社かい しゃの経営けい えいもうまく行いっていたが、息子むすこの代だいになってから、急きゅうに傾かたむきはじめた。／When the father was president, this company did very well, but after the son took over, it suddenly started to decline.
（2）貧乏びん ぼうだった時ときは、その日ひの食たべ物ものにも困こまったものだ。／When I was broke, I had trouble even finding food for the day.
（3）子供こ どもがまだ小ちいさかった時ときは、いろいろ苦労く ろうが多おおかった。／When our children were still small, we had a lot of hardships.
（4）東京とう きょうにいた時ときは、いろいろ楽たのしい経験けい けんをした。／We had so many enjoyable experiences when we were in Tokyo.
（5）ニューヨークで働はたらいていた時ときに、彼女かの じょと知しり合あった。／I got to know her when I was working in New York.
とき is placed after the ta-form of a predicate that refers to a state and expresses that a past state or event takes place concurrently. The dictionary form can be used in the first clause, but it conveys a slightly different nuance.
（例）子供がまだ小さい時は、いろいろ苦労が多かった。／We had a lot of hardships when our children were still small.
The difference between the example above and (3) is that (3), which uses the ta-form, implies that the speaker is thinking back on a time in the past, or that “the present situation is different from the past one.” The example above, which uses the dictionary form, does not have this implication.

3. V-るとき

（1）電車でん しゃに乗のるとき、後うしろから押おされてころんでしまった。／As I got on the train, I was pushed from behind and fell down.
（2）関西かん さいへいらっしゃるときは、前まえもってお知しらせください。／When you come to the Kansai District, please let us know ahead of time.
（3）東京とう きょうへ行いくとき夜行や こうバスを使つかっていった。／I used to take the overnight bus when I went to Tokyo.
（4）父ちちは新聞しん ぶんを読よむときめがねをかけます。／My father wears glasses when he reads the paper.
とき is placed after the dictionary form of an action verb and expresses that another action or event occurs either before the first action has been performed, or at the same time. (1) and (2) are examples of the former, and (3) and (4) illustrate the latter.

4. V-たとき

（1）家いえを出でたときに、忘わすれ物ものに気きがついた。／As I left the house, I realized I had forgotten something.
（2）アメリカへ行いった時ときに、昔むかしの友人ゆう じんの家いえに泊とめてもらった。／When I went to America, I stayed at the home of an old friend.
（3）朝あさ、人ひとと会あったときは「おはようございます」と言いいます。／When you meet people in the morning, you say ohayoo gozaimasu.
（4）火事か じや地震じ しんが起おこったときにはエレベーターを使用し ようしないでください。／If there is a fire or earthquake, please do not use the elevators.
とき is placed after the ta-form of an action verb and expresses that another event or state occurs after the first action.
