【それが】

（1）10時じに会あう約束やく そくだった。それが1時じになっても現あらわれないんだ。／We had an appointment to meet at ten o’clock. But he still hadn’t shown up even by one.
（2）10時じに着つくはずだった。それが道みちに迷まよってひどく遅おくれてしまった。／I was supposed to arrive at ten o’clock. But I got lost and was really late.
（3）A:お父とうさんはお元気げん きでしょうね。／I suppose your father is in good health, isn’t he?
B:それが、このごろどうも調子ちょう しがよくないんですよ。／You’d think so, but actually he hasn’t been feeling very well lately.
（4）A:ご主人しゅ じん相変あい かわらず遅おそいの？／Does your husband still get home late?
B:それが変へんなのよ、このごろ。夕食前ゆう しょく まえにうちに帰かえってくるの。／The funny thing is, recently he’s been coming home before supper.
Means “on the contrary, nevertheless, however.” In (3) and (4) it is used to introduce information that the listener doesn’t expect.
