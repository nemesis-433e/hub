【ながす】

［R-ながす］

（1）このレポートは、何なんの調査ちょう さもせずに、思おもいついたことを適当てき とうに書かき流ながしているだけだ。／In this paper I’m just writing down anything that comes to mind, without doing any research.
（2）彼かれが着物き ものを軽かるく着流き ながした姿すがたは、なかなか粋いきである。／He cuts a fine figure, wearing his kimono so casually.
（3）ざっと読よみ流ながしただけですが、なかなか面白おも しろい本ほんですよ。／I just skimmed through it quickly, but it was really quite an interesting book.
（4）彼かれのいうことは聞きき流ながしておいてください。／Please pay no attention to what he says.
（5）老ろう政治家せい じ かは検察けん さつの執拗しつ ような追及つい きゅうも軽かるく受うけ流ながしている。／The old politician is effortlessly brushing off the prosecutor’s tenacious cross-examination.
When ながす is attached to a verb stem, it means that the action referred to by the verb is performed in an easy and relaxed manner, without a great deal of effort. If used to indicate a response to another person’s action, ながす means to dodge or evade that action without giving it serious consideration. 着流す in (2) means to wear a kimono casually, without putting on a hakama (a long pleated skirt worn over a kimono). It is often used in the noun form 着流し (casually dressed/in kimono with no hakama).
