【となっては】

1. いまとなっては

（1）今いまとなっては、名前な まえも顔かおも思おもい出だすことができない。／Now at this point, I can’t recall either his name or face.
（2）全すべてが終おわってしまった今いまとなっては、じたばたしてもしかたがない。／Now that everything is over and done with, there’s no sense in fretting.
（3）当時とう じはずいぶん辛つらい思おもいをしたものだが、今いまとなっては、それも懐なつかしく思おもい出だされる。／At the time it was quite painful for me, but now I can think back on it with some nostalgia.
Expresses the meaning “having proceeded through a variety of events to reach this point.” Often followed by expressions stating “that was to be expected/as one would think.” Though it is often followed by negative evaluations of “cannot be done” or “there’s no point anymore,” as in (1) and (2), it can also be a neutral evaluation, as in (3).

2. ...となっては

［N／Na　（だ）となっては］

［A／V　となっては］

（1）子供達こ ども たちだけで海外かい がいへ行いくとなっては、親おやとしてはちょっと心配しん ぱいになる。／With the children going overseas all by themselves, as a parent I am a little concerned.
（2）病状びょう じょうがここまで進すすんだとなっては、もうどうすることもできない。／As the illness has progressed to this point, there’s nothing that can be done.
（3）誰だれも引ひき受うけてくれないとなっては、自分じ ぶんでやるしかない。／As there’s no one willing to take on the task, it’s up to me to do it.
Following a clause, this expresses the meaning “in the case that such a situation arises.” Often used for conditions that have already been realized, and the latter half expresses the evaluation or judgment of the speaker regarding what s/he expects as the natural outcome of situation. In many cases, negative evaluations such as 心配だ (worrisome) or 仕方がない (can’t be helped) are expressed.
