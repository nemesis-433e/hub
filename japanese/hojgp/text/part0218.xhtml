【ことだし】

［N／Na　であることだし］

［Na なことだし］

［A／V　ことだし］

（1）雨あめがふってきそうだから、きょうは散歩さん ぽはやめておこうか。こどもたちもかぜをひいていることだし。／Since it looks like it’s going to rain, why don’t we put off going for a walk today? Besides, the children have colds.
（2）おいしそうな料理りょう りもでてきたことですし、私わたしのへたなごあいさつはこのへんで終おわりにしたいと存ぞんじます。／With all this delicious food ready to eat, I will put an end here to my clumsy remarks.
（3）委員い いんも大体だい たいそろったことだし、予定よ てい時間じ かんも過すぎているので、そろそろ委員会い いん かいを始はじめてはいかがですか。／Since most of the committee members are present, and we are already a little behind schedule, how would it be if we started the meeting?
Attaches to a clause. This construction combines ことだ+し and is used when describing the conditions or circumstances that form the reason or basis for some kind of judgment, decision, or hope. ことですし, as in (2), is a more polite form. Sometimes two reasons are stated, as in (3), sometimes one reason is stated, as in (2), and sometimes the reason is tacked on at the end as additional information, as in (1). Although this is a spoken language expression, it is more formal than using only し.
