【なくては】

［N／Na　でなくては］

［A-くなくては］

［V-なくては］

（1）我慢強が まん づよい人ひとでなくては彼女かの じょと付つき合あうのは難むずかしい。／Unless you have the patience of a saint, it’s hard to get along with her.
（2）どんなにお金かねがあっても健康けん こうでなくては幸しあわせだとは言いえない。／No matter how much money you have, if you aren’t in good health, you can’t say you’re happy.
（3）成績せい せきがもっとよくなくてはこの大学だい がくへの合格ごう かくは無理む りだろう。／Without better grades, you probably won’t be able to pass the entrance exam for this university.
（4）彼かれがいなくては生いきていけない。／I can’t live without him.
（5）聞きいてみなくては分わからない。／Unless you ask, you’ll never know.
（6）もっと食たべなくては大おおきくなれないよ。／You won’t grow up to be big and strong unless you eat more.
Used with the negative form of a verb or a negative expression such as 無理だ (impossible) or 難しい (difficult) at the end of the sentence. Has the meaning “unless X, Y is impossible.” Used when the speaker wants to say that realization of the situation or event expressed in the first clause is desirable or necessary. In many cases, なくては can be replaced by なかったら, なければ and/or ないと. In casual spoken language, N／Naじゃなくちゃ and A-くなくちゃ／V-なくちゃ are used instead.
