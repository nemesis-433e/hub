【てくる】

1. V-てくる〈manner at time of movement〉

（1）ここまで走はしってきた。／I ran all the way here.
（2）歩あるいて来きたので汗あせをかいた。／I walked here, so I sweated.
（3）バスは時間じ かんがかかるから、タクシーに乗のって来きて下ください。／The bus will take some time, so please come by taxi.
Expresses a method of traveling and an action undertaken while traveling.

2. V-てくる〈movement toward something or someone〉

（1）先月せん げつ日本に ほんに帰かえってきました。／I came back to Japan last month.
（2）頂上ちょう じょうから戻もどってくるのに1時間じ かんかかった。／It took an hour to come down from the summit.
（3）船ふねはゆっくりとこちらに向むかって来きます。／The ship is slowly approaching.
（4）その物体ぶっ たいはどんどん近ちかづいて来きた。／The object steadily drew near.
Expresses a situation in which a person or thing far away is approaching the speaker’s location.

3. V-てくる〈successive events〉

（1）ちょっと切符きっ ぷを買かってきます。ここで待まっていて下ください。／I’ll go and buy the ticket. Please wait here.
（2）A:小川お がわさんいらっしゃいますか。／Is Ogawa-san here?
B:隣となりの部屋へ やです。すぐ呼よんできますから、中なかに入はいってお待まち下ください。／He’s in the next room. I’ll call him right away, so please wait inside.
（3）A:どこに行いくの？／Where are you going?
B:ちょっと友達とも だちのうちに遊あそびに行いってくる。／I’m going to visit a friend for a little while.
（4）おそくなってごめんなさい。途中と ちゅうで本屋ほん やに寄よってきたものだから。／Sorry to be late. I popped into a bookshop on my way.
（5）A:かさはどうしたの？／What happened to your umbrella?
B:あ、電車でん しゃの中なかに忘わすれて来きちゃった。／Oh, dear. I’ve left it on the train.
Expresses doing an action before arriving somewhere. (1) to (3) mean “depart from the place where one is, conduct an action, and then return to the original location.” (4) and (5) mean “I’ve come to where I am, having conducted an action in another place.” Both can be expressed without using てくる, but てくる is used more often than not. This expression is used for linking what happened in another place to where the conversation is taking place.

4. V-てくる〈duration〉

（1）この伝統でん とうは5百年ひゃく ねんも続つづいてきたのだ。／This tradition has continued for five hundred years.
（2）17 歳さいのときからずっとこの店みせで働はたらいてきました。／I have worked in this shop straight through since I was seventeen.
（3）今いままで一生懸命いっ しょう けん めい頑張がん ばってきたんだから、絶対ぜっ たいに大丈夫だい じょう ぶだ。／You’ve been giving it your very best until now, so I’m sure you’ll be all right.
（4）これまで先祖伝来せん ぞ でん らいの土地と ちを守まもり続つづけてきたが、事業じ ぎょうに失敗しっ ぱいして手放て ばなさなければならなくなった。／I had watched over the land inherited from my ancestors until now, but I was forced to let it go because of a business failure.
Expresses that a change or an action has been continuing from the past to the present.

5. V-てくる〈emergence〉

（1）少すこしずつ霧きりが晴はれて、山やまが見みえてきた。／The fog is slowly clearing, and I can see the mountain now.
（2）雲くもの間あいだから月つきが出でてきた。／The moon appeared between the clouds.
（3）赤あかちゃんの歯はが生はえてきた。／The baby’s teeth have started to come in.
（4）春はるになって木々き ぎが芽吹め ぶいてきた。／Spring has come and the leaves on the trees are coming out.
Expresses that something that didn’t exist or couldn’t be seen before has emerged. Conversely, ...ていく is used when the thing in question is disappearing.

6. V-てくる〈inception〉

（1）雨あめが降ふってきた。／It’s starting to rain.
（2）最近さい きん少すこし太ふとってきた。／I’ve put on a little weight recently.
（3）ずいぶん寒さむくなってきましたね。／It’s really gotten cold, hasn’t it?
（4）このあいだ買かってあげたばかりのくつが、もうきつくなってきた。／Those shoes I bought you just the other day are already starting to pinch.
（5）問題もん だいがむずかしくて、頭あたまが混乱こん らんしてきた。／The question is so hard that I’m getting confused.
Expresses that a change is taking place.

7. V-てくる〈action directed toward speaker〉

（1）友達とも だちが結婚式けっ こん しきの日取ひ どりを知しらせてきた。／My friend let me know the date of the wedding.
（2）化粧品け しょう ひんを買かった客きゃくが苦情く じょうを言いってきた。／A customer who bought some cosmetics has made a complaint.
（3）急きゅうに犬いぬがとびかかってきた。／A dog suddenly jumped on me.
（4）歩あるいていたら、知しらない人ひとが話はなしかけてきました。／I was walking when a stranger spoke to me.
（5）息子むすこは勝手かっ てにスーツを買かって、請求書せい きゅう しょを送おくりつけてきた。／My son bought a suit without asking, and then he sent me the bill.
This expresses that an action is conducted for the speaker or someone the speaker is focusing on. ...が is used for the person conducting the action, and ...に is used for the person on the receiving end of the action.
（例）山田さんが父に電話をかけてきた。／Yamada-san called my father.
When the agent of the action is an organization, such as a company, rather than an individual, ...から is sometimes used.
（例）会社から調査を依頼してきた。／The company has requested an investigation.
Some of these sentences may be rephrased in a passive sense, where the action happens to the subject, as in the following examples.
（例）山田さんから父に電話がかかってきた。／Father was called by Yamada-san.
（例）国の家族から衣類や食料が送られてきた。／I was sent things like clothes and food by my family back home.
In this case, ...から is used for the person conducting an action and ...に for the person on the receiving end of the action.
