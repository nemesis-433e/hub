【られる1】

This is the passive voice. In cases where the V in V-られる is a Group 1 verb, the dictionary form ending changes to the a-row and れる is added, as in 行く(go)→行かれる and 飲む(drink)→飲まれる. Where Group 2 verbs are used られる is added to the stem, as in 食べる(eat)→食べられる. 来る(come) becomes こられる and する(do) becomes される. V-られる conjugates in the same way as Group 2 verbs.

1. NがV-られる〈direct passive〉

（1）この地方ち ほうではおもに赤あかワインが作つくられている。／In this region red wine is primarily made.
（2）木曜日もく よう びの会議かい ぎは3時じから開ひらかれることになっている。／Thursday’s meeting will be started at three o’clock.
（3）この辞書じ しょは昔むかしから使つかわれているいい辞書じ しょだが、最近さい きんの外来語がい らい ごなどはのっていない。／This is a good dictionary that has been used for a long time but it doesn’t contain recent loan words.
（4）昨夜さく や、駅前えき まえのデパートで1億円おく えん相当そう とうのネックレスや指輪ゆび わが盗ぬすまれた。／Last night, necklaces and rings worth 100 million yen were stolen from the department store in front of the station.
（5）来月らい げつ発売はつ ばいされる車くるまのカタログを手てに入いれた。／I got hold of a catalogue of cars to go on sale from next month.
This is used to give as the subject a thing which is acted upon or which is affected. Often used in factual descriptive sentences and in informative sentences. As the agent cannot be identified, it is usual for it not to appear in the sentence.

2. NがNにV-られる

a. NがNに（よって）V-られる〈direct passive〉

（1）漫画まん が週刊誌しゅう かん しは若わかいサラリーマンによく読よまれている。／Weekly manga magazines are often read by young businessmen.
（2）その寺院じ いんは7世紀せい きに中国ちゅう ごくから渡来と らいした僧侶そう りょによって建たてられた。／That temple was built by a priest who came over from China in the seventh century.
（3）このあたりの土地と ちはダイオキシンに汚染お せんされている。／The soil in this area is contaminated with dioxin.
（4）地震後じ しん ご、その教会きょう かいは地域ち いきの住民じゅう みんによって再建さい けんされた。／After the earthquake, that church was rebuilt by the local residents.
（5）その展覧会てん らん かいはフォード財団ざい だんによって支援し えんされている。／That exhibition is supported by the Ford Foundation.
Used to give as the subject a thing which is acted upon or which is affected. It is often used in factual descriptive sentences and in informative sentences. The agent is indicated by Nに or Nによって. In cases regarding the production of a thing (a work or building), and in formal speech, によって is primarily used.

b. NがN　に／から　V-られる〈direct passive〉

（1）おばあさんが犬いぬにかまれた。／An elderly lady was bitten by a dog.
（2）その子こは母親はは おやにしかられて、泣なき出だした。／That child was scolded by his mother and burst out crying.
（3）彼女かの じょは皆みなにかわいがられて育そだった。／She was raised being doted on by everyone.
（4）森もりさんは知しらない人ひとから話はなしかけられた。／Mori-san was spoken to by a stranger.
（5）彼かれは正直しょう じきなので、だれからも信頼しん らいされている。／Because he is honest, he is trusted by everyone.
（6）夜中よ なかに騒さわいだら、近所きん じょの人ひとに注意ちゅう いされてしまった。／When I made a lot of noise late at night, a neighbour admonished me.
Used to give as the subject a person who is acted upon or who is affected. A way of expressing a thing which occurs between two people from the viewpoint of someone other than the agent. The agent is indicated by Nに but when an action showing that a feeling, information or remark is given by the agent Nから is also used. When the speaker is affected by the action, the passive voice is often used to express the speaker’s viewpoint. Furthermore, it is usual for the sentence to convey the point of view of one person.
（×）夜中に騒いだら、近所の人が注意した。／I was noisy in the middle of the night and someone from the neighborhood admonished me.
（○）夜中に騒いだら、近所の人に注意された。／I was noisy in the middle of the night and was admonished by someone from the neighborhood.

c. NがNにV-られる〈indirect passive〉

（1）忙いそがしいときに客きゃくに来こられて、仕事し ごとができなかった。／I had a visitor while I was busy and so I couldn’t do my work.
（2）A:日曜日にち よう びはいかがでしたか。／How was Sunday?
B:家族か ぞくでハイキングに行いったんですが、途中と ちゅうで雨あめに降ふられましてね。／I went hiking with my family but we were rained on while along the way.
A:それは大変たい へんでしたね。／Oh, that was too bad.
（3）彼かれは奥おくさんに逃にげられて、すっかり元気げん きをなくしてしまった。／He was deserted by his wife and completely lost his will to live.
（4）子こどもに死しなれた親おやほどかわいそうなものはない。／There is nothing as sad as a parent who has a child die.
Used to relate the standpoint of a person adversely affected by something which has caused their situation. Corresponds with the respective active sentence which uses an intransitive verb such as 客が来る(visitors are coming) and 雨が降る(it rains). The agent is indicated by Nに but Nによって and Nから cannot be used.

3. NがNにNをV-られる

a. NがNにNをV-られる〈possessor passive〉

（1）森もりさんは知しらない人ひとに名前な まえをよばれた。／Mori-san’s name was called by a stranger.
（2）わたしは今朝け さ、電車でん しゃの中なかで足あしをふまれた。／This morning my foot was trodden upon on the train.
（3）犯人はん にんは警官けい かんに頭あたまを撃うたれて重傷じゅう しょうを負おった。／The offender was injured after being struck over the head by a police officer.
（4）先生せん せいに発音はつ おんをほめられて英語えい ごが好すきになった。／I came to like English after my pronunciation was praised by my teacher.
Used to give as the subject the owner of the thing which is acted upon or affected. Used to show that, through an action which is done to a thing, the owner of that thing is troubled or perplexed. The thing possessed by the owner (such as their name, foot, or head) is indicated in the form Nを. If this possession is taken as the subject, the effect is often unnatural.
（×）私の足がふまれた。／My foot was trodden on.
（○）私は足をふまれた。／I had my foot trodden upon.
When the action is welcome from the point of view of the speaker, expressions which demonstrate gratitude such as V-てもらった are often used but, as in the ほめる of example (4), when a verb which essentially has a positive meaning is used in the passive voice, the development of feelings such as embarrassment or pride is implied.

b. NがNにNをV-られる〈indirect passive〉

（1）せまい部屋へ やでタバコを吸すわれると気き分ぶんが悪わるくなる。／When someone smokes in a small room it makes me feel sick.
（2）夜遅よる おそくまで会社かい しゃに残のこって仕事し ごとをされると、電気代でん き だいがかかって困こまる。／If someone stays late working at the company, I worry that it costs electricity.
（3）次々つぎ つぎに料理りょう りを出だされて、とても食たべきれなかった。／One dish after another was brought out and I just couldn’t eat it all.
（4）台所だい どころのテーブルの上うえに宿題しゅく だいを広ひろげられると晩御飯ばん ご はんのしたくができないから、はやくどけなさい。／I want to prepare dinner but can’t if your homework is spread all over the table, so hurry up and move it.
（5）結婚けっ こんはおめでたいけど、今いまあなたに会社かい しゃをやめられるのは痛手いた でだなあ。／Congratulations on your marriage, but it’s a blow to us that you’re leaving the company.
Used to express the standpoint of a person indirectly bothered by a thing which has happened to cause their situation. It corresponds with the respective active sentence which uses an transitive verb such as (だれかが)タバコを吸う and (だれかが)仕事をする and demonstrates that the speaker is troubled because of that. The agent is indicated by Nに but Nによって and Nから cannot be used. Often the agent is not indicated.
