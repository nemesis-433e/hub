【ゆえ】

An old-fashioned, written expression.

1. ゆえ

（1）ゆえあって故郷こ きょうを捨すて、この極寒ごっ かんの地ちに参まいりました。／Circumstances led me to abandon my hometown and come to this frozen place.
（2）彼かれはゆえなく職務しょく むを解とかれ、失意しつ いのうちに亡なくなった。／He was relieved of his duties without reason and died of despair.
（3）若わかい女おんなが故ゆえありげな様子よう すで門もんのそばにたたずんでいた。／The young woman was standing by the gate looking as though there was a reason why she had to be there.
Means “cause” or “reason.” Example (1) is in the style of literary narration. Example (2) means “to be dismissed from work for no reason.” Example (3) means “as if with some reason.” ゆえあって, ゆえなく, and ゆえありげ are all idiomatic expressions.

2. Nのゆえに

（1）貧困ひん こんのゆえに高等こうとう教育きょう いくを受うけられない子供こ どもたちがいる。／There are children who cannot undertake higher education due to poverty.
（2）政府せい ふの無策む さくの故ゆえに国内こく ないは内乱ないらん状態じょう たいに陥おちいった。／Due to the government’s lack of measures, the country descended into civil war.
This means “with... as the cause/with... as the reason,” “on account of.”

3. ...がゆえ

（1）女性じょ せいであるが故ゆえに差別さ べつされることがある。／Sometimes I am discriminated against because I am a woman.
（2）事ことが重大じゅう だいであるが故ゆえに、報告ほう こくの遅おくれが悔くやまれる。／As the matter is very serious, the delay in reporting it is particularly regrettable.
（3）親おやが放任ほう にんしていたが故ゆえに非行ひ こうに走はしる若者わか ものもいる。／There are young people who turn towards delinquency because their parents have let them do what they want.
（4）容易よう いに会あえぬが故ゆえに会あいたさがつのる。／The fact that it is not easy for us to meet makes us want to see each other even more.
（5）若わかさ（が）故ゆえの過あやまちもあるのだ。／They are the foibles of youth.
Taking the clause in plain form, means “that is the cause/reason for.” As in (5), can be used with a noun.

4. ...のはNゆえである

（1）息子むすこは窃盗せっ とう、万引まん びきで何度なん どつかまったことか。それでも見捨み すてないのは子供こ ども可愛かわいさゆえである。／My son has been caught stealing and shoplifting so many times. Even so, I don’t abandon him because I love him as he is my own flesh and blood.
（2）冬山ふゆ やま登山と ざんは確たしかに死しと隣となり合あわせだ。だがそれでも行いくのは冬山ふゆ やまの魅力み りょくゆえである。／Winter mountain-climbing and death certainly can go together. However, be that as it may, the reason I go is the fascination of the winter mountains.
Means “the reason (I)... is that....” It is used to elaborate the reason something is done in cases where the situation is one of difficulty.
