【だの】

［N／Na　だの　N／Na　だの］

［A／V　だの　A／V　だの］

（1）彼女かの じょは市場いち ばに出でかけると、肉にくだの野菜や さいだの持もちきれないほど買かってきた。／When she went to the market, she bought such a great quantity of meat and vegetables and whatnot that she could hardly carry it all back.
（2）同窓会どう そう かいには中村なか むらだの池田いけ だだの20 年ねんぶりのなつかしい顔かおがそろった。／Many old familiar faces from twenty years ago, such as Nakamura and Ikeda, appeared together at the reunion.
（3）チャリティーバザーには有名人ゆう めい じんの服ふくだのサイン入いりの本ほんだのいろいろなものが集あつまった。／Lots of things were collected for the charity bazaar, including a celebrity’s clothing, a signed book, and so forth.
（4）彼かれは、やれ給料きゅう りょうが安やすいだの休やすみが少すくないだのと文句もん くが多おおい。／He complains a lot about how low his salary is, how little vacation time he gets, and so on and so forth.
（5）彼かれはいつ会あっても会社かい しゃをやめて留学りゅう がくするだのなんだのと実現じつ げん不可能ふ か のうなことばかり言いっている。／Whenever I see him, all he talks about are things he will never accomplish, like quitting his job and going abroad to study.
Like やら and とか, this is a way of listing several items, but as seen in (4) and (5), it is often used to indicate a negative evaluation of something another person has said. Means “it’s annoying because he says a lot of things like that.” The expression ...だのなんだの, as in (5), is idiomatic.
