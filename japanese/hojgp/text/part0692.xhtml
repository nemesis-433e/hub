【など】

In casual speech, なんか is used instead of など.

1. ...など

a. Nなど

（1）ウェイトレスや皿洗さら あらいなどのアルバイトをして学費がく ひを貯ためた。／I did part-time jobs like waitressing and dishwashing to save money for my school expenses.
（2）A:このスーツに合あうブラウスを探さがしているんですけど...。／I’m looking for a blouse that goes with this suit, but....
B:これなどいかがでしょうか。お似合に あいだと思おもいますよ。／How about something like this? I think it’ll look good on you.
（3）デパートやスーパーなどの大おおきな店みせができたために、小ちいさな店みせは経営けい えいが苦くるしくなった。／Since large retailers like the department store and the supermarket were built, the small shops have found it hard to stay in business.
Used to present a few main examples out of a larger range of items. Implies that there are other similar items as well.

b. V-るなどする

（1）ひげをそるなどして、もうすこし身みだしなみに気きを付つけてほしい。／I wish you’d pay a little more attention to your personal appearance, including things like shaving.
（2）時ときには呼よびつけて注意ちゅう いするなどしたのですが、あまり効きき目めはなかったようです。／Sometimes I did call her in and give her advice and whatever, but it doesn't seem to have been very effective.
Used to present a few main examples out of a larger range of actions. Implies that there are other similar actions as well.

2. ...などと

（1）学校がっ こうをやめるなどと言いって、みんなを困こまらせている。／It’s a big headache for all of us because he’s saying things like he’s going to leave school.
（2）来年らい ねんになれば景気けい きが持もち直なおすから大丈夫だい じょう ぶなどとのんきなことを言いっている。／She’s making these casual comments, like everything will be all right next year since the economy is going to improve.
（3）東京とう きょうで仕事し ごとを探さがすなどと言いって、家いえを出でたきり帰かえってこない。／After he said he was going to find a job in Tokyo or whatever, he left home and hasn’t been back.
などと, followed by a verb of utterance such as 言う, is used to express the gist of something that someone has said. This pattern is used for quotation, but it implies that other similar things may also have been said.

3. ...など...ない

（1）あなたの顔かおなど見みたくない。／I don’t even want to look at you.
（2）私わたしは嘘うそなどつきませんよ。／I would never tell a lie.
（3）賛成さん せいするなどと言いっていない。／I never said I would agree.
（4）あんな男おとことなどいっしょに働はたらきたくない。／I don’t want to work with a guy like that.
（5）そんなことで驚おどろいたりなどしないさ。／I just wouldn’t be surprised by such a thing.
（6）別べつにあなたを非難ひ なんしてなどいませんよ。／I’m not blaming you or anything, you know.
（7）こんな難むずかしい問題もん だいが私わたしのようなものになど解とけるはずがありません。／I’m not at all the person to be able to answer such a difficult question.
（8）こんな結果けっ かになるなどとは考かんがえてもみませんでした。／I never thought it would come to something like this.
など attaches to a variety of constituents, including nouns, verbs, and noun + particle combinations, and is followed by a negative expression. This pattern expresses not only the negation of a certain thing, situation, or event but also the speaker’s feeling of contempt, modesty, surprise, etc. with respect to whatever is marked by など.
For instance, (7) simultaneously conveys the meaning that there is no way the speaker could ever answer such a difficult question and the sense that the speaker is humble and does not hold too high an opinion of him/herself.

4. ...など...V-るものか

（1）そんな馬鹿ば かげた話はなしなどだれが信しんじるものか。／Who would ever swallow such a ridiculous story?
（2）お前まえになど教おしえてやるものか。／Why would I ever tell someone like you?
（3）あんなやつを助たすけてなどやるものか。／He’s the last guy I’d ever help!
（4）これくらいの怪我け がでだれが死しになどするものか。／No one ever died from such a minor injury!
（5）私わたしの気持き もちがあなたなどに分わかるものですか。／How on earth would someone like you ever understand my feelings?
May be preceded by a variety of constituents, including nouns, verbs, and noun + particle combinations. Expresses a strong negation, as well as the speaker’s feeling that the person, thing, or situation marked by など is trivial and worthy only of contempt.
