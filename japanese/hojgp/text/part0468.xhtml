【てっきり...とおもう】

（1）彼女かの じょがいろいろな結婚式けっ こん しき場じょうのパンフレットを持もっているので、これはてっきり結婚けっ こんするんだと思おもってしまったんです。／She had all sorts of brochures of places for wedding receptions, so I was convinced she was going to get married.
（2）人ひとが倒たおれていたので、てっきり事故じ こだと思おもって駅員えき いんに知しらせたんです。／I saw someone lying on the floor and was sure there had been an accident, so I went to tell the station staff.
（3）窓まどガラスが割われていたので、これはてっきり泥棒どろ ぼうだと思おもったんです。／I saw the broken window and was sure there had been a robber.
（4）てっきり怒おこられるものと思おもっていたが、反対はん たいにほめられたので、驚おどろいた。／I expected him to be angry at me, so I was surprised to be praised instead.
Used to explain, after the fact, that the speaker believed something was really true, based on an assumption s/he had made on the grounds of a piece of evidence or reason. てっきり emphasizes the strength of the conviction. Often the assumption is actually mistaken. Can only be used for assumptions made in the past.
（×）てっきり帰ったと思っています。／I feel sure you had gone home.
（○）てっきり帰ったと思いました。／I felt sure you had gone home.
