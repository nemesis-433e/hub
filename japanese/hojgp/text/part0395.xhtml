【だとすれば】

（1）A:近ちかくに大おおきなホテルができるのは確実かく じつです。／It’s definite that a large hotel will be built near here.
B:だとすれば、この町まちの雇用率こ よう りつが上あがるかもしれませんね。／In that case, the employment rate in this town may go up.
（2）A:この写真しゃ しんは、夏なつに京都きょう とでとったものです。／This picture was taken in Kyoto in the summer.
B:だとすれば、彼かれは去年きょ ねんの7月がつに日本に ほんにいたことになりますね。／So that means he was in Japan in July last year.
Used at the beginning of a sentence, when the speaker is stating a supposition or judgment based on something the other person has said. Means “judging from such a fact or state of affairs.” Similar to それなら, but だとすれば is somewhat more formal and used in written language, too. Related expressions include そうだとすれば, だとすると and そうだとすると.
