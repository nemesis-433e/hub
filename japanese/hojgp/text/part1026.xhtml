【ようだ1】

［Nのようだ］

［A／V　ようだ］

Conjugating na-adjectives, this becomes ように and ような in the continuative and attributive forms.

1. ...ようだ〈simile〉

a. ...ようだ

（1）この雪ゆきはまるで綿わたのようです。／This snow is just like cotton wool.
（2）彼女かの じょの心こころは氷こおりのように冷つめたい。／Her heart is as cold as ice.
（3）男おとこは狂くるったように走はしり続つづけた。／He kept running like mad.
（4）赤あかん坊ぼうは火ひがついたように泣なき出だした。／The baby burst out crying as if she were on fire.
（5）あたりは、水みずを打うったように静しずまりかえっている。／The area suddenly became completely silent.
（6）新製品しん せい ひんは面白おも しろいようによく売うれた。／The new product sold like hot cakes.
（7）6月がつが来きたばかりなのに真夏ま なつのような暑あつさだ。／Although June has just begun, it is hot like midsummer.
（8）会場かい じょうは割われるような拍手はく しゅの渦うずにつつまれた。／The venue was engulfed by the tumultuous applause.
（9）身みを切きるような寒さむさが続つづいている。／The biting coldness continues.
Used to liken a situation, quality, form, or action to something which is essentially different. Not only used to liken a thing to something with similar properties but also to entirely distinct, fanciful, things. Often attached to nouns and verbs but, occasionally, as in example (6), also attached to i-adjectives. However, cannot be attached to na-adjectives. Also, sometimes used with accompanying adverbs which are used to express similes, such as あたかも, いかにも, さながら, まるで and ちょうど.
（例）町はすっかりさびれてしまって、まるで火が消えたようだ。／The town is in complete decline, just like a fire has gone out.
（例）家族が一堂に揃い、あたかも盆と正月がいっしょに来たようだ。／To have the family all together in one room is just like the Bon Festival and the New Year’s holiday have come at the same time. (= a very busy and happy time)
Often appears in customary, fixed expressions. Idiomatic expressions other than those which appear here are: 雲をつかむような話／竹を割ったような性格／血のにじむような努力／手が切れるような新札／飛ぶように売れる／目を皿のようにして探す (a vague story/a straightforward character/to sweat blood/a crisp new note/to sell like hot cakes/to search with wide eyes) .
In speech, みたいだ is often used. Furthermore, in writing ごとし can also be used.

b. V-る／V-た　かのようだ

（1）彼かれはなにも知しらなかったかのように振ふる舞まっていた。／He was acting as though he didn't know anything.
（2）父ちちはあらかじめ知しっていたかのように、平然へい ぜんとしていた。／My father was calm, as though he already knew.
（3）本当ほん とうは見みたこともないのに、いかにも自分じ ぶんの目めで見みてきたかのように話はなす。／You haven’t even seen (it) but you talk as though you have.
（4）極楽ごく らくにでもいるかのような幸しあわせな気分き ぶんだ。／I’m so happy I feel like I’m in paradise.
（5）犯人はん にんは事件じ けんのことを初はじめて聞きいたかのような態度たい どをとった。／The criminal acted as though he was hearing about the incident for the first time.
（6）あたり一面いち めん霧きりに包つつまれ、まるで別世界べっ せ かいにいるかのようだ。／The whole area is enveloped in mist; it is like I am in another world.
Taking the dictionary or ta-form of a verb, expresses that, although in reality (it) is not the case, one’s conduct or feeling is that it is. It is often used in cases which pick out things as examples which are inconsistent with reality or are imaginary.

2. ...ような／...ように

a. ...ように〈example〉

（1）あの人ひとのように英語えい ごがペラペラ話はなせたらいいのに。／I wish I could speak English as fluently as that person.
（2）ニューヨークのように世界中せ かい じゅうの人々ひと びとが住すむ都市と しでは、各国かっ こくの本格的ほん かく てきな料理りょう りを味あじわうことができる。／In cities like New York, where people from all around the world live, you can taste authentic food from many countries.
（3）母親はは おやが美人び じんだったように、娘むすめたちもみな美人び じんぞろいだ。／The daughters are all beauties like their mother was.
（4）私わたしが発音はつ おんするようにあとについて言いってください。／Please pronounce it as I do, after me.
（5）先生せん せいがおっしゃったようにお伝つたえしておきました。／I told it as my teacher told it to me.
Taking the form XようにY, used to give a specific person or thing as an example of conformity/consistency in quality, content or method with Y. Example (4) means “to imitate an action” and (5) can be rephrased as とおりに.
In contrast to Usage 1 inようだ〈simile〉, an expression which compares an essentially different and distinct thing to X in the phrase まるでXのように, with this usage X is given as an example of a specific thing which shares a property or content with Y. However, it is not always possible to distinguish between these two usages.

b. ...ようなN〈example〉

（1）風邪か ぜをひいたときは、みかんのようなビタミンCびたみんCを多おおく含ふくむ果物くだ ものを食たべるといい。／When you catch a cold, it’s good to eat a lot of fruit like oranges which contain a lot of vitamin C.
（2）あなたのようなご親切しん せつな方かたにはなかなか出会で あえません。／It’s not very often we meet people as kind as you.
（3）これはどこにでもあるようなものではない。／This is not the kind of thing you find everywhere.
（4）彼かれはあなたが思おもっているような人ひとではない。／He is not the kind of person you think he is.
（5）このまま放ほうっておくと、取とり返かえしがつかないようなことになりかねない。／If you leave it as it is, it’s quite possible that you won’t be able to sort it out.
（6）これを食たべても死しぬようなことはありません。安心あん しんしてください。／You won’t die if you eat this. Please rest assured.
（7）薬くすりを飲のんでもよくならないような場合ば あいは医者い しゃに相談そう だんしてください。／In cases where you do not recover after taking medicine, please consult your doctor.
Used to give a concrete example of the noun which follows it. For example, in みかんのような果物(a fruit like an orange), the latter noun represents a higher concept than the former. When continued after a clause, the sentence can be valid without ような but there will be some difference in the meaning if it is included or not. For example, if ような is removed from (7) as 薬を飲んでもよくならない場合 it refers to a specific situation where “the condition does not improve after taking the medicine,” but in case of 薬を飲んでもよくならないような場合 then it means “cases like when you take the medicine but the condition does not improve” indicating that there are many other similar situations.

c. ...ように〈introductory utterance〉

（1）ご存ぞんじのように、日本に ほんは人口密度じん こう みつ どの高たかい国くにです。／As you know, Japan is a country with a high density of population.
（2）あなたがおっしゃっていたように、彼かれは本当ほん とうに素敵す てきな方かたですね。／As you were saying, he’s a really lovely man.
（3）すでに述のべたようにアフリカの食糧しょく りょう不足ぶ そくは深刻しん こくな状況じょう きょうにある。／As has already been mentioned, Africa’s food shortage is serious.
（4）ことわざにもあるように、外国がい こくに行いったらその国くにの習慣しゅう かんに従したがって暮くらすのが一番いち ばんである。／As the maxim says, when you go abroad, it’s best to live by the customs of that country.
（5）あのにこにこした表情ひょう じょうが表あらわしているように、彼かれはとても明あかるい性格せい かくの人ひとです。／As his smiling expression shows, he has a very cheerful character.
Shows that what is about to be said is consistent with what has been stated previously or is a well-known fact. Used to give notice that further explanation is about to be given. Can be replaced with とおり.

d. つぎのように／いかのように

（1）結果けっ かは次つぎのようにまとめることができる。／The results can be summarized in the following manner.
（2）中なかには以下い かのような意見い けんもあった。／Among them there were the following kinds of opinion.
（3）本稿ほん こうの結論けつ ろんをまとめれば、次つぎのようになる。／We can summarize the article’s conclusions as follows.
（4）以下い かで示しめすように、我わが国くにの出生率しゅっ しょう りつは下さがる一方いっ ぽうである。／As seen below, the birthrate of our country is declining.
Used to announce that concrete detail is to follow. In vertical text compositions, 右のように and 左のように are also used.
