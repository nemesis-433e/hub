【がかり】

がかり is a word derived from the verb かかる. かかる may mean “take,” as in時間／お金がかかる (take time/money), “see/receive treatment,” as in 医者にかかる (see a doctor), “be affected,” as in 雨がかかる (get wet in the rain), “start,” as in仕事にかかる (get started with work), and so on.

1. 数すう量りょう詞し+がかり

（1）グランドピアノを5人にんがかりでやっと運はこんだ。／It took five people to move the grand piano.
（2）3日かがかりで作つくり上あげた巨大きょ だいな雪ゆきだるまは、翌日よく じつのポカポカ陽気よう きですぐに溶とけてしまった。／The enormous snowman it had taken us three days to build melted away quickly in the warm sunshine of the following day.
（3）5年ねんがかりの調査ちょう さの結果けっ か、その湖みずうみの生態系せい たい けいは壊こわれかかっているということがわかった。／As a result of five long years of research, we found that the ecosystem of the lake was breaking down.
（4）さすが横綱よこ づなは体からだが大おおきくて力ちからも強つよいので、高校生こう こう せい力士りき しが3人にんがかりで向むかっていってもまるで勝かち目めはなかった。／The yokozuna (grand champion) was as big and strong as you might expect, so there was no chance for a win by any of the three high school wrestlers who faced off against him.
Attaches to words like 人, 日, or 時間 and expresses an action that requires many people or a lengthy time to be accomplished. Followed by an expression of the action that was difficult and required such effort.

2. Nがかり

（1）彼女かの じょは 30 才さいにもなって、親おやがかりで留学りゅう がくした。／She studied abroad totally financed by her parents, even though she was already thirty years old.
（2）男おとこは「君きみはバラのように美うつくしいね」などと、芝居しば いがかりのせりふを吐はいた。／The man melodramatically spewed out lines like “You are as beautiful as a rose.”
Means something like “depending on/being served by (one’s parents),” as in (1), or “has the quality of (a theatrical performance),” as in (2). There are no other examples but 親がかり for the first type of usage. For the second type, 神がかり (possessed; fanatic) is also used, but there are very few other nouns to which がかり can be attached. The second type is also used in the forms Nがかっている and Nがかった; in such cases, a wider range of words is used, like 青みがかった (with blue overtones) and 左がかった (left-leaning).

3. R-がかり

（1）広場ひろ ばでトランペットの練習れん しゅうをしていると、通とおりがかりの人ひとが何人なん にんも足あしを止とめて聞きいていった。／When I was practicing my trumpet in a public square, a number of passersby stopped to listen.
（2）それは他ほかの部署ぶ しょの企画き かくだったが、担当者たん とう しゃにいくらかアドバイスもしたので、行いきがかり上じょうしかたなく私わたしも関かかわることになってしまった。／Though it was another department’s project, since I had given some advice to the person in charge, I ended up getting involved with it myself.
Example (1) means “happened to pass by.” 行きがかり上 in (2) means “because of the sequence of events or situation up to this time.” (1) and (2) are idiomatic expressions, and がかり cannot be attached to any other verbs.
