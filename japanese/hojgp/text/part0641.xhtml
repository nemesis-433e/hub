【とんだ】

［とんだN］

（1）あなたが邪魔じゃ ましたなどと、とんだ思おもい違ちがいをしていました。／Saying that you had disturbed me was a complete misunderstanding on my part.
（2）A:通勤つう きんの途中と ちゅうで、事故じ こに遭あってしまったんですよ。／I had an accident on my way to work, you know.
B:それはとんだ災難さい なんでしたね。／What a terrible thing to have happened!
（3）親おやにかくれてたばこを吸すうとは、とんだ不良ふ りょう娘むすめだ。／What a terrible daughter, to smoke behind her parents’ backs.
（4）もし1分ぷんでも気きが付つくのが遅おくれていたら、とんだ大事故だい じ こになっていたかもしれない。／If I had noticed even just one minute later, it might have turned into a disastrous accident.
（5）とんだ野郎や ろうに見込み こまれてしまったものだ。／Some guy like that is counting on me.
（6）委員会い いん かいの議長ぎ ちょうに選えらばれるとは、とんだことになってしまった。／Being elected to chair the committee was totally unexpected.
とんだ precedes a noun referring to a person or situation and means that the person or situation is contrary to one’s expectations. It usually conveys a negative evaluation, with the meaning of “awful,” “stunning; shocking,” “troublesome,” or “terrible,” and is used to describe an unexpected result or a person who doesn’t conform to commonly-accepted standards of behavior. However, in cases such as (5) above, とんだ can also convey a positive evaluation that implies warmth and familiarity, with the meaning of “(a) unique and uncommon (individual).”
