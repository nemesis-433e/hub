【だから】

The more polite equivalent is ですから.

1. だから〈consequence〉

（1）踏切ふみ きりで事故じ こがあった。だから、学校がっ こうに遅刻ち こくしてしまった。／There was an accident at the railroad crossing. So I was late for school.
（2）部屋へ やの電気でん きがついている。だから、もう帰かえって来きているはずだ。／There’s a light on in the room. So he must be back already.
（3）時間じ かんがありません。だから、急いそいでください。／Time is short. So please hurry.
（4）A:今夜こん やは雨あめになるそうですね。／I hear it will rain this evening.
B:だから、私わたし、傘かさをもって来きました。／That’s why I brought my umbrella.
Used to state a consequence derived as a result of the cause, reason, or basis described in the preceding sentence. The sentence that follows may be a factual sentence, or it may present a supposition, request, invitation, etc. (4) is an example of use in conversation, with one speaker stating the reason and the other stating the consequence.

2. だから　...のだ／...わけだ

（1）A:ジャクソンさんは、小学生しょう がく せいの時ときからもう 10 年ねんも日本語に ほん ごを習ならっているそうです。／I heard that Jackson-san has been studying Japanese for ten years already, since he was in elementary school.
B:だから、あんなに日本語に ほん ごが上手じょう ずなんですね。／So that’s why his Japanese is so good.
（2）A:今日きょうは吉田よし だ先生せん せい、休講きゅう こうだそうだよ。／I heard Yoshida-sensei’s class has been canceled today.
B:ああ、そう。だからいくら待まってもだれも来こないわけか。／Oh, really? So that’s why I’ve been waiting for so long and no one has come.
（3）やっぱり、不合格ふ ごう かくだったか。だから、もっと簡単かん たんな大学だい がくを受うけろと言いったのだ。／So, just as I expected, you didn’t pass. That’s why I told you to try for an easier university.
Used to express a current state of affairs after discovering a certain fact; conveys the feeling that the speaker accepts the state of affairs as a natural result having arisen from that fact. In conversation, this pattern is used when the cause or reason for something has become clear from something the other person has said, and it is accompanied by sentence-final particles such as ね, indicating confirmation, or か, indicating understanding. The first syllable in だから is stressed and held slightly longer.

3. だから〈question〉

（1）A:みんなお前まえのためにこんなに遅おそくまで働はたらいているんだ。／Everyone is working this late because of you.
B:だから、どうだって言いうの。／So, what are you saying?
（2）A:できることは全部ぜん ぶやったつもりです。／I’ve tried to do all that I can.
B:だから、何なんなんですか。／So what?
（3）A:たった一度いち ど会あっただけだよ。／I’ve only met her once.
B:だから？／So?
Used in conversation, with だから followed by a question. Does not express cause and effect, but is used in response to a statement made by the listener, in order to demand clarification of the intention behind that statement. Means “so what is it that you’re trying to say?” Can be replaced by それで or で. In some cases, as in (3), it is pronounced with a rising tone and the question part is omitted. Because this usage of だから has an impolite nuance, even if the sentence ends in a polite form, it is usually not possible to say ですから.

4. だから〈assertion〉

（1）A:ちょっと、どういうことですか。／Hey, what’s going on?
B:別べつに特別とく べつのことはないよ。／Nothing in particular.
A:だから、どういうことって聞きいているんだよ。／So I’m asking you, what’s going on?!
（2）A:何なんで、電話でん わしてくれなかったの。／Why didn’t you call me?
B:だから、時間じ かんがなかったんだ。／I told you, I didn’t have time to.
Used in conversation. Does not express cause and effect, but is used when the speaker is trying to make the listener understand his/her intention, in situations when his/her opinion differs from the listener’s. Means “what I want to say is this.” (2) is an example of using だから to make an excuse. Because it expresses a strong assertion, it sounds forceful and often has an impolite nuance.
