【かろうじて】

A formal, expression with the feel of written language. In everyday spoken language, どうにか and なんとか are often used. やっと and ようやく are similar expressions.

1. かろうじてV-た

（1）試験し けんの開始かい し時間じ かんに、かろうじて間まに合あった。／I barely arrived in time for the start of the exam.
（2）試験し けんのできは良よくなかったが、かろうじて合格ごう かくできた。／I did badly on the exam but somehow managed to pass it.
（3）雨あめでタイヤがスリップした。危あぶないところだったが、かろうじて事故じ こはまぬがれた。／My car skidded because of rain. It was really dangerous, and I just avoided having an accident.
（4）国連こく れんの介入かい にゅうで、かろうじて武力ぶ りょく衝突しょう とつは避さけられた。／Because of the intervention of the United Nations, the use of arms was narrowly averted.
（5）ひどい怪我け がだったが、かろうじて死しなずにすんだ。／It was a terrible injury, and I narrowly escaped death.
Means “barely” or “narrowly managing to do something.” Used when a good result is achieved or the worst situation is avoided. Used in the forms かろうじて...をまぬがれた, かろうじて...せずにすんだ, and かろうじて...は避けられた.
やっと has the implication that a long time or great trouble is needed to achieve a result. On the other hand, with かろうじて a process is not always necessary, and the result is the important point. It is more formal than やっと and used in written language.

2. かろうじてV-ている

（1）毎日まい にちの生活せい かつは苦くるしいが、かろうじて借金しゃっ きんはせずに済すんでいる。／Everyday life is hard, and I just manage to get by without borrowing money.
（2）病人びょう にんは機械き かいの力ちからを借かりて、かろうじて生いきている。／The patient is on life support, barely alive.
（3）現代人げん だい じんは、毎日まい にちのストレスに耐たえて、かろうじてバランスを保たもっているに過すぎない。／People today are only just managing to endure the stress of everyday life and make ends meet.
（4）彼女かの じょも、かろうじて涙なみだをこらえているようだった。／She also seemed to be fighting back tears.
Expresses “barely managing” or “accomplishing something with difficulty.” Used when conditions are bad, but the worst outcome is avoided and the present condition can be maintained with difficulty, as in (1)-(3). Also used when the present condition is maintained by great effort or trouble, as in (4). The meaning of (4) is that she is almost crying but refrains from it with effort.

3. かろうじてV-るN

（1）この道みちは、車くるま二台に だいがかろうじてすれ違ちがえる広ひろさしかない。／This road is just wide enough to let two cars pass each other.
（2）列車れっ しゃの寝台しん だいというのは、人ひとひとりが、かろうじて横よこになれる大おおきさだ。／The berth in the train is barely big enough for a person to lie down.
（3）その家いえは、僕ぼくにもかろうじて買かえそうな値段ね だんだ。／The price of that house is at the limit of what I can afford.
（4）私わたしの英語えい ごは、かろうじて日常にち じょう会話かい わができる程度てい どだ。／My English ability is barely enough for daily conversation.
With expressions of potentiality, as in (1) to (4), かろうじて means “someone somehow manages to achieve something.” Used when something is difficult to do, but someone just barely can do it, with no margin to spare.
