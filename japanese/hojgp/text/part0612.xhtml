【との】

1. ...とのことだ

（1）みなさんによろしくとのことでした。／He said to give his regards to everyone.
（2）無事ぶ じ大学だい がくに合格ごう かくなされたとのこと、まことにおめでとうございます。／I heard that you passed your university entrance exams—sincere congratulations on your success!
（3）社長しゃ ちょうはすこし遅おくれるので、会議かい ぎを始はじめておいてくれとのことでした。／The company president will be a little late, so she said we should start the meeting without her.
（4）そちらは、寒さむい日ひが続つづいているとのことですが、皆様みな さまお変かわりありませんか。／I hear that you have been having a cold spell—how is everyone doing?
（5）あの二人ふたりも、長ながかった婚約こん やくに終止符しゅう し ふを打うち、6月がつに挙式きょ しきするとのこと。／I hear that those two are bringing their long engagement to a close and getting married in June.
Means “I hear/they say that....” Used by the speaker to say something that s/he has heard or learned from another person. As in (2) and (5), sometimes だ is dropped and the clause or sentence ends with こと. May take the ta-form, as in とのことだった／でした (I heard/they said that...). Cannot be used in the negative form.

2. ...とのN

（1）恩師おん しから結婚式けっ こん しきには出席しゅっ せきできないとの返事へん じを受うけ取とった。／I received a reply from my mentor that she won’t be able to attend the wedding.
（2）学生がく せいから留学りゅう がくするため一年いち ねん休学きゅう がくさせてほしいとの希望き ぼうが出だされている。／The student has submitted a request for a year's leave of absence in order to study abroad.
（3）この件けんについては、次回じ かいの審議しん ぎに回まわしてはどうかとの議長ぎ ちょうの提案てい あんに全員ぜん いん賛成さん せいした。／Everyone agreed to the proposal made by the chair for deliberation on this matter to be tabled until the next meeting.
（4）来月らい げつから一年間いち ねん かん、札幌さっ ぽろの支社し しゃに出向しゅっ こうせよとの辞令じ れいを受うけた。／I’ve received an official notice that I’ll be reassigned to our branch office in Sapporo for a year, starting next month.
（5）文部もん ぶ大臣だい じんは、学校がっ こう教育きょう いくを改善かい ぜんするためには、高等こうとう教育きょう いく機関き かんの入学にゅう がく試験し けん制度せい どの抜本的ばっぽんてき改革かいかくが必要ひつ ようだとの見解けん かいを述のべた。／The Minister of Education has stated his view that, in order to improve formal education, a drastic overhaul of the entrance examination system for institutions of higher education is necessary.
In the pattern ...とのN, a clause that describes the content of a certain linguistic expression or thought is used to modify a noun. Used in formal style. The N slot is filled by a noun that denotes either a speech activity, such as 手紙 (letter), 返事 (reply), 依頼 (request), 提案 (proposal), 警告 (warning), or 命令 (order), or a thought activity, such as 意見 (opinion), 見解 (viewpoint), 考え (idea), or 希望 (hope). Used when talking about something that another person has said or thought. When the speaker’s own thoughts are being expressed, usually the form という is used instead of との, as below.
（例）私は夫婦別姓を合法化すべきだという意見をもっている。／I am of the opinion that the use of different surnames by married couples should be legalized.
