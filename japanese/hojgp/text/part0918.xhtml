【べき】

［N／Na　であるべき］

［A-くあるべき］

［V-るべき］

This is a conjugation of the literary auxiliary verb べし. It attaches to the dictionary form of verbs in modern usage. する becomes either するべき or すべき in form.

1. ...べきだ

（1）学生がく せいは勉強べん きょうす（る）べきだ。／Students ought to study.
（2）他人た にんの私生活し せい かつに干渉かん しょうす（る）べきではない。／You should not interfere with someone else’s private life.
（3）近頃ちか ごろは小学生しょう がく せいまで塾じゅくに通かよっているそうだが、子供こ どもはもっと自由じ ゆうに遊あそばせるべきだ。／I hear nowadays even primary school children attend cram schools, but I think children should have greater freedom just to play.
（4）女性じょ せいは常つねに化粧け しょうをして美うつくしくあるべきだなどという考かんがえには賛成さん せいできない。／I cannot agree with the idea that women should always be made up and look pretty.
（5）地球的ち きゅう てき規模き ぼで自然破壊し ぜん は かいが進すすんでいる。人間にん げんは自然し ぜんに対たいしてもっと謙虚けん きょであるべきだ。／Ecological destruction is progressing on a global scale. People ought to be more thoughtful about nature.
（6）教師きょう し: 君きみ、成績せい せきが良よくないね。もっと勉強べん きょうするべきだね。／Teacher: Your academic record is not good. You ought to study harder.
学生がく せい: すみません。／Student: Sorry.
（7）A:海外かい がい研修けんしゅうに行いくかどうか迷まよっているんだ。／I am debating whether I should go on an overseas study tour.
B:そりゃ、行いくべきだよ。いいチャンスじゃないか。／You should go! It is a good opportunity, isn’t it?
（8）この仕事し ごとはきみがやるべきだ。／You should do this job.
（9）会社かい しゃの電話でん わで私用し ようの電話でん わをするべきじゃないね。／You shouldn’t be making personal calls on the company telephone, you know.
Means that “it should be done,” “it is proper to do ...,” or “... must be done.” The negative form is べきではない meaning “it is not good to do ...” or “doing ... is not appropriate” or “must not do ....”
In (1)-(5), the speaker is expressing his view on general matters. If used in relation to the actions of another, it becomes advice for or against doing something, prohibition, or an order. This expression is used in both spoken and written language.

2. ...べき　だった／ではなかった

［V-る／V-ておく　べきだった］

（1）あの時とき買かっておくべきだった。／I should have bought it then.
（2）あんなひどいことを言いうべきではなかった。／I shouldn’t have said such a nasty thing!
（3）君きみはやっぱりあのときに留学りゅう がくしておくべきだったんだよ。／In hindsight, you should have gone abroad to study then.
Indicates one’s regrets over an action one took or didn’t take and means “I wish I had done ...” or “I wish I hadn’t done ....”
(1) means that the speaker wished he had bought it then but he did not. (2) means that the speaker wished he had not said something unkind but in reality, he did. In (3), the speaker is saying to his friend that he should have gone abroad to study. When the speaker talks about himself, it shows his regrets or remorse. This expression is often used in both everyday written and spoken language.

3. ...べきN

（1）外交がいこう政策せいさくについて、議論ぎ ろんすべきことは多おおい。／With regard to the diplomatic policies, there are many issues that need to be discussed.
（2）エジプトのピラミッドは、永遠えい えんに残のこすべき人類じん るいの遺産い さんである。／The pyramids of Egypt are a world legacy that should be protected for future generations forever.
（3）エイズは恐おそるべき速はやさで世界中せ かい じゅうに広ひろがっている。／AIDS is spreading around the world at a fearful speed.
（4）人ひとは皆みな死しすべき運命うん めいを背負せ おっている。／Everyone is fated to die.
Means “something that has to be done” or “the inevitable consequence.”
(1) indicates what has to be discussed as an important issue. (2) means that a world heritage should be maintained as a matter of course and should be passed on to the future generations. (3) and (4) are idiomatic phrases each meaning “incredible speed” and “unavoidable eventual death” respectively.
A formal expression that is often used in written language.
