【それにしても】

（1）A:予選よ せんではあんなに強つよかったのにどうして決勝けっ しょうで負まけたんでしょうね。／He was so good in the preliminaries—I wonder why he lost in the final.
B:プレッシャーでしょう。／Probably due to pressure.
A:それにしてもひどい負まけ方かたですね。／Even so, he really lost badly.
（2）A:坂本さか もとさん、あの高校こう こうに受うかったんだってね。／I hear Sakamoto-san passed the exam to get into that high school.
B:必死ひっ しで勉強べん きょうしてたらしいよ。／Apparently she studied really hard for it.
A:それにしてもすごいね。／Even so, that’s really something!
（3）A:またガソリン代だい、値上ね あがりしたよ。／The cost of gasoline has gone up again.
B:それにしても政治家せい じ かはなにをしてるんだろう。われわれがこんなに苦くるしんでいるのに。／And what are the politicians doing about it? While we’re struggling so hard.
（4）A:太郎た ろう、また背せがのびたようよ。／Taro seems to have grown more.
B:それにしても、あいつはよく寝ねるなあ。／Even so (even though children who sleep well grow well), he really does sleep a lot, doesn’t he.
（5）《A、Bが竹下たけ したさんを待まっている》／《A and B are waiting for Takeshita-san》
A:よく降ふりますね。／It’s really raining hard, isn’t it?
B:ええ、それにしても竹下たけ したさん遅おそいですね。／Yes, but even so, Takeshita-san is quite late, isn’t he?
Means “even taking that (fact) into account.” In (1)-(3), it is used to say something that contrasts with a previous statement, while acknowledging that the previous statement is by and large true. In (4) and (5), it is used to introduce a new topic, after expressing provisional agreement with what has been said up to that point. In the latter case, it often expresses the feelings of the speaker at that time.
