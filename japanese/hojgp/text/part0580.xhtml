【ところだ】

1. Vところだ〈phase of a situation〉

ところだ attaches to verbs and is used to report the current status or progress of a situation or event. ところだった expresses that something was at a certain stage in the past. The expression ところだ itself does not usually appear in negative or interrogative forms. The preceding verb does not usually take the negative form, either.

a. V-たところだ

（1）今いま帰かえってきたところです。／I just got home.
（2）海外かい がい勤務きん むを終おえ、先月せん げつ帰国き こくしたところです。／I finished my overseas assignment and returned home just last month.
（3）電話でん わしたら、あいにくちょっと前まえに出でかけたところだった。／When I called, unfortunately she had just stepped out a little earlier.
Expresses the stage immediately after an action or change has taken place. Often used with adverbs that refer to the time immediately preceding, such as 今 (now), さっき (just a moment ago), or ちょっと前 (a little while ago).

b. V-ているところだ

（1）A:もしもし、和雄かず お君くんいますか。／Hello, is Kazuo-kun there?
B:今いまお風呂ふ ろに入はいっているところなんです。／Right now he is taking a bath.
（2）だだ今いま電話でん わ番号ばんごうを調しらべているところですので、もう少々しょう しょうお待まちください。／I am looking up that phone number right now, so please wait a few moments.
（3）ふすまを開あけると、妻つまは着物き ものを片付かた づけているところだった。／When I slid the doors open, my wife was in the middle of putting her kimono away.
Expresses that an action is currently in progress.

c. V-ていたところだ

（1）いい時ときに電話でん わをくれました。私わたしもちょうどあなたに電話でん わしようと思おもっていたところなんです。／You’ve called at a good time. I was just thinking about giving you a call, too.
（2）思おもいがけなくも留学りゅう がくのチャンスが舞まい込こんできた。そのころ私わたしは、将来しょう らいの進路しん ろが決きめられずいろいろ思おもい悩なやんでいたところだった。／I was unexpectedly blessed with the chance to study overseas. At that time in my life, I couldn’t decide on a future direction and was worrying about all sorts of things.
Expresses that a certain condition has continued from a previous time to the point in time indicated in the sentence. Often used when explaining one’s thinking or psychological condition, to express that it has changed or a new development has come about.

d. V-るところだ

（1）これから家いえを出でるところですから、30 分ぷんほどしたら着つくと思おもいます。／I am just about to leave home, so I should be there in about thirty minutes.
（2）飛行機ひ こう きは今いま飛とび立たつところだ。／The plane is just about to take off.
（3）A:ご飯はんもう食たべた？／Have you already had (breakfast/lunch/supper)?
B:ううん、これから食たべるところ。／No, I’m just about to eat.
（4）家いえに戻もどって来くると、妻つまは買かい物ものに出掛で かけるところだった。／When I returned home, my wife was just leaving to go out shopping.
Expresses the stage immediately before an action or change takes place. Sometimes used with adverbs like ちょうど (just now), 今 (now), and これから (after this/now).

2. Vところだ〈counter-factual〉

Expresses a situation counter to reality. Means “if things were different, then such-and-such would be the case.” Often preceded by a conditional clause marked with たら, なら, or ば.

a. ...たらVところだ

（1）昔むかしだったらそんな過激か げきな発言はつ げんをする人間にん げんは、処刑しょ けいされているところだ。／If this were still the old days, people who make such radical statements would be executed.
（2）父ちちがそのことを知しったら激怒げき どするところだ。／If my father found out about that, he would be furious.
（3）先生せん せいがお元気げん きだったら、今日きょうのような日ひには一緒いっ しょに中華料理ちゅう か りょう りでも食たべているところでしょう。／If my teacher were still in good health, on a day like today we would be together eating Chinese food or something.
（4）知しらせていただかなければ、とっくにあきらめていたところです。／If you hadn’t let me know, I would already have given up.
Placed after the dictionary form or ta-form of a verb and preceded by a conditional clause marked by ...ば, ...たら, etc. Expresses that, although the reality is different, the speaker can imagine a situation that would arise if a certain condition were realized.
For example, (1) means “If this were the old days, we would expect them to be executed,” but this does not happen in present-day society.

b. ...ところだった

［V-るところだった］

［V-ていたところだった］

（1）もし気きがつくのが遅おそかったら、大惨事だい さん じになるところだった。／If we had noticed too late, it could have been a real tragedy.
（2）あっ、あなたに大事だい じな話はなしがあるのを思おもい出だしました。うっかり忘わすれるところでした。／Oh, I just remembered that I have something important to discuss with you. I had almost forgotten.
（3）ありがとうございます。注意ちゅう いしていただかなければ忘わすれていたところでした。／Thank you so much. If you hadn’t warned me, I would have forgotten.
Means that under different circumstances a certain event would have occurred, but it was narrowly avoided, just in time. Often used with conditional clauses marked with ...ば or ...たら. To emphasize that the event was right on the verge of occurring, the expression もうすこしで...ところだった (any longer and...would have...) may be used.

c. ...なら（ば）...ところ　だが／を

（1）普通ふ つうならただではすまないところだが、今回こん かいだけは見逃み のがしてやろう。／Ordinarily I wouldn't let this pass, but I'll overlook it just this once.
（2）本来ほん らいならば直接ちょく せつお伺うかがいすべきところですが、書面しょ めんにて失礼しつ れい致いたします。／I know that under normal circumstances I should come to see you in person, but I hope you don’t mind my sending you a letter instead.
（3）通常つう じょうは定価てい かどおりのところを、お得意とく いさんに限かぎり特別とく べつに1万円まん えん引びきになっております。／Although normally we charge the list price, we are offering a special 10,000 yen discount to our best customers only.
（4）いつもなら1時間じ かんで行いけるところを、今日きょうは交通こう つう事故じ こがあって3時間じ かんもかかった。／We can usually get there in an hour, but today there was a car accident, so it took us three hours.
Used with expressions such as 普通 (usually), 通常 (normally), and 本来ならば (under ordinary circumstances). Expresses that the current state of affairs is special and differs from the usual one.
