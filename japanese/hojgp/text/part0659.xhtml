【ないでもない】

Used with the negative form of a verb or the negative adjective ない, ないでもない means that it isn’t possible to say that something never happens; on the contrary, there are times that it does occur or exist. ないこともない and なくもない are also used.

1. V-ないでもない

（1）A:納豆なっ とうはお好すきですか。／Do you like nattoo (fermented soybeans)?
B:食たべないでもないですが、あまり好すきじゃありません。／It’s not that I avoid eating it, but I don’t like it very much.
（2）A:ねえ、行いきましょうよ。／Come on, let’s go.
B:そんなに言いうなら行いかないでもないけど。／If you’re going to insist, I guess I might go.
（3）自分じ ぶんにも悪わるい点てんがあったことは認みとめないでもない。／I admit that I was wrong too, in some respects.
（4）考かんがえてみれば、彼かれの意見い けんももっともだという気きがしないでもない。／When I think about it, I can’t say that his opinion was completely without reason.
ないでもない is used with the negative form of a verb and means that a certain action or perception is to some extent possible. When used with verbs of thought or perception, such as 言う (say), 考える (think/consider), 思う (think), 認める (acknowledge), 感じる (feel), and 気がする (have the sense that), it means “to somehow have a feeling (that).”

2. Nがないでもない

（1）時ときには一人ひとりになりたいと思おもうことがないでもない。／I sometimes want to be alone.
（2）娘むすめは、見合み あいで結婚けっ こんするつもりがないでもないらしい。／It seems that my daughter is not completely against the idea of an arranged marriage.
（3）海外旅行かい がい りょ こうをしたい気きもないではないが、なかなかその時間じ かんがとれない。／It’s not that I don’t want to go abroad, but I just can’t seem to find the time for it.
Used mainly with nouns that refer to intentions or feelings. Means it would not be true to say that a person has no such intentions/feelings at all. When preceded by N +も, this expression can take the form Nもないではない, as in (3).
