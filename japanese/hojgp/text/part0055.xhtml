【いつか】

1. いつか

（1）本ほんを読よんでいる間あいだにいつか眠ねむり込こんでしまったようだ。／It seems like I dozed off at some point while I was reading a book.
（2）いつか雨あめはやみ、雲くもの間あいだから日ひが射さしていた。／Before I was aware of it, the rain had stopped and the sun was shining through the clouds.
（3）動物園どう ぶつ えんはいつか人影ひと かげもまばらになり、閉園へい えんのアナウンスが流ながれていた。／Before I noticed, there were hardly any people left in the zoo, and there was an announcement about closing time.
Means “before one knows/realizes it.” Often used in written language. いつのまにか is used often in spoken language, and いつしか is a literary expression.

2. いつかV-た

（1）いつか見みた映画えい がの中なかにもこんな台詞せりふがあった。／I heard a dialogue like this in a movie I saw once.
（2）彼かれとはいつかどこかであったことがあるような気きがする。／I feel like I’ve met him somewhere before.
（3）この道みちは前まえにいつか通とおったことがあったね。／I remember we took this road before.
Used in a sentence about a past event and indicates a point in the past that is indefinite or cannot be determined exactly.

3. いつか（は）

（1）あいつもいつかはきっと自分じ ぶんの間違ま ちがいに気きづくだろう。／He will surely realize his mistake one day.
（2）がんばっていれば、いつかはだれかがこの努力ど りょくを認みとめてくれるはずだ。／Someone will recognize all my effort one day, if I keep doing my best.
（3）いつか一度いち どでいいから世界中せ かい じゅうを放浪ほう ろうしてみたい。／Someday, even just once, I want to try wandering around the world.
（4）あの美術館び じゅつ かんへいつかは行いこうと思おもいながら、全然ぜん ぜん行いく暇ひまがない。／I always think about going to that art museum someday, but I have no time at all.
When used in a sentence about a future event, いつか（は） indicates a point in the future that cannot be determined exactly. At the end of the sentence, ...するor ...するはずだ, するだろう, したい, or しよう may be used. いつか is frequently used with adverbs such as きっと (surely) and かならず (certainly).

4. いつかのN

（1）いつかのセールスマンがまた来きた。／That salesman from the other day came again.
（2）彼かれはいつかの交通こう つう事故じ この後遺症こう い しょうがいまだにあって苦くるしんでいるそうだ。／I heard he is still suffering from the after-effects of the traffic accident he was in before.
（3）いつかの件けんはどうなりましたか。ほら、田村た むらさんに仕事し ごとを頼たのんでみるって言いっていたでしょ。／What happened to that thing from before? Remember, you said that you would ask Tamura-san to do the job.
（4）いつかのあの人ひとにもう一度いち ど会あいたいなあ。／Someday I really want to meet that person once again.
Indicates a point in the past that cannot be determined exactly. Implies that something happened at that point, but what happened exactly depends on the context. For example, “the salesman from the other day” has many possible meanings, such as “he came / he talked with me / he called me” the other day.
