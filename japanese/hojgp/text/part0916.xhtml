【べからざる】

［V-るべからざる］

（1）川端かわばた康成やすなりは日本に ほんの文学史ぶん がく し上じょう、欠かくべからざる作家さっ かだ。／Kawabata Yasunari is a writer who could not be omitted in any history of Japanese literature.
（2）大臣だい じんの地位ち いを利用り ようして、企業き ぎょうから多額た がくの金かねを受うけ取とるなどは、政治家せい じ かとして許ゆるすべからざる犯罪はん ざい行為こう いである。／It is an unforgivable criminal act for a politician to use his or her position as a government minister to receive large sums of money from companies.
（3）いかなる理由り ゆうがあったにせよ、警官けい かんが一般いっ ぱん市民し みんに暴行ぼう こうを加くわえるなど、あり得うべからざる異常事態い じょう じ たいだ。／Whatever the reason, it is beyond belief that a policeman should use physical violence on an ordinary citizen.
Used in the form of べからざるN. It is the classical written language version of べきでないN. It indicates that the action or the state is not right or undesirable and means ...ことができないN or ...てはいけないN (N that cannot do something or N that should not do something).
(1) means that the person cannot be omitted or must not be forgotten. (2) means that the criminal act is so terrible that it should be stopped. (3) refers to an incident that would not/should not take place.
A limited number of verbs can take this form. As in the cases of (1)-(3), it is often used in idiomatic phrases such as 欠くべからざる人物, 許すべからざる行為, あり得べからざる事態. Note that in the case of 得る in (3), べからざる is attached to 得, forming 得べからざる. It is a formal written language style. Also note that the pronunciation of 得 in 得べからざる is u rather than e.
