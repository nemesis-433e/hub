【てでも】

［V-てでも］

（1）どうしても留学りゅう がくしたい。家いえを売うってでも行いきたいと思おもった。／I was set on studying abroad. I even considered selling my house and going.
（2）彼女かの じょがもしいやだと言いえば、引ひきずってでも病院びょう いんへ連つれて行いくつもりだ。／If she were to refuse, I was ready to drag her to the hospital with me.
（3）いざとなれば、会社かい しゃをやめてでも、裁判さい ばんで争あらそうつもりだ。／Were the day to come, even if I quit my company, I still plan to fight it out in court.
（4）由起子ゆ き こはまだ熱ねつが下さがらないが、この試合し あいだけは、這はってでも出でたいと言いっている。／Though Yukiko still had a temperature, she insisted that this was the one match she would play even if she had to crawl to it.
Used to indicate tough measures for dealing with a situation. Accompanied by expressions of strong intent or desire, it conveys a determination to accomplish something even by employing extreme measures.
