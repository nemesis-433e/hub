【おもう】

1. ...とおもう

a. ...とおもう

（1）今日きょうは雨あめが降ふると思おもいます。／I think it’ll rain today.
（2）山田やま だ先生せん せいは来こないと思おもう。／I think Yamada-sensei won’t come.
（3）あの人ひとのやり方かたはひどいと思おもいます。／I think the way he does things is awful.
（4）彼かれの言いったことはうそだと思おもう。／I feel what he said was a lie.
（5）確たしか、机つくえの上うえに置おいたと思おもう。／I’m sure I put it on the desk.
（6）あなたには幸しあわせになってほしいと思おもうから、あえてこういうきつい忠告ちゅう こくをするのです。／I want you to be happy, so that is why I am giving you such stern advice.
（7）こんな忙いそがしい会社かい しゃにいつまでもいては過労死か ろう ししかねないと思おもって、思おもいきって転職てん しょくすることにした。／I thought there was a real chance of dying from overwork if I stayed at such a busy company, so I took the plunge and changed jobs.
Follows a clause and indicates that the content of the clause is the speaker’s subjective judgment or personal opinion. When used in an interrogative sentence, its function is to ask for the listener’s personal judgment or opinion . When used in the dictionary form (と思う) or masu-form (と思います) as a declarative statement, the subject is always the speaker and never another person. For example, in (2) the person doing the thinking is 私 (I), not 山田先生 (Yamada-sensei). If the speaker wishes to say that the person doing the thinking is 山田さん, then use of the form 思っている is required, as in 山田さんは（田中さんが来ない）と思っている (Yamada-san thinks that (Tanaka-san will not come)). However, 思う can refer to a third person’s judgment when it is used in ta-form, as in the example below.
（例）山田さんは来ないと思った。／I thought Yamada-san wouldn’t come./Yamada-san thought (someone else) wouldn’t come.
In this case, the original sentence can be interpreted as either “I thought Yamada-san wouldn’t come” or “Yamada-san thought (someone else) wouldn’t come.”

b. ...とおもっている

（1）私わたしは自分じ ぶんのしたことが正ただしいと思おもっている。／I think what I did was right.
（2）イギリスに留学りゅう がくしてよかったと思おもっている。／I feel that studying in the U.K. was very good for me.
（3）警察けい さつはあの男おとこが犯人はん にんだと思おもっている。／The police think that man is the culprit.
（4）その実力じつ りょくで合格ごう かくできると思おもっているの。／Do you really think your ability can get you a passing grade?
Follows a clause and expresses an opinion, judgment, or belief on the part of the speaker or another person. The difference between 思っている and 思う (see Usage 1a above) is that 思う is interpreted to mean that the speaker’s judgment has been made on the spot, while 思っている implies that someone has held a certain opinion or belief from some point in the past until the present. Also, 思っている can express the opinion or judgment of another person, as in (3) and (4), while 思うcannot.

c. ...とおもわれる

（1）このままの状態じょう たいでは環境かん きょう汚染お せんは進すすむ一方いっ ぽうだと思おもわれる。／There is reason to believe that if things are left as they are, environmental pollution will only get worse.
（2）私わたしにはこのことが正ただしいとは思おもわれません。／This doesn’t seem right to me.
Means “a certain judgment is formed spontaneously.” Used when stating an opinion objectively rather than arbitrarily, or for softening the impact of an assertion. Often used in academic papers, lectures, and other kinds of written language. と can be replaced by ように to form the expression ように思われる.

2. ...とはおもわなかった

（1）まさか今日きょうあの人ひとが来くるとは思おもわなかった。／I never thought that person would come today!
（2）こんな街中まち なかにこんな静しずかな公園こう えんがあるとは思おもわなかった。／I didn’t expect there to be such a quiet park like this right in the middle of downtown.
（3）独身寮どく しん りょうの部屋へ やは狭せまいとは聞きいていたが、こんなに狭せまいとは思おもわなかった。／I’d heard that the rooms in the singles dormitory were small, but I never imagined they would be this small.
（4）いつも反抗的はん こう てきなお前まえがそんなに素直す なおに謝あやまるとは思おもわなかったな。／I never thought that someone as contrary as you would apologize so sincerely.
（5）A:引ひっ越こし先さきのおとなりが田中た なかさんだなんて思おもってもみませんでしたよ。奇遇き ぐうですね。／I never imagined that I was moving in next to you, Tanaka-san. What a coincidence!
B:いや、ぼくも隣となりに越こしてくるのが君きみだとは思おもわなかったよ。／Well, I never thought you were the person moving in next door either!
Means that the speaker views a situation or event as completely unexpected. Usually implies a feeling of surprise.

3. R-たいとおもう

（1）アメリカに留学りゅう がくしたいと思おもいます。／I want to go to the U.S. to study.
（2）結婚式けっ こん しきには是非ぜ ひ参加さん かしたいと思おもっております。／I’d very much like to attend your wedding ceremony.
（3）一流いち りゅう会社がい しゃに就職しゅう しょくしたいと思おもっている。／I’d like to work for a top-ranked company.
（4）では、ご一緒いっ しょに乾杯かん ぱいをしたいと存ぞんじます。／I would now like to ask you all to join me in a toast.
思う is combined with ...たい, which expresses a wish or desire on the part of the speaker, to soften the directness of the ...たい expression and make the statement more polite. 存じます can be used instead of 思う／思います, as in (4), to create an even politer expression. Sentence-final use of ...たい（です） gives an impression of childishness and is not appropriate in formal conversation among adults, in which case たい is usually followed by 思う or のだ.

4. ...おもう

［Na におもう］

［A-くおもう］

（1）先生せん せいに指導し どうしていただけることになって、本当ほん とうに幸しあわせに思おもいます。／I am very happy to have the opportunity to be instructed by you.
（2）バスが全然ぜん ぜん来こないので、不思議ふ し ぎに思おもって聞きいてみたら、昨日きのうからダイヤが変かわったとのことだった。／I wondered why there were no buses coming, so I asked, and they said that the bus schedule had changed starting yesterday.
（3）この度たびの突然とつ ぜんのご逝去せい きょをまことに辛つらく悲かなしく思おもいます。／I am deeply grieved to hear of his sudden death.
（4）お会あいできてうれしく思おもいます。／It was very nice to have the chance to see you.
（5）このような賞しょうをいただくことができ、まことに光栄こう えいに存ぞんじます。／It is truly an honor to receive a prize such as this.
When 思うfollows the conjunctive form of i-adjectives and na-adjectives that express feelings or emotions, it expresses how the speaker feels. The pattern XをYに（Yく）おもう is also used, as in (2) and (3). To ask about the listener’s impression, どう思う／思いますか are used, as in the example below.
（例）あの人についてどう思いますか。／What do you think of that person?

5. V-ようとおもう

（1）今日きょうはゆっくり休やすもうと思おもう。／I’m going to take it easy and rest today.
（2）この仕事し ごとをやめようと思おもっている。／I’m thinking of quitting this job.
（3）A:夏休なつ やすみはどうするつもりですか。／What are you planning to do this summer vacation?
B:ヨーロッパを旅行りょ こうしようと思おもっています。／I’m going to tour Europe.
（4）将来しょう らい、どんな仕事し ごとをしようと思おもっているんですか。／So what sort of work are you going to do in the future?
When combined with the intentional form of a verb, と思う expresses the speaker’s plans or intentions. In interrogative sentences, the function of this combination is to ask about the listener’s intentions. When と思う is used with the dictionary form, as in the following example, it indicates that the speaker’s plans are uncertain; this form cannot be used to express intention.
（例）私は来年アメリカに行くと思う。／I think I’ll go to the U.S. next year.

6. ...ようにおもう

［N／Na　であるようにおもう］

［A／V　ようにおもう］

（1）太田おお たくんは内気うち きなので、ウェイターの仕事し ごとは向むいていないように思おもう。／Because Ota-kun is shy, I don’t think he is suited to be a waiter.
（2）住民じゅう みんの多おおくが反対はん たいしていることを考かんがえると、マンションの建築けん ちくは見合み あわせた方ほうがいいように思おもう。／Considering the fact that many local residents are opposed to it, I think it would be better to postpone construction of the condominiums.
（3）この社員しゃ いん旅行りょ こうのプランはちょっとゆとりがなさすぎるように思おもうのですが。こんなに短期間たん き かんであちこち動うごき回まわっても疲つかれるだけではないでしょうか。／I think the plan for this company trip doesn’t include enough free time. Moving from one place to another in such a short time will only tire everyone out.
（4）《上司じょう しに》パソコンは一人ひとりに一台いち だいあった方ほうが仕事し ごとの能率のう りつも上あがるように思おもうのですが、購入こう にゅうするわけにはいきませんか。／《to one’s boss》I think we would be able to work more efficiently if each person had his/her own computer. Couldn’t we buy more computers?
（5）国民こく みん一人一人ひとりひとりの幸しあわせを考かんがえることは首相しゅ しょうとしての当然とう ぜんの義務ぎ むであるように思おもわれますが、首相しゅ しょうはいかがお考かんがえでしょうか。／It seems to me that considering the happiness of each and every citizen is a given duty of the prime minister. What do you, as prime minister, think about this?
Expresses a mild assertion of opinion. Often used when the speaker thinks there is a possibility that the listener has a different opinion, or when the speaker offers an opinion with which it is difficult for the listener to agree. When an even more euphemistic expression is desired, ように思われる is used.

7. N（のこと）をおもう

（1）親おやが子供こ どもを思おもう気持き もちは何なににも変かえられない。／Nothing can replace the love parents have for their children.
（2）いつもあなたのことを思おもっている。／I’m always thinking about you.
（3）試験し けんのことを思おもうと心配しん ぱいで眠ねむれない。／When I think about the exam, I get so worried that I can’t sleep.
（4）母ははの優やさしさを思おもうと気持き もちが安やすらぐ。／My mind is at peace when I remember my mother’s gentleness.
When combined with a noun or a noun followed by のこと, 思う means that the speaker thinks or feels strongly about something. Depending on the meaning of the preceding noun, it can express a variety of cognitive processes, including imagination, retrospection, anxiety, reserve, and love.

8. Nを...とおもう

［Nを　N／Na　だとおもう］

［Nを　A／V　とおもう］

（1）最初さい しょは保子やす こさんを男おとこの子こだと思おもった。／At first, I thought Yasuko-san was a boy.
（2）人々ひと びとは私わたしの考かんがえを奇想天外き そう てん がいだと思おもったようだ。／People seemed to consider my idea to be bizarre.
（3）みんな、彼かれの提案てい あんを実現じつ げん不可能ふ か のうだと思おもって相手あい てにしなかった。／Everyone thought his suggestion was unfeasible and wouldn’t go along with him.
（4）彼女かの じょの横顔よこ がおを美うつくしいと思おもった。／I thought her profile was beautiful.
（5）みんなが彼かれのことを死しんだと思おもっていた。／Everyone thought he had died.
（6）彼かれは自分じ ぶんのことを天才てん さいだと思おもっている。／He considers himself a genius.
Used to express one’s feeling, impression, or judgment concerning something. Sometimes Nが is used instead of Nを, as below:
（例）人々は私の考えが奇想天外だと思ったようだ。／People seemed to think my idea was bizarre.
Can also be used to indicate that someone has mistaken something or someone for something or someone else, as in (1).
