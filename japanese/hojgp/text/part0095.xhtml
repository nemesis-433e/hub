【おきに】

［数量詞+おきに］

（1）大学だい がく行いきのバスは 10 分ぷんおきに出でている。／The buses to the university run every ten minutes.
（2）この薬くすりは2時間じ かんおきに飲のんでください。／Please take this medicine every two hours.
（3）この道路どう ろには 10 mおきにポプラが植うえられている。／On this street, poplars have been planted at intervals of ten meters.
（4）このあたりは高級こう きゅう住宅じゅう たく街がいで、2軒けんおきぐらいに外車がい しゃを持もっている家いえがある。／This area is an upscale residential district, and every third house or so has an imported car.
（5）映画館えい が かんに入はいると、座席ざ せきは一ひとつおきにしかあいていなかったので、友達とも だちとは離はなれて座すわることになった。／When we got into the theater, every other seat was taken, so my friend and I ended up sitting separately.
Usually follows a word expressing time or distance and means “with that amount of time or space in between.” The words used with おき in (4) and (5) don’t express distance, but they do refer to things arranged in a line (houses (4), seats (5)) and are interpreted to express distance. When おきに refers to a point in time or space, as in (1)-(3), it can be replaced with ごとに. However, when おきに and ごとに are used to refer to the number one, they differ in meaning, as shown in the examples below.
（例）1年おきに大会が開かれる。（2年に1回）／The meeting is held every other year. (= once every two years)
（例）1年ごとに大会が開かれる。（1年に1回）／The meeting is held every year. (= once a year)
