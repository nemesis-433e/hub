【おもったら】

［N／Na　だとおもったら］

［A／V　とおもったら］

（1）息子むすこの姿すがたが見みえないと思おもったら、押おし入いれの中なかで寝ねていた。／I was wondering where my son could be, and there he was sleeping in the closet.
（2）なんだか寒さむいと思おもったら、窓まどが開あいていたのか。／I was wondering why it was so chilly, but now I can see it’s because someone left the window open.
（3）めがねがないないと思おもったら、こんなところに置おき忘わすれていたよ。／I was wondering why I couldn’t find my glasses, but now I see that I left them here, of all places!
（4）冷蔵庫れい ぞう こにおいしそうなケーキがあると思おもったら、お客きゃくさん用ようだった。／I was thinking that the cake in the refrigerator looked delicious, but it was for our guests.
（5）最近さい きん上田うえ ださんが学校がっ こうに来こないと思おもっていたら、交通こう つう事故じ こで入院にゅう いんしているらしい。／I was wondering why Ueda-san isn’t coming to school lately, and then I heard that she’s in the hospital because of a traffic accident.
（6）誰だれもいないのにうちに電気でん きがついていると思おもったら、弟おとうとが遊あそびに来きて勝手かっ てに上あがり込こんでいたのだった。／I was wondering why the lights were on even though no one was home, but it turned out that my younger brother had dropped by for a visit and let himself in.
Placed after a clause and expresses an unsettled feeling about something for which the cause or reason is unknown. Followed by a statement of the cause, reason, or explanation, which also indicates that the matter has been satisfactorily resolved for the speaker. Example (1) means “I thought it was strange that my son wasn’t here, but I found him sleeping in the closet, so then I understood,” and (2) means “I was wondering why it was chilly, but then I noticed that the window had been left open, so I could understand the reason.” When an inexplicable situation continues over a period of time, と思っていたら can be used, as in (5).
