【とにかく】

Used to put aside discussion of a certain matter or action for the time being in order to focus on another matter or action. The form ともかく is also used.

1. とにかく

（1）あの人ひとはとにかく大変たい へんな秀才しゅう さいです。／At any rate, she’s exceptionally bright.
（2）田中た なかさんの新あたらしい家いえ、とにかくすごく大おおきい家いえなんですよ。／You know Tanaka-san’s new house—whatever else you might say about it, it's incredibly huge!
（3）戦闘せん とうの後あとの町まちは、とにかくひどい状況じょう きょうです。／Now that the battle is over, the town is all in all in a terrible state.
Means “although many things could probably be said, this one comes first.” Followed by an expression that indicates an extraordinary degree of something, such as 非常に／大変／すごく...だ (it is extremely/very/incredibly...). Used to emphasize this meaning. Tends to be used in spoken language.

2. とにかくV

（1）うまくいくか分わかりませんが、とにかくやってみます。／I don’t know whether or not I'll succeed, but at any rate, I’ll try.
（2）とにかく言いわれたことだけはやっておきました。／Anyway, I’ve done at least what I was told to do.
（3）お忙いそがしいとは存ぞんじますがとにかくおいでくださいますようお願ねがいいたします。／We know how very busy you are, but we would be grateful if you would please just honor us with your presence.
（4）まだ全員ぜん いんそろっていませんが、時間じ かんですのでとにかく始はじめることにしましょう。／Not everyone is here yet, but since it’s time, let’s just get started.
Followed by a verb expressing an intentional action and means “giving priority to this action for now, regardless of other considerations.” Used by the speaker to assert his/her own intention, a fact, etc. or to urge the addressee to implement an action.

3. Nはとにかく（として）

（1）見みかけはとにかく味あじはよい。／Never mind what it looks like, it tastes good.
（2）成績せい せきはとにかくとして、明あかるくて思おもいやりのあるいい子供こ どもです。／Putting aside the matter of his grades, he is a cheerful and considerate child.
（3）私わたしはとにかく、あなたはこの仕事し ごとに満足まん ぞくしているんですか。／Never mind me—are you satisfied with this job?
（4）あいさつはとにかくまずは中なかにお入はいりください。／The greetings can wait until later—please come inside.
（5）A:先日せん じつはお世話せ わ様さまでした。／Thank you so much for the other day.
B:いいえ。それはとにかく、お願ねがいした仕事し ごとの方ほうは引ひき受うけてくださいますか。／Not at all. Be that as it may, are you willing to take on the work I asked you about?
Placed after a noun and used to contrast that thing with something else that is more important or should be done first. In conversation, as in (5), it can also be used at the beginning of a sentence in forms like それはとにかく（として） to refer to something the other person has said and introduce a different topic.
