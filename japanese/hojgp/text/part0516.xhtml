【とあって】

1. ...とあって

［Nとあって］

［Vとあって］

（1）今日きょうは三さん連休れん きゅうとあって、全国ぜん こくの行楽地こう らく ちは家族か ぞく連づれの観光客かん こう きゃくで賑にぎわいました。／Since today is part of a three-day holiday, the nation's leisure spots are bustling with family vacationers.
（2）一年いち ねんに一回いっ かいのお祭まつりとあって、村むらの人ひとはみんな神社じん じゃへ集あつまっていた。／This being the only festival of the year, the villagers all gathered at the shrine.
（3）めったに聞きけない彼かれの生演奏なま えん そうとあって、狭せまいクラブは満員まん いんになった。／As he rarely performed live, the tiny club was filled to capacity.
（4）大型おお がたの台風たい ふうが接近せっ きんしているとあって、どの家いえも対策たい さくにおおわらわだ。／With the huge typhoon drawing near, every household was frantic with preparations.
（5）名画めい がが無料む りょうで見みられるとあって、席せきははやばやと埋うまってしまった。／Since the famous film could be seen for free, every seat was soon filled.
Means “due to such-and-such a situation.” Used to indicate special circumstances and implies that a statement of something that will naturally occur under those circumstances, or a statement of an action that should be taken, will follow. A written language expression used in news broadcasts and so on.

2. ...とあっては

（1）伊藤い とうさんの頼たのみとあっては、断ことわれない。／Since it's a request from Ito-san, I cannot refuse.
（2）彼かれが講演こう えんするとあっては、何なんとかして聞ききに行いかねばならない。／He’s the one giving the lecture, so I have to find some way to go hear it.
（3）高価こう かなじゅうたんが定価てい かの一割いち わりで買かえるとあっては、店みせが混雑こん ざつしないはずがありません。／With such an expensive carpet being sold for a tenth of its list price, I wouldn't expect the store not to be packed with people.
（4）最新さい しんのコンピューター機器き きがすべて展示てん じされるとあっては、コンピューターマニアの彼かれが行いかないわけがない。／With all the latest computer equipment on display, there's no way a computer geek like him wouldn't go.
Means “because this is the situation.” Used for special circumstances and is followed by a statement of something that will naturally occur under those circumstances, or a statement of an action that should be taken. For example, (1) would be used in a situation where a refusal is unthinkable because Ito-san is a very important person to the speaker. Although this is a somewhat formal expression, it can be used in spoken language.
