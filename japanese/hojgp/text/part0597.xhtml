【とちゅう】

1. とちゅうで

（1）いつもの時間じ かんに家いえを出でたが、途中と ちゅうで忘わすれ物ものに気きづいて引ひき返かえした。／I left home at the usual time, but along the way I realized I had forgotten something and came back.
（2）やりかけた仕事し ごとは途中と ちゅうで投なげ出だしてはいけないよ。／You shouldn’t give up on a job partway through once you've started it.
（3）泥棒どろ ぼうの足跡あし あとは途中と ちゅうで途切と ぎれている。／The robber’s footprints disappeared along the way.
（4）この道みちは途中と ちゅうで行いき止どまりになっている。／This road eventually turns into a dead end.
Means “in the middle of” a place or time. Used to express that before something is finished, it is interrupted or something else occurs. With this usage, the particle で cannot be dropped.

2. ...とちゅう（で／に）

［Nのとちゅう（で／に）］

［V-るとちゅう（で／に）］

（1）通勤つう きんの途中と ちゅう、突然とつ ぜん雨あめが降ふりだした。／On my way to work, it suddenly started to rain.
（2）買かい物ものの途中と ちゅうで、急きゅうに気き分ぶんが悪わるくなって倒たおれてしまった。／In the middle of shopping, I suddenly felt sick and collapsed.
（3）買かい物ものに行いく途中と ちゅうで、ばったり昔むかしの友人ゆう じんに会あった。／On my way to the store, I happened to run into an old friend.
（4）家いえに帰かえる途中と ちゅう、居酒屋い ざか やに立たち寄よった。／I stopped at a bar on my way home.
（5）駅えきに行いく途中と ちゅうに郵便局ゆう びん きょくがあるので、そこでこの手紙て がみを出だしてくれませんか。／There’s a post office on the way to the station, so could you take this letter there and mail it for me?
Follows action nouns or verbs. Indicates that another event occurs at some point before the action in question is completed, or that something exists at a place along the path one is taking. Generally, the particle で is used to indicate the point at which an event occurs, and the particle に is used to indicate a location, but these are sometimes dropped, as shown in (1) and (4).

3. ...とちゅう（は）

（1）会社かい しゃに来くる途中と ちゅう、ずっとこの小説しょう せつを読よんでいた。／On my way to the office, I was reading this novel the whole time.
（2）歩あるいている途中と ちゅう、彼かれに言いわれたことばが頭あたまを離はなれなかった。／While I was walking, I couldn’t get his words to me out of my head.
（3）旅たびの途中と ちゅうは眠ねむってばかりいた。／All she did was sleep through the entire trip.
（4）通勤つう きんの途中と ちゅうは語学ご がくの勉強べん きょうをすることにしている。／I make it a rule to do language study during my commute.
...とちゅう（は）is placed after a noun or verb that expresses movement from one location to another. Used to show that the second action or state continues throughout the period of motion.
