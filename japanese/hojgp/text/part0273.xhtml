【しまつだ】

［V-るしまつだ］

（1）彼女かの じょは夫おっとの欠点けっ てんを延々えん えんと並ならべ上あげ、あげくの果はてには離婚り こんすると言いって泣なき出だす始末し まつだった。／She went on and on listing her husband’s shortcomings, and in the end she finally said she was going to divorce him and burst into tears.
（2）息子むすこは大学だい がくの勉強べん きょうは何なんの役やくにも立たたないと言いってアルバイトに精せいを出だし、この頃ごろは中退ちゅう たいして働はたらきたいなどと言いい出だす始末し まつだ。／Saying that college studies serve no purpose whatsoever, my son has put all his energy into his part-time job, and now he says he wants to drop out and get a job.
（3）一度いち ど相談そう だんにのってあげただけなのに、彼かれはあなただけが頼たよりだと言いって、真夜中ま よ なかにでも電話でん わをかけてくる始末し まつだった。／I only listened to his troubles once, but now he ends up calling me in the middle of the night and telling me I’m the only one he can depend on.
Attached to the dictionary form of a verb, しまつだ expresses that a troublesome situation or annoyance has arisen due to another person’s actions. The first half of the sentence describes all the things that have led up to the situation, and the second half states the situation that has arisen as a result.
The expression この始末だ in the example below is idiomatic and implies criticism when indicating something that has occurred.
（例）山田はどうもこの頃学校に来ないと思ったらこの始末だ。バイクで人身事故を起こすような学生には、もう退学してもらうしかない。／I’ve been wondering why Yamada hasn’t come to school lately, and now this! A student like that who causes a fatal accident with his motorcycle—the only thing we can do is have him quit school.
