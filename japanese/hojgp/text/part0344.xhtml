【それでこそ】

（1）彼かれは部下ぶ かの失敗しっ ぱいの責任せき にんをとって、社長しゃ ちょうの座ざを降おりた。それでこそ真しんのリーダーと言いえる。／He took responsibility for his subordinate’s mistake and stepped down as company president. That’s what we can call a true leader.
（2）A:あの大学だい がく、卒業そつ ぎょうするのがむずかしいそうだよ。／I’ve heard that university is hard to graduate from.
B:それでこそ本当ほん とうの大学だい がくだね。／That’s how a real university should be.
（3）A:今度こん どのコピー機きは、まったく人手ひと でがいらないそうだ。／I hear the new photocopier doesn’t need anyone to operate it at all.
B:それでこそオフィス革命かく めいと言いえるね。今いままでのは人ひとを忙いそがしくさせるだけだったから。／You can call that a real office revolution, can’t you. Up till now it had just made people even busier.
Used at the beginning of a sentence, this means “because of, for that reason.” Used to highlight a certain quality of a person or thing and express a high evaluation of that person/thing, based on the previously-stated reason. Used only for positive evaluation. An old-fashioned expression. In conversation, it is often used in semi-formulaic expressions such as それでこそ本当のNだ (that’s a real/true N.)
