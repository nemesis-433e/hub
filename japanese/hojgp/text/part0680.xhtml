【なくもない】

Attaches to a verb or an adjective and means that a certain thing is not entirely unfeasible, but sometimes exists or arises. It is also possible to say ないこともない and ないでもない.

1. V-なくもない

（1）A:お酒さけは召めし上あがらないんですか。／Won’t you have a drink?
B:飲のまなくもないんですが、あまり強つよくはありません。／It’s not that I never have any alcohol, but I’m not really that much of a drinker.
（2）時ときには転職てん しょくすることを考かんがえなくもない。／I won’t deny that I sometimes think about changing jobs.
（3）日本語に ほん ごの会話かい わは、日本に ほんに来きてから少すこし上達じょう たつしたと言いえなくもない。／I can say that my spoken Japanese has improved somewhat since I came to Japan.
（4）最近さい きん彼女かの じょは少すこし元気げん きがないような気きがしなくもない。／I have to admit that recently she seems to have less energy.
Means that a certain action or awareness may sometimes be realized. When used with verbs of thinking or perception, such as 言う (say), 考える (think), 思う (think/believe), 認める (acknowledge), 感じる (feel), or 気がする (have the sense that), it means that for some reason the speaker has a certain kind of feeling.

2. Nがなくもない

（1）再婚さい こんするつもりがなくもない。／I’m not saying that I won’t get married again.
（2）あの人ひとを恨うらむ気持き もちがなくもない。／I can’t say that I hold no grudge against her.
（3）A:あの人ひとまだ独身どく しんですが、結婚けっ こんするつもりがないのでしょうか。／He’s still single, but does that mean he has no intention of getting married?
B:その気きもなくはないようですが、今いまのところは特とくにそんな様子よう すはありませんね。／I wouldn’t say that he’s not interested at all, but at the moment there aren’t any indications that anything’s happening.
Used mainly with nouns expressing some kind of intention or feeling. Means it is not the case that the person in subject position has no such intention or feeling at all. When なくもない is preceded by N +も, the form Nもなくはない may be used instead, as in (3).
