【のだったら】

［N／Na　なのだったら］

［A／V　のだったら］

（1）風邪か ぜなんだったら、そんな薄着うす ぎはだめだよ。／If you have a cold, you shouldn’t wear such light clothing.
（2）そんなに嫌きらいなんだったら、むりに食たべなくてもいいよ。／If you dislike it that much, you don’t have to eat it.
（3）こんなに寒さむいんだったら、もう1枚まい着きて来くるんだった。／If I’d known it was going to be this cold, I would have come wearing another layer.
（4）A:そのパーティ、私わたしも行いきたいな。／I want to go to that party, too.
B:あなたが行いくんだったら私わたしも行いこうかな。／If you’re going, then maybe I’ll go, too.
Refers to something the speaker has just heard, a current state of affairs, etc. Means “if so” or “under those circumstances.” In spoken Japanese, the form んだったら is often used.
