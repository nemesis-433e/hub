【むやみに】

（1）人ひとの物ものにむやみにさわらないほうがいい。／You shouldn’t handle other people’s things so freely.
（2）山やまで道みちに迷まよったときはむやみに歩あるき回まわらないほうがいい。／When you lose the path in the mountains, it is better not to wander around aimlessly.
（3）たとえ小ちいさな虫むしでも、むやみに殺ころしてはいけない。／Even if it’s a small insect, it is wrong to kill it recklessly.
（4）最近さい きん、父ちちは年としのせいか、むやみに怒おこる。／Recently, probably because of his age, my father gets angry for no reason.
Shows the condition of doing something without thinking of the consequences or results. Prohibitive phrases such as するな, してはいけない and しない方がよい or するのはよくない are often used after this. Also, (4) demonstrates the passing of time. むやみやたらに is a more emphatic way of expressing this. The form むやみと can also be used.
