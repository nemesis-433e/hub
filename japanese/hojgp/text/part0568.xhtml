【ときたひには】

This is a spoken language expression that is rather outdated. Usually ときたら is used instead.

1. Nときたひには

（1）うちの女房にょう ぼうときたひには、暇ひまさえあれば居眠い ねむりしている。／Take my wife—whenever she has a little free time, she dozes off!
（2）うちの親父おやじときたひには、天気てん きさえよければ釣つりに行いっている。／That father of mine—if the weather is clear, he's off fishing!
Used to introduce, as the topic of conversation, a person with extreme actions or characteristics. Expresses the feeling that the speaker is utterly astonished or dumbfounded.

2. ...ときたひには

（1）毎日まい にち残業ざん ぎょうで、しかも休日きゅう じつなしときたひには、病気びょう きになるのも無理む りはない。／What with overtime every day and, on top of that, no days off, it’s no wonder she got sick.
（2）授業じゅ ぎょうには毎回まいかい遅刻ち こくで、試験し けんも零点れい てんときたひには、落第らく だいするのも当然とう ぜんだ。／He came late every time and got zero on his tests—of course he failed that class!
（3）毎日まい にちうだるような暑あつさが続つづいて、しかも水不足みず ぶ そくときたひには愚痴ぐ ちもいいたくなる。／With this sweltering heat day after day, and a lack of water to boot, anyone would want to complain!
Placed after clauses or nouns that express a situation that is extreme or has an unusual degree of something. Indicates that, given such a situation, the resulting condition is to be expected. Usually conveys a negative evaluation.
